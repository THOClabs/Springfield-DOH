## The 8 Key Learnings (From Field Research)

### Learning 1: Characters Are Access Points
Users self-select the perspective they need. Position as "find the perspective you're missing."

### Learning 2: The Permission Effect Is Real
Characters provide psychological safety. Users acknowledge hard truths because a cartoon "said it."

### Learning 3: Individual Use Precedes Team Use
Design journey as: Individual exploration → Personal value → Selective sharing → Team adoption

### Learning 4: Non-Technical Market Is Larger Than Expected
Anyone who needs to think through problems can benefit. "Developer tool" may be limiting.

### Learning 5: Skeptics Are Best Converts
Don't avoid skeptics. Design for them. Let the product prove itself.

### Learning 6: Group Settings Amplify Impact
One person's "obvious question" becomes a group's "aha moment."

### Learning 7: Some Value Is Planted, Not Harvested
Not every interaction ends with conversion. Some users carry questions with them.

### Learning 8: The Core Positioning Is Validated
Homer's insight + Priya's validation = Our thesis is correct.
