## The Messaging Principles

### Principle 1: Lead with Utility

**WRONG:** "It's a funny Simpsons tool!"
**RIGHT:** "It's a CLI tool that helps you think better. Also, it's Simpsons-themed."

The entertainment is real, but it's the MEANS, not the END.

### Principle 2: Anticipate Skepticism

People will think:
- "This is a joke tool"
- "Characters are just gimmicks"
- "Real developers don't need entertainment"

Always have counter-messaging ready.

### Principle 3: Show, Don't Tell

**WEAK:** "Homer helps you think differently!"
**STRONG:** "Here's a question Homer asked that found a $500 bug."

Concrete examples beat abstract claims.

### Principle 4: Match the Audience

- **Enterprise:** ROI, productivity, methodology
- **Individual:** Joy, perspective, fun
- **Technical:** Architecture, integration, capabilities
- **Social:** Personality, community, shared experience

## Message Don'ts

### Never Say:

| Don't Say | Why Not | Say Instead |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| "Just for fun" | Undermines utility | "Fun AND useful" |
| "It's a joke tool" | False | "Theme is fun, output is serious" |
| "Better than [competitor]" | Unverifiable | "Unique approach" |
| "Works with everything" | Too absolute | "Works with most workflows" |
| "Guaranteed results" | Legal risk | "Designed to help" |
| "Homer IS dumb" | Misunderstands the point | "Homer asks beginner's mind questions" |

### Avoid These Tones:

- **Apologetic:** "I know it sounds silly, but..."
- **Defensive:** "Look, it actually works..."
- **Desperate:** "Please just try it..."
- **Arrogant:** "Obviously better than..."

### Target Tone:

- **Confident:** "Here's what it does."
- **Welcoming:** "Try it yourself."
- **Honest:** "The characters are fun, the output is real."
- **Curious:** "See if Homer finds something you missed."

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Twitter/X
- Max personality, min explanation
- Use thread format for full story
- Include screenshot of character interaction
- Encourage replies with "What would Homer ask about your project?"

### LinkedIn
- Professional tone, ROI focus
- Emphasize methodology over entertainment
- Target: Engineering managers, CTOs
- Hashtags: #DevTools #DeveloperProductivity #SoftwareEngineering

### Reddit/HN
- Honest, technical, self-aware
- Acknowledge the "this sounds silly" elephant
- Invite criticism constructively
- Share the architecture and decisions

### YouTube/Demo Videos
- Show, don't tell
- Real project, real interaction
- Character voice should come through
- End with clear CTA

### Documentation
- Utility first, personality second
- Complete technical information
- Character examples as illustrations
- Welcoming to skeptics

