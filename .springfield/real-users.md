## User Feedback Synthesis

### What Worked (Most Mentioned)

| Feature | Mentions | Representative Quote |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Character differentiation | 8/8 | "Each one genuinely thinks differently" |
| "Obvious" questions | 6/8 | "Questions like my wife does" |
| Psychological safety | 5/8 | "Blame the cartoon for surfacing it" |
| Unexpected depth | 5/8 | "Not just a gimmick" |
| Accessibility | 4/8 | "Useful for more than programmers" |

### Concerns Raised

| Concern | Mentions | Representative Quote |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| Social embarrassment | 2/8 | "Too good at finding things I don't want to admit" |
| Over-reliance on Lisa | 2/8 | "Too good - like I should let her do my thinking" |
| Team dynamics | 1/8 | "Who controls which character?" |
| Entertainment vs utility balance | 1/8 | "Don't let it become a joke" |

### Unexpected Discoveries

1. **Non-technical users love it**: The retired teacher may have had the most positive reaction of anyone.

2. **Skeptics are convertible**: Both the startup founder and the journalist came in skeptical and left changed.

3. **Group settings amplify impact**: The Austin meetup showed emergent social dynamics around the tool.

4. **Some seeds take time**: The journalist didn't "convert" but was clearly affected.

## User Archetypes Identified

### The Skeptic → Convert
*Example: Jessica, Derek*
- Comes in dismissive
- One moment of genuine insight catches them
- Leaves wanting more

### The Private Reflector
*Example: Marcus*
- Values the tool
- Needs privacy to be honest with it
- Individual use precedes team use

### The Unexpected Advocate
*Example: Eleanor*
- Not the target market
- Finds profound value anyway
- Expands our understanding of use cases

### The Power User
*Example: Priya*
- Immediately grasps full potential
- Provides strategic feedback
- Becomes advisory resource

### The Seed Recipient
*Example: Derek*
- Doesn't convert immediately
- Carries a question away
- May return later, transformed

## Key Takeaways for Product

1. **Lead with utility, let entertainment emerge**
2. **Provide private modes for vulnerable reflection**
3. **Don't dismiss non-technical users**
4. **The skeptics are often the best converts**
5. **Some value is planted, not harvested immediately**
