# The Simpsons - S19E14: "Dial 'N' for Nerder"

## Episode Information

**Season:** 19

**Production Code:** KABF07
**Original Air Date:** March 9, 2008
**Written By:** Carolyn Omine and William Wright

## Plot Summary

### Main Plot (A-Story)
Bart and Lisa go bike-riding in Springfield National Park, where they encounter Martin Prince excavating for arrowheads. While Lisa joins Martin in his archaeological dig, Bart becomes frustrated and decides to play a prank on him. Using a bone stolen from Sideshow Mel's hair, Bart rigs it on a string. When he tugs the string, the bone flies up and knocks Martin in the head, causing him to stumble over the edge of a cliff. He lands on a smaller ledge, and when Lisa attempts to help him by extending a long stick, she inadvertently knocks him off the ledge entirely. Martin falls into the trees at the bottom of the cliff.

Bart and Lisa rush home, believing they have accidentally killed their classmate. They are consumed with guilt and behave suspiciously, watching the news where Kent Brockman reports that Martin has disappeared and is presumed dead. Principal Skinner holds a memorial service where he eulogizes Martin with various unflattering nicknames ("Martin Priss, Martin Princess, Fartsin Prince, Martin the Brown-Nose Reindeer").

Nelson Muntz, displaying unexpected detective skills reminiscent of Columbo, notices the Simpson children's suspicious behavior and begins investigating. He deduces that Martin had a fear of heights, making it unusual for him to be at the top of a cliff. Nelson travels to Springfield National Park and discovers Sideshow Mel's bone on a string, connecting it back to Bart and Lisa.

The tension builds until Martin suddenly reappears alive. It's revealed that he survived the fall thanks to his special anti-wedgie briefs with extra-extendable elastics, which caught on a tree branch as he fell. Nelson punches Martin upon his return but comments, "it's good to have you alive."

### Subplot (B-Story)
Homer struggles with his weight, prompting Marge to hire a nutritionist named Betsy Bidwell (a formerly obese woman who successfully lost weight). Betsy puts Homer on a strict diet, but Homer sneaks food behind Marge's back, eating at the "Taj Mah-All-You-Can-Eat" restaurant and other locations. When Marge notices Homer isn't losing weight despite supposedly following the diet, she becomes suspicious.

Marge contacts the producers of "Sneakers," a reality TV show parody of "Cheaters" that specializes in catching people in acts of infidelity. However, in this case, they help Marge catch Homer cheating on his diet rather than with another person. The show's host (resembling Joey Greco from "Cheaters") confronts Homer with video evidence of his secret eating, leading to Homer's embarrassment and confession.

## Main Characters

**Bart Simpson** - Central protagonist who orchestrates the prank on Martin and subsequently deals with overwhelming guilt over potentially causing Martin's death.

**Lisa Simpson** - Bart's accomplice who accidentally knocks Martin off the cliff ledge while trying to help him, sharing equal guilt and paranoia with Bart.

**Martin Prince** - The nerdy victim of Bart's prank who falls off a cliff but survives due to his wedgie-proof underwear catching on a tree branch.

**Nelson Muntz** - Takes on an unexpected role as an amateur detective, investigating Martin's disappearance using methods similar to the TV detective Columbo.

**Homer Simpson** - Struggles with dieting in the B-plot, repeatedly cheating on his diet by sneaking food.

**Marge Simpson** - Hires a nutritionist for Homer and later enlists a reality TV show to catch him cheating on his diet.

**Betsy Bidwell** - A nutritionist and former obese person who puts Homer on a strict diet.

**Principal Skinner** - Holds a memorial service for Martin at Springfield Elementary.

**Kent Brockman** - News anchor who reports Martin's disappearance.

**Sideshow Mel** - Unwittingly provides the bone from his hair that Bart uses in the prank.

## Cultural References

**Title Reference**
The episode title "Dial 'N' for Nerder" is a pun on Alfred Hitchcock's 1954 thriller film "Dial M for Murder."

**Columbo Homage**
Nelson's investigation methods closely mirror those of Lieutenant Columbo from the classic detective series "Columbo" (1971-2003). The episode's closing sequence directly recreates the opening sequence of the "NBC Mystery Movie," featuring:
- Nelson Muntz as Columbo
- Dr. Hibbert as Quincy (from "Quincy, M.E.")
- Rich Texan as McCloud (from "McCloud")
- Mr. Burns and Smithers as McMillan & Wife (from "McMillan & Wife")

**Cheaters Parody**
"Sneakers," the reality TV show Marge hires to catch Homer, is a parody of the reality television series "Cheaters." The host of Sneakers resembles Joey Greco, the long-time host of "Cheaters."

**Taj Mahal**
The "Taj Mah-All-You-Can-Eat" restaurant where Homer secretly binges is a play on the Taj Mahal, the famous monument in India.

**Rocky**
The first song played during Homer's diet montage is "Gonna Fly Now," the iconic theme from the 1976 film "Rocky."

**Sarah McLachlan**
The second song played during a slideshow sequence is "I Will Remember You" by Sarah McLachlan, known for its use in emotional contexts and ASPCA commercials.

**Hamlet**
Bart paraphrases the famous "The play's the thing" line from Shakespeare's "Hamlet" when planning his prank: "The bone's the very thing, methinks / to prank my sister and the dink."

## Production Notes

**Writers:** The episode was penned by Carolyn Omine and William Wright. This marked another collaboration between the two writers on a suspense-themed episode for Season 19.

**Director:** Bob Anderson directed this episode. Anderson is a veteran Simpsons director who has helmed numerous episodes throughout the series' run.

**Music:** The background score was composed by longtime Simpsons composer Alf Clausen, who created film noir-style suspense music with jazzy, tension-building cues during the children's amateur investigation sequences.

**Production Code:** KABF07

**Animation:** The episode features detailed animation of Springfield National Park and the cliff sequence, requiring careful storyboarding to convey the tension of Martin's fall and the subsequent guilt experienced by Bart and Lisa.

**Home Video Release:** The episode was released as part of "The Simpsons: The Complete Nineteenth Season" DVD set on December 3, 2019, by 20th Century Fox Home Entertainment in Region 1. The four-disc collection includes all 20 episodes from the season, with special features including audio commentaries for every episode. The commentary for "Dial 'N' for Nerder" was recorded by producers including showrunner Al Jean and writer Carolyn Omine, along with other crew members, discussing aspects like the episode's mystery plot and the humorous diet subplot.

## Reception and Reviews

**Viewership**
During its first broadcast on March 9, 2008, "Dial 'N' for Nerder" attracted an estimated 7.3 million viewers and received a 10 percent audience share, solid numbers for Season 19.

**Critical Reception**

**IGN - 8.5/10**
Robert Canning of IGN awarded the episode an 8.5 out of 10, his second-highest rating for Season 19. He declared it "quite simply the best of the season," stating "There wasn't one moment in the episode that didn't work." Canning praised the tight writing and variety of humor that ranged from parody to silliness. He particularly highlighted Nelson's prominent role as a standout element, noting that "while the diet cheating storyline was great silly fun, it was the turmoil that Bart and Lisa went through that really made this an outstanding episode." He commended both storylines as well-constructed with strong character moments, especially appreciating the development of Nelson beyond his typical bully persona.

**The A.V. Club - B-**
The A.V. Club gave "Dial 'N' for Nerder" a grade of B-. The reviewer found the Bart and Lisa story "quite good" and mentioned missing Lisa-centric plots throughout the season. However, they criticized the Homer and Marge subplot, stating that "if Homer and Marge were actual living actors, they would have zero onscreen chemistry left." The reviewer also took issue with the ending, calling the NBC Mystery Movie recreation "lazy" and describing it as "a straight-up recreation."

**TV Squad**
Richard Keller of TV Squad described the episode as "pretty good," praising its balance of dual plots and the bold decision to include Nelson as an investigator, which gave the character unexpected depth.

**IMDB - 7.4/10**
The episode holds a rating of 7.4/10 on IMDB based on user reviews. Many users consider it "an amazing episode" and "one of the best episodes of Season 19, if not one of the best middle age (S11-S20) episodes." Several reviewers appreciated the Columbo references and the episode's suspenseful atmosphere.

**No Homers Club**
User reviews on the fan site No Homers Club averaged around 3.8 out of 5, with praise for the mystery elements and tension building, though some criticized the subplot's predictability.

**Overall Assessment**
The episode is generally regarded as one of the stronger entries in Season 19, with particular praise for its suspenseful main plot, Nelson's character development, and the successful blending of mystery parody with traditional Simpsons humor.

## Trivia

- This episode features a rare reversal of the typical Bart and Lisa dynamic, with both children acting as partners in guilt rather than adversaries. Lisa's participation in the prank (albeit accidentally) showcases a darker, more morally complex side of her character.

- Martin's survival method - special anti-wedgie briefs with extra-extendable elastics - is a clever callback to his character's consistent victimization through wedgies throughout the series.

- Nelson's detective work represents one of his most significant character-development moments, showing intelligence and deductive reasoning rarely displayed by the school bully.

- Lisa's imagined concern about prison wasn't about the punishment itself, but rather being forced to read Danielle Steel novels instead of Joyce Carol Oates - a very Lisa-specific worry that highlights her literary snobbery.

- Homer's quote "Oh boy, dinner time! The perfect break between work and drunk" was selected by Entertainment Weekly as one of the best TV quotes of the week when the episode aired.

- The "Taj Mah-All-You-Can-Eat" restaurant is one of many punny Springfield establishment names that plays on real-world landmarks.

- The episode's production code KABF07 follows the standard Simpsons production code format, with "KABF" being the prefix used for Season 19 episodes.

- This marks one of the few times in the series where Martin Prince is central to a main plot rather than serving as a background character or minor player.

- The memorial service scene where Principal Skinner lists Martin's various unflattering nicknames demonstrates the dark humor the show is known for, even in supposedly somber moments.

- The episode aired during Season 19, which marked the series' transition into its third decade, and represented the production team's continued efforts to find fresh storytelling angles for the long-running characters.

## Continuity and Callbacks

- Martin Prince's characterization as an intelligent, nerdy, and socially awkward student who is frequently bullied remains consistent with his portrayal throughout the series.

- Nelson's bullying behavior toward Martin is well-established in series continuity, making his role as investigator particularly ironic.

- Bart's prank using Sideshow Mel's hair bone continues the series' running gag about Mel's distinctive hairstyle with the bone accessory.

- Homer's struggles with weight and diet have been recurring themes throughout the series, with notable episodes including "King-Size Homer" (S07E07) and "Brush with Greatness" (S02E18).

- The episode's portrayal of Springfield Elementary and its students maintains continuity with the established school hierarchy and social dynamics.

## Themes and Analysis

**Guilt and Conscience**
The episode's primary theme explores how Bart and Lisa cope with the belief that they've accidentally killed someone. Unlike Bart's typical carefree attitude toward his pranks, this incident forces him to confront genuine remorse. His line "Usually when I do something bad, I feel good. But not this time" represents significant character introspection rarely seen from Bart.

**Role Reversal**
Nelson's transformation from bully to detective subverts audience expectations and demonstrates that characters can possess unexpected depths. His methodical investigation and ultimate relief at Martin's survival suggest genuine care beneath his tough exterior.

**Sibling Complicity**
Bart and Lisa's shared guilt creates an unusual bond between the siblings, who typically exist in conflict. Their partnership in crime (accidental as it may be) and shared paranoia about being caught showcases a different dimension of their relationship.

**Deception and Trust**
The B-plot examines Homer's deception regarding his diet and Marge's response, which parallels infidelity investigations. The use of "Sneakers" to catch Homer emphasizes how breaches of trust, even about minor matters like dieting, can damage relationships.

**Character Complexity**
The episode demonstrates that even in Season 19, the writers could still find ways to add complexity to established characters. Both Nelson's detective skills and Lisa's involvement in an accidental near-murder showcase character dimensions not typically explored.

**Mystery and Suspense**
The episode successfully employs mystery genre conventions, including red herrings, investigative sequences, and mounting tension, demonstrating The Simpsons' ability to parody and execute different genres effectively.
