## Synopsis

After suffering a nervous breakdown from the stress of managing her chaotic household, Marge causes a traffic standoff on the Springfield Memorial Bridge. Following her release, she takes a solo vacation to the Rancho Relaxo spa resort. While she's away, Bart and Lisa are sent to stay with Patty and Selma, while Homer is left to care for Maggie at home. When Maggie escapes from the house searching for her mother, Homer must frantically find her before Marge returns.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: A Typical Morning Disaster
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The episode opens with Marge's typical overwhelming morning routine. She's responsible for everything:
- Cleaning up after the family
- Preparing breakfast
- Managing the children
- Ironing Homer's clothes
- Handling every household task

The family is oblivious to her exhaustion.

#### Scene 2: Running Late
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / School Bus Stop
**Characters:** Marge, Bart, Lisa

Bart and Lisa miss the school bus. Marge must drive them herself, adding to her stress.

In the car, Bart and Lisa fight in the backseat, pushing Marge further toward her breaking point.

#### Scene 3: Errands and Frustrations
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Marge, Maggie

Marge runs errands around town. She attempts to deal with Homer's bowling ball (needs to be flushed/fixed). Nothing goes smoothly.

She listens to the radio where DJs Bill and Marty are making cruel prank calls, which irritates her further.

#### Scene 4: The Final Straw
**Location:** Marge's car
**Characters:** Marge, Maggie

While driving, Maggie spills milk - on Homer's freshly ironed shirt AND on Marge.

This is the final straw. Something snaps in Marge.

#### Scene 5: The Bridge Standoff
**Location:** Springfield Memorial Bridge
**Characters:** Marge, Chief Wiggum, Mayor Quimby, Kent Brockman, Arnie Pye, police, crowd

Marge stops her car in the middle of the Springfield Memorial Bridge and refuses to move.

Traffic backs up in both directions. A standoff begins.

**Arnie Pye:** (from helicopter) "This is Arnie Pye with Arnie in the Sky. We've got big problems on the Springfield Memorial Bridge, people. Traffic going waaaay back in both directions."

Police surround the car. Mayor Quimby arrives to negotiate.

Kent Brockman is lowered on the Channel 6 Sky Harness to get an exclusive interview, but the winch malfunctions and he slams repeatedly into the bridge support.

The standoff becomes a media circus.

#### Scene 6: Resolution and Arrest
**Location:** Springfield Memorial Bridge / Springfield Police Station
**Characters:** Marge, Chief Wiggum, police

Eventually, Marge is coaxed out of the car. She's arrested.

Mugshots are taken of Marge - and of her distinctive beehive hairdo.

#### Scene 7: Family Intervention
**Location:** Simpson Home / Police Station
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Marge

The family realizes Marge has had a breakdown. She's been doing everything for them, and they've taken her for granted.

Homer and the family convince Marge she needs a vacation - alone, away from all of them.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 8: Planning the Vacation
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Patty, Selma

Marge books a stay at Rancho Relaxo, a spa resort.

The question becomes: who will watch the children?

Bart and Lisa will stay with Patty and Selma.

Maggie will stay home with Homer.

#### Scene 9: The Train Station Goodbye
**Location:** Springfield Train Station
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Marge boards a train to Rancho Relaxo.

Homer chases alongside the train as it departs, saying goodbye.

**Easter Egg:** A man on the platform checking his watch is modeled after director Mark Kirkland.

#### Scene 10: Rancho Relaxo Arrival
**Location:** Rancho Relaxo Resort
**Characters:** Marge, Troy McClure (on video)

Marge arrives at the beautiful spa resort.

In her room, she watches a promotional video hosted by Troy McClure.

**Troy McClure:** Introduces the resort's amenities in his signature style.

Marge begins to relax for the first time in ages.

#### Scene 11: Patty and Selma's Apartment
**Location:** Patty and Selma's Apartment
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Patty, Selma

Bart and Lisa arrive at their aunts' apartment. It's immediately clear this will be miserable.

Patty and Selma's apartment is filled with cigarette smoke and cat hair.

The aunts serve disgusting meals: tongue sandwiches, Clamato, Mr. Pibb, and soy milk.

They watch their favorite shows: MacGyver and Divorce Court.

**Notable First:** This is the first episode to show Patty and Selma's obsession with MacGyver.

**Selma:** "Richard Dean Anderson will be in my dreams tonight."

Bart and Lisa are horrified.

#### Scene 12: Homer and Maggie - Night One
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie

Homer attempts to care for Maggie on his own. He's completely out of his depth.

Bedtime is a disaster. Homer struggles to get Maggie to sleep.

Eventually, Maggie does fall asleep... but she has plans of her own.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 13: Marge's Relaxation
**Location:** Rancho Relaxo Resort
**Characters:** Marge

Marge enjoys the spa: massages, mud baths, peace and quiet.

She watches Thelma and Louise in her room, finally getting time for herself.

She's happy and stress-free for the first time in ages.

#### Scene 14: Maggie's Escape
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie

During the night, Maggie escapes from the house. She's going to find her mother.

The next morning, Homer wakes up and goes to check on Maggie.

Her crib is empty.

#### Scene 15: Homer's Panic
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer

Homer realizes Maggie is gone. He mimics the famous Home Alone poster pose - hands on cheeks, mouth open in horror.

He searches the house frantically. No Maggie.

Homer is terrified.

#### Scene 16: The Search Begins
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer, Barney

Homer calls the Department of Missing Babies to report Maggie's disappearance.

While on hold, the hold music is "Baby Come Back" by Player - darkly ironic since it's actually a breakup song.

Homer tells Barney that losing Maggie is "the worst thing he's ever done."

He searches everywhere with Barney's help but can't find her.

#### Scene 17: More Suffering at Patty and Selma's
**Location:** Patty and Selma's Apartment
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Patty, Selma

Meanwhile, Bart and Lisa continue to suffer.

The aunts snore loudly at night. The TV is always on MacGyver. The food is inedible.

The kids count the hours until Marge returns.

#### Scene 18: Maggie Found
**Location:** Springfield streets / 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie, Chief Wiggum

Chief Wiggum finds Maggie wandering the streets and returns her to Homer.

Homer is overwhelmed with relief.

To prevent any future escapes, Homer duct-tapes Maggie to her car seat.

Problem solved (Homer-style).

#### Scene 19: Marge's Return
**Location:** Springfield Train Station / 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Marge returns from her vacation, refreshed and rejuvenated.

The family is reunited. Homer has managed to keep everything together (barely).

He doesn't tell Marge about losing Maggie.

Bart and Lisa are relieved to escape Patty and Selma.

#### Scene 20: The New Normal
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family appreciates Marge a little more now.

Marge is happy to be home, and the family promises to help out more.

Things return to normal... but with a renewed appreciation for all Marge does.

## Production Notes

- Written by David M. Stern
- Directed by Mark Kirkland
- Mark Kirkland appears as an Easter egg (man checking watch at train station)
- The title references Home Alone (1990), which starred writer David Stern's brother Daniel Stern
- Homer's reaction to finding Maggie missing mimics the Home Alone poster
- Originally, Marge's trip was to a "distressed mother's institute" rather than a spa
- Troy McClure was added as a "panic button" when writers felt the episode needed more humor
- The Rancho Relaxo video music is from an old Disney Channel bumper
- Nielsen rating of 14.2, approximately 13.08 million homes
- Highest-rated Fox show that week, finishing 25th overall

## Themes

- **Underappreciated labor** - Marge does everything for the family and gets no thanks
- **Mental health** - The importance of recognizing and addressing burnout
- **Self-care** - Marge's vacation is necessary, not selfish
- **Family dysfunction** - The family literally cannot function without Marge
- **Parental incompetence** - Homer's struggles highlight Marge's skill
- **Gender roles** - Commentary on expectations placed on mothers

## Character Development

**Marge Simpson:**
- Shows she's human and has limits
- Demonstrates that self-care is necessary
- Her breakdown is a wake-up call for the family
- Returns refreshed and appreciated

**Homer Simpson:**
- Forced to appreciate what Marge does daily
- Discovers he can't manage without her
- Shows genuine fear at losing Maggie
- Learns (temporarily) to value Marge

**Bart and Lisa:**
- Realize how good they have it at home
- Suffer under Patty and Selma
- Gain appreciation for their mother

**Patty and Selma:**
- MacGyver obsession established
- Their lifestyle contrasted with the Simpsons'
- Their care of children is hilariously inadequate

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Marge-centered storylines as viable and compelling
2. Patty and Selma's MacGyver obsession
3. Rancho Relaxo as a location
4. The family's complete dependence on Marge
5. Homer's limitations as a caregiver
6. The show's ability to address burnout and mental health
7. The 50th episode milestone
