## Synopsis

After reading "The Bridges of Madison County," Marge becomes concerned that the romance has faded from her marriage. She gathers the family the next morning to discuss their experiences with love and romance. Through flashback clips from previous episodes, each family member recounts their romantic experiences - most of which ended in heartbreak. Marge worries that all love stories have sad endings, but Homer saves the day by recalling how they met and fell in love in high school. The episode ends with Homer and Marge reconnecting while the kids escape to watch Itchy & Scratchy.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Marge Reads Romance Novel
**Location:** Simpson Bedroom
**Characters:** Marge, Homer

Marge is reading "The Bridges of Madison County" in bed.

She wakes Homer to ask if he thinks the romance has gone out of their marriage.

Marge grumbles back and goes to sleep, unsatisfied with his response.

#### Scene 2: Family Meeting on Romance
**Location:** Simpson Living Room
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The next morning, Marge gathers the family to discuss romance.

She wants to explore whether love can last.

Each family member will share their romantic experiences.

The family recalls the love triangle between Grampa, Mr. Burns, and Marge's mother Jacqueline.

**Location:** Simpson Living Room / Flashback clips
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, family

Homer tells the story of how he and Marge met and fell in love in high school.

**Clips from:** "The Way We Was" (S02E12)

Their love story had a happy ending - they're still together.

**Location:** Simpson Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer and Marge passionately embrace, their romance rekindled.

**The Kids:** Run off to watch Itchy & Scratchy, uncomfortable with their parents' display.

#### Scene 12: Meta-Commentary
**Location:** Simpson Living Room (TV)
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Maggie

While watching Itchy & Scratchy, Marge notes they watch the same episodes all the time.

**Lisa:** "Well, these Itchy & Scratchy cartoons are just pasted together from pieces of old episodes."

**Meta-Joke:** This is a self-aware joke about the clip show format itself.

## The Clip Show Format

The Simpsons has produced several clip shows, often reluctantly:

**Why Clip Shows Existed:**
- Fox contractually required them to save money
- Allowed production team to meet episode quotas
- Gave animators a break

**How This One Differs:**
- Strong framing narrative about romance
- Self-aware meta-jokes about the format
- Lisa's famous critique of commercialized love
- Jon Vitti's pseudonym shows writer embarrassment
