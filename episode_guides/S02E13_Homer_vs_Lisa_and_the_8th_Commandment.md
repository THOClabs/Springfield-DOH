## Synopsis

When a cable installer offers Homer an illegal free hookup, he jumps at the opportunity. The family enjoys endless premium channels until Lisa learns about the Eighth Commandment ("Thou shalt not steal") in Sunday School and becomes terrified that Homer's soul is in jeopardy. As Homer prepares to host a pay-per-view boxing match for his friends, Lisa stages a peaceful protest, eventually convincing Homer to cut the cable - though he accidentally blacks out the entire neighborhood in the process.

### Opening Sequence

### COLD OPEN: Ancient Egypt

#### Scene 1: Mount Sinai Dream Sequence
**Location:** Ancient Egypt / Mount Sinai
**Characters:** Homer (as "Homer the Thief"), Moses (Phil Hartman), Egyptians

The episode opens with a biblical flashback. Homer appears as a thief in ancient Egypt, stealing from his neighbors.

Moses (voiced by Phil Hartman) descends from Mount Sinai carrying the stone tablets with the Ten Commandments.

**Moses:** Reads the commandments aloud, including "Thou shalt not steal."

Homer-the-Thief looks guilty as the commandment is proclaimed.

This dream sequence foreshadows Homer's moral dilemma in the present day.

**Production Note:** Phil Hartman voices Moses in his Simpsons debut episode.

---

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 2: Flanders Home - The Offer
**Location:** 740 Evergreen Terrace - Flanders Home
**Characters:** Ned Flanders, Cable Guy (Phil Hartman)

A shady cable installer approaches Ned Flanders, offering to hook up free illegal cable for a one-time fee.

Ned righteously refuses, citing his moral principles.

**Ned:** Declines the offer firmly.

#### Scene 3: Simpson Home - Homer Takes the Bait
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Cable Guy

Homer overhears the cable guy's offer to Ned. He chases after the installer and eagerly accepts the illegal hookup.

The cable guy hooks up the Simpsons with free premium cable service.

**Notable First:** Phil Hartman's first Simpsons appearance (voicing the Cable Guy).

#### Scene 4: Simpson Home - Cable Paradise
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family discovers the joys of unlimited cable:
- Premium movie channels showing Jaws, Die Hard, and Wall Street
- Sports channels
- Adult channels (which Bart discovers)

Homer becomes addicted to the endless programming.

The family initially enjoys their new entertainment options.

#### Scene 5: Bart's Entrepreneurship
**Location:** Simpson Home / Backyard
**Characters:** Bart, neighborhood kids

Bart discovers the adult channels and sees a business opportunity. He charges neighborhood kids money to watch forbidden content.

Bart becomes a junior entrepreneur, profiting from the stolen cable.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Springfield Church - Sunday School
**Location:** First Church of Springfield - Sunday School
**Characters:** Lisa, Sunday School teacher, children

Lisa attends Sunday School where the lesson focuses on the Ten Commandments, particularly the Eighth Commandment: "Thou shalt not steal."

The teacher describes Hell and the eternal punishment awaiting sinners who violate God's laws.

Lisa becomes deeply troubled, realizing her father is stealing cable.

#### Scene 7: Church - Lisa's Fear
**Location:** First Church of Springfield
**Characters:** Lisa, Reverend Lovejoy

Lisa approaches Reverend Lovejoy after the service to discuss her concerns.

She explains that someone she knows is stealing and asks about the spiritual consequences.

**Reverend Lovejoy:** Confirms that stealing is a sin that could result in damnation.

Lisa is now terrified for Homer's immortal soul.

#### Scene 8: Simpson Home - Lisa Confronts Homer
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Homer

Lisa confronts Homer about the stolen cable, explaining her fears about the Eighth Commandment and Hell.

Homer dismisses her concerns, justifying the theft with various rationalizations.

Lisa is heartbroken that Homer won't see the moral problem.

#### Scene 9: Lisa's Moral Crisis
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Lisa

Lisa wrestles with her conscience. She begins to see stealing everywhere:
- Homer's office supplies from work
- Beer mugs Homer took from Moe's
- Various petty thefts throughout the community

Lisa realizes her father isn't the only thief - society is full of small-scale stealing.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: The Boxing Match Announcement
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, family, TV

Homer sees a commercial for "The Bout to Knock the Other Guy Out!" - a pay-per-view boxing match featuring Drederick Tatum fighting for the World Heavyweight Championship.

Homer decides to host a viewing party, inviting all his friends to watch the fight on his illegal cable.

**Commercial/Announcer:** Troy McClure appears promoting the fight.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Troy McClure (Phil Hartman) in his signature role.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Drederick Tatum, the Mike Tyson parody.

#### Scene 11: Party Preparations
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart

Homer prepares for the big viewing party. In a panic, he realizes he needs to hide evidence of his various petty thefts:
- Office supplies stolen from the nuclear plant (before Mr. Burns arrives)
- Beer mugs stolen from Moe's (before Moe arrives)

Homer frantically conceals his thievery.

#### Scene 12: The Viewing Party
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers, Moe, Barney, Lenny, Carl, Apu, others

Guests arrive for the fight. Notably, Mr. Burns invites himself after learning about Homer's viewing party.

The living room fills with Homer's coworkers and bar buddies.

Everyone is excited for the championship bout.

#### Scene 13: Lisa's Protest
**Location:** Simpson Home - Front Yard
**Characters:** Lisa

Lisa refuses to participate in the stolen entertainment. She stages a peaceful protest outside the house.

She sits on the front lawn, refusing to watch the fight or be complicit in the theft.

**Lisa:** Maintains her principled stance despite family pressure.

#### Scene 14: The Fight Begins
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room / Front Yard
**Characters:** Homer, guests, Lisa, Marge, Maggie

The boxing match begins. Drederick Tatum enters the ring.

**Announcer:** "The challenger learned how to fight in the notorious province of Capital City, and honed his skills while serving time for aggravated assault and manslaughter in Springfield Prison."

Inside, the men cheer for the fight.

Outside, Marge and Maggie join Lisa in solidarity, refusing to watch.

#### Scene 15: Homer's Conscience
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, guests

Homer watches the fight but can't enjoy it. Lisa's protest weighs on his conscience.

He looks outside at his daughter, wife, and baby sitting in the cold, refusing to participate in his sin.

Homer's guilt builds throughout the match.

#### Scene 16: Homer's Decision
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, guests, Bart

Homer finally breaks. He makes an announcement to his guests:

**Homer:** "I hate to interrupt your judging me, but I wanted you to know that I've made a couple of really important decisions. Number 1: I'm cutting the cable as soon as the fight's over, and Number 2: I'm not very fond of any of you."

The guests are stunned.

#### Scene 17: Cutting the Cable
**Location:** Simpson Home - Exterior
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Lisa, Marge, Maggie

After the guests leave, Homer goes outside with wire cutters to disconnect the illegal cable.

Bart objects strenuously, but Homer is determined to do the right thing.

Lisa watches hopefully as her father finally chooses morality over convenience.

#### Scene 18: The Blackout
**Location:** Simpson Home / Springfield neighborhood
**Characters:** Simpson family, neighbors

Homer cuts a wire... and accidentally blacks out the entire neighborhood instead of just his cable.

The Simpsons stand in the dark, but Lisa is proud of her father.

Despite the mishap, Homer has done the right thing.

## Production Notes

- Written by Steve Pepoon (freelance writer)
- Directed by Rich Moore
- Originally titled "Homer vs. the 8th Commandment" but Lisa was added to make cast feel equally represented
- Phil Hartman voices Cable Guy, Moses, AND Troy McClure - three characters in one episode
- Troy McClure was named after actors Troy Donahue and Doug McClure
- Drederick Tatum's opponent Watson is based on Marvin Hagler
- Nielsen rating of 15.2, finishing 25th for the week
- Won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Animated Program

---

## The Eighth Commandment

"Thou shalt not steal" - the moral center of this episode.

The episode explores various forms of stealing:
1. **Cable theft** - The primary sin
2. **Office supplies** - Homer steals from work
3. **Beer mugs** - Homer steals from Moe
4. **Bart's scheme** - Profiting from stolen goods

Lisa's moral awakening shows how seemingly small thefts violate the same commandment as major crimes.

## Troy McClure's Debut

This episode introduces Troy McClure, who would become one of the show's most beloved recurring characters:

- Voiced by Phil Hartman
- Based on "washed up" Hollywood B-movie actors
- Name combines Troy Donahue and Doug McClure
- Famous catchphrase: "Hi, I'm Troy McClure. You may remember me from..."
- Character retired in 1998 after Phil Hartman's tragic death
- In this episode, he promotes the boxing match

- Only the second Simpsons episode to win this award

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Troy McClure as a recurring character (Phil Hartman's signature role)
2. Drederick Tatum as Springfield's boxing champion
3. Lisa as the family's moral compass
4. The show's willingness to explore religious themes
5. Phil Hartman as a key guest voice
6. The Simpsons' Emmy-winning quality
7. Homer's capacity for moral growth when pushed

---

## Legacy

- Troy McClure became a fan-favorite character until Phil Hartman's death in 1998
- "Hi, I'm Troy McClure" became a cultural catchphrase
- The episode won the Emmy, validating the show's critical acclaim
- Lisa's moral activism became a defining character trait
- Drederick Tatum returned in multiple future episodes
- Established the template for religion-focused episodes

---

## Trivia

- Phil Hartman was so effective in this episode that he became a regular guest voice
- The episode was renamed to include Lisa to make the cast feel represented
- Steve Pepoon was a freelance writer, not a regular staff member
- The Moses dream sequence mirrors the 1956 Charlton Heston film
- Homer stealing office supplies and beer mugs adds layers to the "stealing" theme
- The neighborhood blackout provides a comic ending to Homer's moral choice
- This was the show's second Emmy win in the animated program category
