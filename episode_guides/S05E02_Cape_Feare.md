## Synopsis

Bart begins receiving anonymous death threats written in blood. The culprit is revealed to be Sideshow Bob, who has been paroled from prison and is obsessed with killing Bart for repeatedly foiling his schemes. When Bob's stalking intensifies, the FBI places the Simpson family in the Witness Protection Program, relocating them to a houseboat on Terror Lake under the new identity "The Thompsons." However, Bob has strapped himself under the family's car and followed them. In a climactic confrontation on the houseboat, Bob corners Bart with a knife, but Bart stalls him by requesting that Bob perform the entire score of Gilbert and Sullivan's "H.M.S. Pinafore." Bob's lengthy performance gives the houseboat time to drift into Springfield, where Chief Wiggum arrests him. This episode is widely considered one of the greatest Simpsons episodes ever made.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Death Threats Begin
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge, Lisa

Bart receives a letter written in blood: "I'm going to kill you." He's terrified.

**Bart:** "Mom! Dad! Someone wants to kill me!"

**Homer:** "Join the club."

More threatening letters arrive over the following days, each more gruesome.

**Marge:** "We need to go to the police!"

#### Scene 2: Springfield Police Station
**Location:** Springfield Police Department
**Characters:** Bart, Marge, Chief Wiggum, Lou, Eddie

The family reports the threats. Wiggum is characteristically unhelpful at first.

**Wiggum:** "Hmm, threatening letters written in blood. Let's see who has access to blood..."

**Lou:** "Everyone, Chief."

**Wiggum:** "Good point."

They eventually trace the letters to the state prison.

#### Scene 3: Sideshow Bob's Parole Hearing
**Location:** State Prison
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Parole Board, Guards

Flashback/reveal: Sideshow Bob appears before the parole board. He's covered in "Die Bart Die" tattoos and clearly still obsessed.

**Parole Board Member:** "What about this tattoo on your chest? It says 'Die Bart Die.'"

**Sideshow Bob:** "No, that's German for 'The Bart, The.'"

**Parole Board Member:** "No one who speaks German could be an evil man! Parole granted!"

Bob is released despite obvious red flags.

#### Scene 4: Bob Stalks Bart
**Location:** Various Springfield Locations
**Characters:** Bart, Sideshow Bob

Bob begins stalking Bart throughout Springfield:
- Following him to school
- Watching him from bushes
- Appearing in windows

**Bart:** (seeing Bob everywhere) "Ahhh! Sideshow Bob!"

Each time Bob appears, ominous music plays.

#### Scene 5: The Witness Protection Program
**Location:** Simpson Home / FBI Meeting
**Characters:** Simpson Family, FBI Agents

The FBI offers the family witness protection to escape Bob.

**FBI Agent:** "We'll give you new identities and relocate you. You'll be safe."

**Homer:** "Will there be beer?"

**FBI Agent:** "Yes."

**Homer:** "I'm in!"

The family becomes "The Thompsons" and prepares to move to Terror Lake.

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: "Hello, Mr. Thompson"
**Location:** FBI Training Session
**Characters:** Homer, FBI Agents

The FBI attempts to train Homer to respond to his new name "Homer Thompson." This becomes an extended gag.

**FBI Agent:** "When I say 'Hello, Mr. Thompson' and step on your foot, you smile and nod."

The agent repeatedly says "Hello, Mr. Thompson" and stomps on Homer's foot. Homer stares blankly.

**FBI Agent:** (after many attempts) "I think he's getting it."

**Homer:** "..."

**FBI Agent:** (hours later) "This is your new name."

**Homer:** "Ohhhh! 'Homer Thompson!'"

#### Scene 7: The Move to Terror Lake
**Location:** Highway / Terror Lake Community
**Characters:** Simpson Family, FBI Agents, (Bob hidden under car)

The family drives to their new home at Terror Lake. Unknown to them, Sideshow Bob has strapped himself underneath their car.

*Dramatic shot of Bob clinging to the undercarriage*

**Bob:** (to himself) "Soon, Bart. Soon."

The family arrives at their houseboat home.

**Marge:** "This is lovely!"

**Lisa:** "A houseboat! How exciting!"

**Homer:** "Where's the beer?"

#### Scene 8: Bob Follows
**Location:** Under the car / Terror Lake
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob

Bob extracts himself from under the car, disheveled but determined. He surveys the houseboat.

**Bob:** "There you are, Bart Simpson. Soon you will meet your end."

#### Scene 9: Rakes Sequence
**Location:** Terror Lake / Docks
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob

In one of the most famous comedy sequences in television history, Bob walks through a field and repeatedly steps on rakes, each one hitting him in the face.

**Bob:** (stepping on rake) "NGGHHHH!"
(steps on another) "NGGHHHH!"
(continues for many rakes) "NGGHHHH!"

The sequence continues far longer than expected, becoming funnier with each repetition.

#### Scene 10: Houseboat Life
**Location:** Houseboat
**Characters:** Simpson Family

The family adjusts to houseboat life. They feel safe at Terror Lake.

**Bart:** "You think Bob will find us here?"

**Marge:** "Of course not, honey. We're safe now."

**Lisa:** "This is actually kind of nice."

Homer struggles with the boat's mechanics and narrow spaces.

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: Bob Boards the Houseboat
**Location:** Houseboat - Night
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Simpson Family

At night, Bob swims to the houseboat and climbs aboard. He cuts the mooring ropes, sending the boat drifting.

**Bob:** "Now, Bart... there's no escape."

He enters the cabin, knife in hand.

#### Scene 12: The Confrontation
**Location:** Houseboat - Interior
**Characters:** Bart, Sideshow Bob, Homer, Marge, Lisa, Maggie

Bob corners Bart in the main cabin. The family is terrified.

**Bob:** "Hello, Bart."

**Bart:** "Sideshow Bob!"

**Bob:** "This time, there's no escape. I'm going to kill you."

Homer, Marge, and Lisa are tied up. Bart seems doomed.

#### Scene 13: Bart's Request - H.M.S. Pinafore
**Location:** Houseboat
**Characters:** Bart, Sideshow Bob

Bart stalls for time with a desperate request.

**Bart:** "Wait! Before you kill me, I have one last request."

**Bob:** "What is it?"

**Bart:** "Could you... sing the entire score of the Gilbert and Sullivan operetta 'H.M.S. Pinafore'?"

**Bob:** (flattered) "Very well. A cultured request for a doomed boy."

Bob begins performing the elaborate Gilbert and Sullivan operetta with full theatrical commitment, complete with costume changes from a trunk on the boat.

**Bob:** (singing) "I am the Captain of the Pinafore! / And a right good captain too!"

The performance goes on... and on... and on.

#### Scene 14: Springfield Approaches
**Location:** Houseboat / Springfield Harbor
**Characters:** All

As Bob finishes his performance (over two hours later), the drifting houseboat has returned to Springfield Harbor.

**Bob:** (finishing triumphantly) "...for he is an Englishman!"

**Bart:** "Thanks for the show, Bob!"

**Bob:** "Now, where were weâ€” wait."

Bob realizes they've drifted back to Springfield. Chief Wiggum and police are waiting on the dock.

**Wiggum:** "Sideshow Bob! You're under arrest!"

**Bob:** "CURSE YOU, BART SIMPSON!"

Bob is arrested and taken away.

#### Scene 15: Resolution
**Location:** Springfield Dock
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Wiggum

The family is reunited and safe. Bob is back in prison.

**Homer:** "Well, that was close."

**Marge:** "Bart, you were so clever to keep him singing!"

**Bart:** "All those hours of watching PBS finally paid off."

**Lisa:** "Can we keep the houseboat?"

**Homer:** "No."

The family heads home as the episode ends.

## The Rake Gag

One of television's most famous comedy sequences:

- Bob steps on a rake: *WHACK* "NGGHHHH!"
- Takes a step, another rake: *WHACK* "NGGHHHH!"
- Another step, another rake: *WHACK* "NGGHHHH!"
- Continues for approximately 9 rakes
- The extended duration transforms it from simple slapstick to absurdist comedy
- The groan sound became iconic

## Cultural References

- **Cape Fear (1962/1991):** The title and plot parody the thriller films
- **Witness Protection Program:** Real FBI program for protected witnesses
- **Gilbert and Sullivan:** Victorian-era comic opera composers (H.M.S. Pinafore, 1878)
- **Psycho (1960):** Shower scene imagery
- **The Night of the Hunter (1955):** Bob's pursuit of children
- **German Language Joke:** Subversion of assumptions about language

---

## Episode Themes

1. **Obsession and Vendetta:** Bob's single-minded pursuit of Bart
2. **Wit Over Violence:** Bart defeats Bob through cleverness, not strength
3. **Institutional Failure:** The parole board's incompetence
4. **Art and Culture:** Bob's love of high culture is his weakness
5. **Family Protection:** The family works together to survive

## Trivia

- Consistently ranked as one of the top 5 Simpsons episodes of all time
- The rake gag was extended during production because it kept getting funnier
- Kelsey Grammer performed all of Bob's Gilbert and Sullivan singing
- The "Hello, Mr. Thompson" sequence was also extended for comedic effect
- Originally written for Season 4 but held over to Season 5
- Parodies both the 1962 and 1991 versions of "Cape Fear"
- Won an Emmy Award for Alf Clausen's music
