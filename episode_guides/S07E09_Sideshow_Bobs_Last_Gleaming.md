## Synopsis

At Springfield Minimum Security Prison, Sideshow Bob grows increasingly disgusted with his fellow inmates' obsession with mindless television, particularly Krusty the Clown's show. Believing television is a "fountain of mindless drivel," Bob escapes during work duty at a local Air Force base during an air show. By impersonating a colonel, he gains access to a restricted hangar and steals a 10-megaton nuclear weapon. Bob broadcasts a televised ultimatum: Springfield must eliminate all television broadcasting within two hours or he will detonate the bomb. Mayor Quimby capitulates and TV stations shut down, but Krusty continues broadcasting from a civil defense bunker using the Emergency Broadcast System. Bob tracks down the pirate broadcast, kidnaps Bart, and commandeers a Duff blimp. After the nuclear bomb proves to be a non-functional dud, Bob attempts to crash the Wright Brothers' replica airplane into Krusty's shack. His plans are foiled, and Bob is recaptured. This episode marks the fifth major appearance of Sideshow Bob and the directorial debut of Dominic Polcino.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Prison Television Room
**Location:** Springfield Minimum Security Prison
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Prisoners, Rupert Murdoch (on TV)

Prisoners gather around the television laughing at Krusty's show, which features a food drive competition using donated items as obstacle course props. Bob sits apart, visibly disgusted by the mindless entertainment.

**Bob:** "Television! That vast wasteland of formulaic dreck!"

**Prisoner:** "Shut up, Bob! Krusty's on!"

Rupert Murdoch appears on screen defending the Fox Network, which gives Bob an idea.

**Bob:** (eyes narrowing) "Hmm..."

#### Scene 2: Air Show Announcement
**Location:** Springfield
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Various Springfield Residents

The Simpson family and other residents prepare to attend the Springfield Air Show at the nearby Air Force base.

**Homer:** "An air show! I love things that fly through the air and might crash!"

**Marge:** "It should be educational for the children."

**Bart:** "I wanna see jets blow stuff up!"

**Lisa:** "I want to learn about aerospace engineering."

#### Scene 3: Prison Work Detail
**Location:** Air Force Base
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Prison Guards, Military Personnel

Bob is part of a prison work detail at the air show. He observes the layout of the base, noting security protocols and restricted areas.

**Guard:** "Keep working, prisoners! No funny business!"

Bob spots a colonel entering a restricted hangar and begins formulating his plan.

#### Scene 4: The Air Show
**Location:** Springfield Air Force Base - Air Show
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Milhouse, Martin, Todd Flanders, Colonel Hapablap, Various Attendees

The Simpson family arrives at the air show. Various displays and demonstrations are underway.

**Colonel Hapablap:** "Welcome to the Springfield Air Show! You'll see some of the finest killing machines ever built!"

Milhouse climbs into a stationary jet display.

**Milhouse:** (pretending to fire missiles) "Pew pew pew! Take that, Mom and Dad! That's for making me eat vegetables!"

**Homer:** "Ooh, look! Free hot dogs!"

Lisa examines the historical aircraft displays with interest.

#### Scene 5: Bob's Infiltration
**Location:** Air Force Base - Restricted Hangar
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Guards

Bob slips away from the work detail. He steals a colonel's uniform and impersonates him to gain access to the restricted hangar.

**Guard:** "Identification, sir?"

**Bob:** (in authoritative voice) "Colonel... Hapablap. I'm here to inspect the special weapons."

**Guard:** "Of course, Colonel."

Bob enters the hangar and finds the 10-megaton nuclear weapon. He whistles "We'll Meet Again" as he works to steal it.

#### Scene 6: The Ultimatum
**Location:** Various - Television Broadcast
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Mayor Quimby, Kent Brockman, Springfield Residents

Bob broadcasts his message across Springfield's television stations. He appears on screen with the nuclear bomb visible behind him.

**Bob:** "I've stolen a nuclear weapon. If you do not rid this city of television within two hours, I will detonate it."

**Kent Brockman:** "This just in! A mad bomber threatens to destroy Springfield!"

**Mayor Quimby:** (sweating) "Uh, we must take this threat seriously, er, ah..."

Bob's broadcast continues.

**Bob:** "Television is an insult to our collective intelligence. The irony that I must use this medium to condemn it is not lost on me."

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 7: Springfield's Response
**Location:** City Hall / Various Springfield Locations
**Characters:** Mayor Quimby, Kent Brockman, Various Residents, Dr. Hibbert

Springfield panics. Mayor Quimby addresses the public.

**Quimby:** "In accordance with the, er, ah, terrorist's demands, all television broadcasting will cease immediately!"

TV stations begin their farewell broadcasts. Representatives of television programming appear for a final goodbye, including parody versions of Tom Baker's Doctor Who and Steve Urkel.

**Dr. Hibbert:** "A world without television? I guess there goes VH1. Heh heh heh."

#### Scene 8: Maggie's Flower Scene
**Location:** Outdoor Area
**Characters:** Maggie Simpson

In a scene referencing the famous 1964 "Daisy" political advertisement, Maggie picks flower petals while ominous nuclear countdown imagery plays.

**Maggie:** (pulling petals) "..."

*Nuclear explosion mushroom cloud imagery*

This scene parodies the Lyndon Johnson campaign's infamous anti-Goldwater advertisement.

#### Scene 9: The War Room
**Location:** Air Force Base - Command Center
**Characters:** Colonel Hapablap, Military Officers

The military attempts to track Bob. Their command center visually parodies the War Room from Dr. Strangelove.

**Hapablap:** "What is your major malfunction?! Find that bomb!"

**Officer:** "Sir, we've lost all trace of the fugitive!"

**Hapablap:** "I want planes in the air! I want choppers! I want everything!"

#### Scene 10: Television Shutdown
**Location:** Various Springfield Homes
**Characters:** Springfield Residents

Springfield's TV stations go dark. Residents stare at blank screens in horror.

**Homer:** (staring at static) "Nooooo! My precious television!"

**Lisa:** "We could read books, Dad."

**Homer:** "Books are for people who can't afford televisions!"

#### Scene 11: Krusty's Pirate Broadcast
**Location:** Civil Defense Bunker
**Characters:** Krusty the Clown, Sideshow Mel

Krusty refuses to give up. He finds a civil defense bunker with Emergency Broadcast System equipment.

**Krusty:** "The survivors would envy the dead? Ha! Not if Krusty has anything to say about it!"

**Sideshow Mel:** "Are you sure this is legal, Krusty?"

**Krusty:** "Legal? I'm Krusty the Clown! The show must go on!"

Krusty begins broadcasting improvised content over the EBS frequency.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 12: Bob Discovers Krusty
**Location:** Bob's Hideout / Civil Defense Bunker
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Krusty

Bob is furious when he discovers Krusty is still broadcasting.

**Bob:** "What?! That insufferable clown is still on the air! This will not stand!"

Bob tracks the signal to its source.

#### Scene 13: The Duff Blimp
**Location:** Springfield Air Space
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart Simpson

Bob commandeers the Duff Blimp floating over Springfield. He spots Bart below and kidnaps him.

**Bart:** "Sideshow Bob!"

**Bob:** "Hello, Bart. You're coming with me. Consider yourself a hostage."

**Bart:** "Man, not again!"

Bob uses the blimp to fly toward Krusty's bunker location.

#### Scene 14: The Bomb Is a Dud
**Location:** Duff Blimp
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart

Bob prepares to detonate the nuclear weapon, but discovers it won't work.

**Bob:** (pressing buttons frantically) "What?! This bomb is... it's a dud!"

**Bart:** "Ha! Your plan is ruined!"

**Bob:** "No matter. I shall find another way to destroy that clown and you, Bart Simpson!"

The 10-megaton weapon is revealed to be non-functional, a Cold War-era prop.

#### Scene 15: The Wright Flyer Chase
**Location:** Air Force Base / Sky
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart, Military Personnel, Colonel Hapablap

Bob abandons the blimp and attempts to commandeer various aircraft. Military forces pursue him.

**Hapablap:** "Get that man! Scramble everything!"

Bob ultimately steals the replica Wright Brothers airplane from the historical display.

**Bob:** "The Wright Brothers' plane! It's poetic, really. The birth of flight will bring the death of Krusty!"

**Bart:** "This thing can barely fly!"

**Bob:** "It doesn't need to fly far!"

#### Scene 16: The Shack Attack
**Location:** Civil Defense Bunker Area
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart, Krusty, Colonel Hapablap, Military

Bob attempts to crash the Wright Flyer into the shack where Krusty is broadcasting. The antique plane sputters through the air.

**Krusty:** "What theâ€”?!"

**Bob:** "Die, Krusty! Die!"

The plane moves slowly and awkwardly. Military forces easily intercept Bob.

#### Scene 17: Bob's Capture
**Location:** Ground Level
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart, Colonel Hapablap, Chief Wiggum

The Wright Flyer crashes harmlessly. Bob is captured by military police and Chief Wiggum.

**Wiggum:** "Sideshow Bob, you're under arrest... again."

**Bob:** "Curse you all! I would have succeeded if not for that infernal clown's stubbornness!"

**Bart:** "Face it, Bob. You can't beat television."

**Bob:** "Perhaps not, Bart. Perhaps not. But I'll be back!"

Bob is led away in handcuffs.

#### Scene 18: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Simpson Family

Television is restored to Springfield. The family watches TV, relieved.

**Homer:** "Sweet, sweet television. I'll never leave you again."

**Lisa:** "You know, Bob did have a point about the quality of programming."

**Homer:** "Shh! Krusty's on!"

**Marge:** "Well, at least everything's back to normal."

The family settles in to watch Krusty, as Springfield returns to its television-obsessed ways.

## Episode Themes

1. **Media Criticism:** Bob's hatred of television's "mindless drivel" is a satirical commentary on programming quality
2. **Hypocrisy:** Bob uses television to condemn television, acknowledging his own irony
3. **Institutional Incompetence:** Military and government fail to prevent a prisoner from stealing a nuclear weapon
4. **The Power of Entertainment:** Despite Bob's efforts, television cannot be stopped; Krusty's determination to broadcast prevails
5. **Obsession and Vendetta:** Bob's ongoing vendetta against Bart and Krusty continues

## Trivia

- This was Dominic Polcino's directorial debut on The Simpsons; he described it as a "tough one to start with"
- The role of Colonel Hapablap was specifically written for R. Lee Ermey
- Writers stayed in the office until the middle of the night to come up with the name "Hap Hapablap"
- Spike Feresten received writing credit, but the staff completely rewrote the script; very little of his original draft remained
- Bill Oakley called it "one of the most arduous re-writes in the history of the show"
- Rupert Murdoch personally approved his caricature's appearance after censors initially objected
- An early draft featured Hans Moleman flying an early flying machine at the air show
- Milhouse's jet missile fantasy scene inspired the later episode "A Milhouse Divided"
- The episode finished 49th in ratings for its broadcast week with a Nielsen rating of 8.7

## Production Notes

- **Production Code:** 3F08
- **Showrunners:** Bill Oakley and Josh Weinstein
- **First episode directed by Dominic Polcino**, who had previously been an assistant director
- Complex action sequences with the Wright Flyer were particularly challenging to animate
- The script underwent extensive rewrites from Feresten's original draft
- R. Lee Ermey's dialogue was difficult to write; the team wanted to honor his iconic military persona
- Episode is considered a parody of "'60s-era nuclear war movies"

---

## Reception

In its original broadcast, "Sideshow Bob's Last Gleaming" finished 49th in ratings for the week of November 20-26, 1995, with a Nielsen rating of 8.7 and a 13 share of the audience. It was the fourth highest-rated show on Fox that week behind The X-Files, Beverly Hills, 90210, and Melrose Place.

Ben Rayner of the Toronto Star listed "Cape Feare," "Sideshow Bob's Last Gleaming," and "Brother From Another Series" among the best episodes of the series, writing "forget Frasier, these are Kelsey Grammer's best roles."
