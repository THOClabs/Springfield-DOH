## Synopsis

When a new show featuring ventriloquist Arthur Crandall and his dummy Gabbo becomes a sensation, Krusty the Clown's ratings plummet and his show is cancelled. Depressed and forgotten, Krusty seems destined for obscurity until Bart and Lisa decide to help their hero stage a comeback. They organize an elaborate prime-time special featuring an all-star lineup of celebrity guests including Johnny Carson, Bette Midler, the Red Hot Chili Peppers, and more.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Gabbo's Mysterious Arrival
**Location:** Springfield - Various
**Characters:** Springfield residents

A mysterious marketing campaign takes over Springfield. Billboards, TV ads, and signs everywhere ask: "Who is Gabbo?"

The viral campaign creates massive curiosity and anticipation.

#### Scene 2: Gabbo's Debut
**Location:** Television studios / Springfield homes
**Characters:** Arthur Crandall, Gabbo, viewers

The mystery is revealed: Gabbo is a ventriloquist dummy operated by Arthur Crandall. Gabbo has a Jerry Lewis-style voice and a catchphrase: "I'm a bad widdle boy!"

Gabbo's show debuts at 4 PM - directly competing with Krusty's time slot.

Springfield is instantly captivated.

#### Scene 3: Krusty's Decline
**Location:** KBBL Studios / Springfield
**Characters:** Krusty, Sideshow Mel, viewers

Gabbo's show becomes a sensation. Krusty's ratings plummet dramatically.

**Newspaper headline:** "Gabbo Fabbo, Krusty Rusty"

Even Mayor Quimby publicly uses Gabbo's catchphrase. The Itchy & Scratchy Show is acquired by Gabbo's program.

Krusty tries to compete by creating his own dummy, but it fails miserably.

#### Scene 4: Cancellation
**Location:** KBBL Studios
**Characters:** Krusty, network executives

Krusty's show is officially cancelled. After 35 years in show business, he's finished.

**Krusty:** "35 years in show business and already no one remembers me, just like what's-his-name..."

Krusty falls into depression.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Bart and Lisa's Plan
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, family

Bart and Lisa are unimpressed by Gabbo. They still love Krusty and decide to help him.

First, Bart hatches a scheme to expose Gabbo's true nature.

#### Scene 6: The Gabbo Incident
**Location:** KBBL Studios
**Characters:** Bart, Gabbo, Kent Brockman

Bart sneaks into the studio and provokes Gabbo into saying something profane on a hot mic.

Unfortunately, the plan backfires - Kent Brockman is standing nearby and gets blamed. Kent is fired, not Gabbo.

#### Scene 7: The Comeback Special Idea
**Location:** Simpson Home / Various
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Krusty

Bart and Lisa come up with a new plan: organize a prime-time comeback special for Krusty featuring major celebrity guests.

They begin recruiting stars.

#### Scene 8: Getting Krusty in Shape
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Krusty

Before the special, Krusty needs to get back in shape - both physically and mentally.

Bart and Lisa help him train and regain his confidence.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: Recruiting the Stars
**Location:** Various celebrity locations
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, and all guest stars

Bart and Lisa travel to recruit celebrity guests for the special:

- **Johnny Carson** - The legendary talk show host agrees to appear
- **Hugh Hefner** - The Playboy founder joins
- **Bette Midler** - The singer/actress signs on
- **Luke Perry** - Revealed to be Krusty's half-brother
- **Barry White** - The soul legend agrees
- **Elizabeth Taylor** - Hollywood royalty participates
- **Red Hot Chili Peppers** - The band will perform

Each celebrity cameo has its own comedic moment.

#### Scene 10: Red Hot Chili Peppers Scene
**Location:** Recording studio
**Characters:** Krusty, Red Hot Chili Peppers

Krusty asks the Red Hot Chili Peppers to change the lyrics to "Give It Away."

This references Ed Sullivan asking The Doors to change the lyrics to "Light My Fire."

The band performs in their signature style.

#### Scene 11: The Comeback Special
**Location:** Television studio
**Characters:** Krusty, all celebrity guests, audience

The comeback special airs in prime time. It's a spectacular variety show featuring all the celebrity guests.

Each star performs or appears in sketches with Krusty:
- Johnny Carson does a classic talk show segment
- Bette Midler sings
- Red Hot Chili Peppers rock out
- Luke Perry and Krusty bond as half-brothers
- Barry White provides smooth vocals
- Elizabeth Taylor makes a glamorous appearance
- Hugh Hefner adds Playboy sophistication

The special is a massive success.

#### Scene 12: Krusty's Return
**Location:** KBBL Studios / Springfield
**Characters:** Krusty, Bart, Lisa, Springfield residents

The comeback special revitalizes Krusty's career. His show is back on the air.

Gabbo's popularity fades as quickly as it rose. The fickle public has moved on.

Krusty thanks Bart and Lisa for saving his career. He's back on top - for now.

## Production Notes

- **Writer:** John Swartzwelder
- **Director:** David Silverman
- **Inspiration:** Followed model of "Homer at the Bat" with celebrity guests
- **Challenges:** Many celebrities declined; extensive rewrites needed
- **Johnny Carson:** Initially objected to being portrayed as a "freeloader"; script was rewritten
- **Red Hot Chili Peppers:** Directed by George Meyer; told to ad-lib lines
- **Rejected celebrities:** Rolling Stones, Wynonna Judd, and others declined
- **Near cancellation:** The comeback special portion was almost scrapped

---

## Guest Star Details

### Johnny Carson
- One of his few post-retirement TV appearances
- Required script rewrites to get him to agree
- Originally portrayed unfavorably; changed to be more respectful

### Red Hot Chili Peppers
- Directed by George Meyer
- Ad-libbed much of their dialogue
- "Give It Away" lyric change parodies Ed Sullivan/Doors incident

### Luke Perry
- Was at peak "Beverly Hills, 90210" fame
- Plays both himself AND Krusty's half-brother

### Elizabeth Taylor
- Brief but memorable appearance
- Hollywood royalty lending credibility

## "Gabbo" as Antagonist

Gabbo represents:
- The threat of new media/trends
- How quickly audiences can abandon old favorites
- The power of marketing over substance
- The superficiality of entertainment trends

His catchphrase "I'm a bad widdle boy!" perfectly captures vapid catchphrase comedy.
