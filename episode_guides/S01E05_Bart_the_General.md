## Synopsis

When Bart defends Lisa from school bully Nelson Muntz, he becomes Nelson's new target. After failed attempts to fight back using Homer's advice, Bart turns to Grampa, who introduces him to Herman, a one-armed military surplus store owner. Together they train the neighborhood kids into an army that defeats Nelson with a water balloon assault, culminating in a peace treaty.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence

**Note:** This episode features a shortened opening sequence - one of only two Season 1 episodes without a chalkboard gag or couch gag.

---

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Home - Kitchen
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge

Lisa bakes cupcakes for Miss Hoover's birthday party at school. She's proud of her creation.

#### Scene 2: Springfield Elementary - Schoolyard
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Playground
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Nelson Muntz, Nelson's goons

Lisa brings her cupcakes to school. Nelson Muntz and his gang intercept her in the schoolyard. They stomp on the box of cupcakes, destroying Lisa's hard work.

Bart witnesses his sister's distress and stands up to defend her. This triggers a confrontation with Nelson.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Nelson Muntz.

#### Scene 3: After School - The First Beating
**Location:** Near Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart, Nelson

Nelson beats up Bart after school. He warns Bart to expect the same punishment the next day.

Bart returns home bruised and humiliated.

#### Scene 4: Simpson Home - Parental Advice
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Marge, Homer

Bart's parents offer conflicting advice:

**Marge** encourages Bart to reason with Nelson, use diplomacy, and try to understand why Nelson acts the way he does. She calls Bart her "special little guy."

**Homer** urges Bart to "bend the rules and fight dirty." He believes the only way to deal with a bully is to fight back harder.

#### Scene 5: Springfield Elementary - Failed Attempt
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, Nelson

Bart follows Homer's advice and tries to fight dirty. Nelson still beats him up, even worse than before.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Bart's Bedroom - Nightmares
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Bart's Room
**Characters:** Bart (dreaming), Nelson (in nightmare), Marge (in dream)

Bart has vivid nightmares about Nelson stalking him. In one dream sequence, Marge appears calling Bart her "special little guy."

He also envisions his own funeral - a dark fantasy of what will happen if Nelson keeps beating him.

#### Scene 7: Springfield Retirement Home
**Location:** Springfield Retirement Castle
**Characters:** Bart, Grampa Simpson, Jasper

Bart visits Grampa at the Springfield Retirement Home, seeking advice from the toughest member of the Simpson family.

Grampa recognizes Bart's plight and knows exactly who can help.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Springfield Retirement Home.

#### Scene 8: Herman's Military Antiques
**Location:** Herman's Military Antiques Store
**Characters:** Bart, Grampa, Herman

Grampa introduces Bart to Herman, the "crazed one-armed proprietor" of a military surplus store. Herman is a Vietnam veteran with expertise in military tactics.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Herman.

Herman and Grampa devise a military strategy to defeat Nelson. They teach Bart about:
- Strategic planning
- Troop recruitment
- Tactical assault operations
- The element of surprise

Bart learns he needs to build an army.

#### Scene 9: Recruitment Montage
**Location:** Various - Springfield Elementary, neighborhoods
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse, various Springfield kids

Bart recruits Nelson's other victims - nearly all of his friends and classmates. Everyone who has been bullied by Nelson joins the cause.

The children are eager for revenge and willing to follow Bart's leadership.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: Training Montage
**Location:** Simpson backyard, playground, various training grounds
**Characters:** Bart, Grampa, Herman, child army

A training montage shows Bart drilling his army for combat:
- Physical training exercises
- Climbing the jungle gym at sunset (homage to Full Metal Jacket)
- Water balloon construction and deployment
- Tactical maneuvers
- Chain of command structure

Herman oversees the operation from Bart's treehouse, which serves as the command center.

#### Scene 11: The Patton Parody
**Location:** Training grounds
**Characters:** Bart, child soldiers, Grampa

In a famous scene parodying the 1970 film Patton, one child complains he "can't stand the barking anymore."

Bart slaps the child, shouting: "I won't have cowards in my army!"

**Grampa intervenes:** "You can push them out of a plane, you can march them off a cliff, you can send them off to die on some godforsaken rock, but for some reason you can't slap them. Now apologize to that boy right now."

**Production Note:** Several lines of dialogue, the slap, and the music were lifted directly from the 1970 film Patton (composed by Jerry Goldsmith). Fox owned the rights, making this possible.

#### Scene 12: The Battle - Water Balloon Assault
**Location:** Springfield streets/park
**Characters:** Bart, Nelson, Nelson's goons, child army, Homer, Grampa

Bart leads his forces into battle. Herman commands from the treehouse.

Nelson confronts Bart, sneering: "You and what army?"

**Bart:** "This one."

Bart's water-balloon-armed troops emerge from hiding places, surrounding Nelson and his cronies on all sides.

**Bart** (commanding): "Artillery, COMMENCE SATURATION BOMBING!"

The children unleash a devastating barrage of water balloons. Nelson's goons surrender immediately.

**Comedic moment:** Homer walks by and is hit by a water balloon thrown by Grampa, who gleefully says: "He-he-he, got him."

Nelson, completely soaked and outnumbered, has no choice but to surrender. He is taken prisoner.

#### Scene 13: Victory Celebration - V-J Day Parody
**Location:** Springfield streets
**Characters:** Lisa, random boy in sailor suit, celebrating kids

In a parody of the famous V-J Day photograph of a sailor kissing a nurse in Times Square, a random boy in a sailor suit kisses Lisa.

Lisa promptly slaps him in the face.

#### Scene 14: Peace Treaty
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Nelson, Herman, Marge, kids

With Herman mediating, Bart and Nelson negotiate a peace treaty.

Key terms:
- Nelson agrees to recognize "Bart's right to exist"
- Hostilities between the parties will cease
- A formal document is drawn up and signed

Marge celebrates the peace by providing everyone with fresh cupcakes - bringing the story full circle from Lisa's destroyed cupcakes at the beginning.

#### Scene 15: Closing Address
**Location:** Direct to camera
**Characters:** Bart

Bart delivers a closing address directly to the audience about the nature of war and conflict, in a somber tone that parodies war documentaries.

## Production Notes

- First episode written by John Swartzwelder, who would become the show's most prolific writer
- Herman's design (except missing arm) was inspired by Swartzwelder himself
- Herman's voice (Harry Shearer) was partly inspired by George H.W. Bush
- Dana Hill was originally cast as Nelson but didn't attend recording; Nancy Cartwright took over
- Fox's ownership of Patton allowed direct use of its soundtrack
- One of only two Season 1 episodes with shortened opening (no chalkboard/couch gag)

---

## Reception

In its original broadcast, "Bart the General" finished 31st in weekly ratings with a Nielsen rating of 14.3 (approximately 27 million viewers). It was the highest-rated show on Fox that week.

---

## Trivia

- Nelson would evolve from pure antagonist to occasional ally ("Bully Turned Buddy")
- Herman's one arm is never fully explained in this episode
- The water balloon assault is one of the show's earliest elaborate action sequences
- This episode establishes Bart's leadership capabilities
- Grampa's military knowledge becomes a recurring character trait

## Themes

- **Standing up to bullies** - The central message about confronting oppression
- **Family support** - Each family member offers help in their own way
- **Leadership** - Bart discovers his ability to lead and organize
- **Diplomacy vs. force** - Marge's peaceful approach vs. Homer's aggressive one
- **Community action** - The bullied children unite against a common enemy
- **War satire** - Parodies military culture while telling a kid-friendly story

---

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Nelson Muntz as Springfield's primary school bully
2. Herman as an eccentric recurring character
3. Grampa's military background
4. Springfield Retirement Castle location
5. Bart's potential as a leader
6. John Swartzwelder's distinctive writing voice
