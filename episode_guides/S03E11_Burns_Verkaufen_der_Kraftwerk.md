## Synopsis

When Mr. Burns sells the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant to German investors for $100 million, Homer fears for his job. After the new German owners, Hans and Fritz, conduct a thorough evaluation, they fire Homer for his incompetence. Meanwhile, Burns realizes he misses the power and fear he once commanded. When Burns discovers the plant will cost another $100 million to bring up to code, he shrewdly buys it back for only $50 million and rehires Homer.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Homer's Stock Decision
**Location:** Simpson Home / Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, stockbroker

Homer owns 100 shares of Springfield Nuclear Power Plant stock. Needing quick cash, he sells all his shares for mere quarters each.

Shortly after, the stock price skyrockets to $52 per share. Homer realizes he sold too early and missed out on a fortune.

#### Scene 2: The German Investors
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Hans, Fritz, Homer, Moe, barflies

Two German businessmen, Hans and Fritz, are drinking at Moe's Tavern. They overhear conversations about the nuclear plant and become interested in acquiring it.

Homer inadvertently gives them the idea to buy the plant through his bar talk.

#### Scene 3: Burns Sells the Plant
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Hans, Fritz

Mr. Burns, in an unusual mood, decides to sell the power plant to the German investors for $100 million.

Burns believes he can enjoy retirement and that his legacy is secure. Smithers is concerned but follows his boss's decision.

The sale is completed, and Hans and Fritz become the new owners.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 4: The German Evaluation
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Hans, Fritz, Horst, plant employees

The new German owners immediately begin a thorough evaluation of the plant and all employees. They are shocked by the substandard conditions and poor worker performance.

The Germans bring in Horst as an efficiency expert to assess every worker.

#### Scene 5: Homer's Interview
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Office
**Characters:** Homer, Hans, Fritz, Horst

Homer is called in for an evaluation interview. He's nervous about his lax work ethic being exposed.

When the Germans mention their homeland, Homer daydreams about "The Land of Chocolate" - an elaborate fantasy sequence where he frolics through a world made entirely of chocolate.

**THE LAND OF CHOCOLATE SEQUENCE:**
- Homer imagines Germany as a literal land of chocolate
- He runs through streets of chocolate, eating everything
- Chocolate dogs, chocolate lampposts, chocolate buildings
- He finds a chocolate shop selling chocolate at half price
- One of the most iconic fantasy sequences in Simpsons history

When Homer snaps back to reality, he fails to answer the Germans' questions intelligently.

#### Scene 6: Homer Gets Fired
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Hans, Fritz, Horst

The Germans determine that Homer is grossly incompetent as a safety inspector. They fire him immediately.

Homer returns home unemployed and depressed.

#### Scene 7: Homer's Depression
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer struggles with unemployment. The family tries to support him, but his spirits are low.

Marge worries about their financial situation.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 8: Burns Without Power
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns, now retired, discovers that life without the plant is empty. People no longer fear him. He's just another old man.

Burns realizes his identity was tied to being the powerful owner of the nuclear plant.

#### Scene 9: Confrontation at Moe's
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Moe, barflies (Lenny, Carl, Barney)

Homer, drowning his sorrows at Moe's, encounters Mr. Burns. With nothing left to lose, Homer publicly confronts and humiliates Burns.

The bar patrons, who no longer fear Burns, join in mocking him. They chant "Nobody loves you!" at the former plant owner.

Burns is devastated. He never realized how despised he truly is without his power.

**Burns:** (realizing the truth) "Nobody loves me..."

#### Scene 10: The Germans' Dilemma
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Hans, Fritz, Horst

Hans and Fritz complete their full assessment of the plant. The findings are catastrophic - it will cost another $100 million to bring the facility up to code.

They've spent $100 million on a plant that needs $100 million more in repairs. They're desperate to get out of the deal.

#### Scene 11: Burns Strikes Back
**Location:** Burns Manor / Power Plant
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Hans, Fritz

Burns senses the Germans' desperation. He approaches them with an offer to buy back the plant.

**Burns:** "You will find it most unfair."

Burns offers only $50 million - half what he sold it for. The Germans, trapped by the repair costs, have no choice but to accept.

Burns regains control of the power plant at a massive profit.

#### Scene 12: Homer's Return
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers

With Burns back in charge, he rehires Homer. Everything returns to the status quo.

Burns regains his power, his employees' fear, and his identity. Homer has his job back.

Burns stabs a toy pig symbolically, representing his judgment on the situation.

## Production Notes

- **Writer:** Jon Vitti
- **Director:** Mark Kirkland
- **Original concept:** Writers initially wanted Japanese investors but changed to German to avoid clich√©s
- **Horst character:** Based on Sergeant Schultz from "Hogan's Heroes"
- **Phil Hartman:** Provided German dialogue help since he knew some German; voiced Horst and stockbroker
- **Title translation:** "Burns Verkaufen der Kraftwerk" is intentionally incorrect German; correct would be "Burns verkauft das Kraftwerk"

## "The Land of Chocolate" Sequence

This fantasy sequence became one of the most beloved moments in Simpsons history:

- **Trigger:** Homer hears "Germany" and thinks of chocolate
- **Imagery:** Entire world made of chocolate
- **Elements:** Chocolate dogs, streets, buildings, lampposts
- **Highlight:** A chocolate store with chocolate on sale at half price
- **Music:** Whimsical orchestral score
- **Legacy:** Frequently referenced and parodied; became a ride at Universal Studios

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. The iconic "Land of Chocolate" fantasy sequence
2. Homer's job is only safe because of Burns' personal attachment to power
3. Burns' identity is entirely tied to being feared
4. The status quo is resilient - major changes get undone
5. Phil Hartman's versatility in voicing multiple characters
