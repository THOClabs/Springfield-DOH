## Synopsis

When Homer notices that Lenny and Carl enjoy unexplained privileges at work, he discovers they belong to an ancient secret society called the Stonecutters. After learning his father Grampa is a member, Homer gains admission and enjoys the society's perks. However, he accidentally destroys their sacred parchment and faces expulsion - until the Stonecutters discover a birthmark proving Homer is "The Chosen One" destined to lead them. Homer's attempt to use his power for good backfires spectacularly.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence

**Chalkboard Gag:** "Adding 'just kidding' doesn't make it okay to insult the principal"
**Couch Gag:** M.C. Escher-style impossible stairs couch gag

---

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Unexplained Privileges
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Lenny, Carl

Homer notices Lenny and Carl receiving special treatment at work.

Better chairs, better service, mysterious perks.

Homer is jealous and confused about where these benefits come from.

#### Scene 2: Homer Investigates
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer, Lenny, Carl

Homer follows Lenny and Carl after work.

He discovers them entering a mysterious building.

He witnesses a secret meeting through a window.

#### Scene 3: The Stonecutters Revealed
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer (observing), Stonecutters

Homer learns the society is called the Stonecutters.

Many Springfield men are members - Wiggum, Skinner, Moe, and more.

The society controls many aspects of Springfield life.

#### Scene 4: Homer Tries to Join
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Number One, Stonecutters

Homer attempts to gain membership.

Number One (Patrick Stewart) explains the rules: one must either be the son of a Stonecutter or save a Stonecutter's life.

Homer is rejected.

#### Scene 5: Dinner Table Discovery
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Grampa, family

Homer complains about the Stonecutters at dinner.

Grampa casually reveals he is a member.

Homer realizes he qualifies as "son of a Stonecutter."

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Homer's Initiation
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters, Number One

Homer undergoes painful initiation rituals.

He is paddled, subjected to various trials.

He becomes Stonecutter #908.

#### Scene 7: "We Do" Musical Number
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** All Stonecutters

The Stonecutters perform their famous song, boasting about controlling world events.

The Emmy-nominated musical number showcases the society's supposed power.

Homer joins in enthusiastically.

#### Scene 8: Enjoying the Perks
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters

Homer revels in Stonecutter privileges:
- Better service at restaurants
- Special seating everywhere
- A secret road to avoid traffic
- Rollerskates for faster commute (instead of parking)

Life as a Stonecutter is good.

#### Scene 9: The Sacred Parchment Disaster
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge - Banquet
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters, Number One

During a celebratory dinner, Homer accidentally destroys the Hallowed Sacred Parchment.

The ancient document is irreplaceable.

The Stonecutters are horrified.

#### Scene 10: Punishment
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters

Homer is stripped of his Stonecutter robes.

He is sentenced to walk home naked - the ultimate disgrace.

His membership is revoked.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: The Birthmark Discovery
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer (naked), Stonecutters

Before Homer leaves, the Stonecutters notice something.

Homer has a birthmark in the shape of the Stonecutter emblem.

According to prophecy, this marks him as "The Chosen One."

#### Scene 12: Homer Crowned
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters, Number One

In a scene parodying "The Last Emperor" and "The Man Who Would Be King," Homer is crowned.

He becomes the new leader of the Stonecutters.

The members worship him.

#### Scene 13: Boredom Sets In
**Location:** Stonecutters Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters

Being worshipped becomes boring for Homer.

The Stonecutters simply agree with everything he says.

There's no fun in having absolute power.

#### Scene 14: Lisa's Advice
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Lisa

Homer asks Lisa what to do with his power.

Lisa suggests he have the Stonecutters perform volunteer work for the community.

Homer agrees this is a good idea.

#### Scene 15: The Stonecutters Revolt
**Location:** Various community locations
**Characters:** Homer, Stonecutters

Homer commands the Stonecutters to do good deeds.

They perceive this as "going mad with power."

The members are miserable helping the community.

#### Scene 16: "No Homers"
**Location:** New secret location
**Characters:** Stonecutters (without Homer)

The Stonecutters abandon Homer.

They form a new organization: "The Ancient Mystic Society of No Homers."

Homer is explicitly excluded from membership.

#### Scene 17: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Homer is despondent about losing his secret club.

Marge consoles him: he's already a member of a very selective club - the Simpson family.

To initiate Homer into "the family," Bart and Lisa paddle his butt.

Homer is happy again.

## Production Notes

- **Writer:** John Swartzwelder
- **Director:** Jim Reardon
- **Story by:** David Mirkin (conceived the idea)
- **Inspiration:** Mirkin heard about Freemasonry on religious radio
- **"We Do" song:** Added at Matt Groening's suggestion
- **Lyrics creation:** Writers "threw in as many things that annoyed them as possible"
- **Emmy nomination:** Song nominated for Outstanding Music and Lyrics (1995)
- **Patrick Stewart:** Iconic voice casting as Number One

## Trivia

- David Mirkin conceived the idea while driving home late at night
- He was listening to religious radio discussing Freemasonry
- John Swartzwelder wrote the script at Mirkin's request
- Patrick Stewart recorded his lines in one session
- "We Do" was added during development, not in original script
- Mr. Burns being low-ranked (#29) is a running gag
- The M.C. Escher couch gag is one of the most visually complex
- "No Homers" allows one Homer (a play on "No Homer**s**")
- The birthmark placement is deliberately absurd
