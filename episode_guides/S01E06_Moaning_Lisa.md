## Synopsis

Lisa falls into a deep depression that her family struggles to understand. After hearing distant saxophone music one night, she sneaks out and meets Bleeding Gums Murphy, a jazz musician who teaches her to express her sadness through the blues. Meanwhile, Homer becomes obsessed with beating Bart at a boxing video game. Marge initially advises Lisa to hide her feelings to fit in, but after seeing how this backfires, she changes course and tells Lisa to always be herself.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Home - Breakfast
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Morning at the Simpson house. Lisa is clearly depressed - she defers cupcakes to Homer and Bart at breakfast, showing no interest in food or conversation.

The family notices something is wrong but doesn't understand what.

#### Scene 2: Springfield Elementary - Band Practice
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Music Room
**Characters:** Lisa, Mr. Largo, students

Lisa's depression affects her performance in band practice. Instead of playing the assigned music, she improvises sad jazz melodies.

Mr. Largo, her music teacher, criticizes her for showing "creativity" rather than following the sheet music. He discourages her self-expression.

**Note:** Mr. Largo was inspired by a music teacher Matt Groening had as a child.

#### Scene 3: Springfield Elementary - Lunch/Gym
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Cafeteria/Gymnasium
**Characters:** Lisa, classmates, gym teacher

Lisa shows no effort during a food fight at lunch. In gym class, she is too sad to participate in dodgeball.

Principal Skinner sends a note home informing Homer and Marge that Lisa was "too sad to play dodge ball."

#### Scene 4: Simpson Home - After School
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart

After school, Bart and Homer play a boxing video game called "Super Slugfest." Homer suffers his 49th consecutive loss to Bart.

The game shows Homer's boxer being lowered into a grave while Bart's boxer dances on the grave - a humiliating defeat animation.

Homer becomes obsessed with finding a way to beat Bart.

#### Scene 5: Simpson Home - The Note
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Homer and Marge receive Principal Skinner's note about Lisa. Homer, so accustomed to Bart being in trouble, reflexively punishes Bart anyway - making him vacuum the entire house despite Bart doing nothing wrong.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Noiseland Video Arcade
**Location:** Noiseland Video Arcade
**Characters:** Homer, Howard, Howard's Mother

Homer goes to the local video arcade seeking advice on Super Slugfest. He finds Howard, a cocky young expert who teaches him tricks and strategies.

After spending considerable time learning, Homer feels confident he can defeat Bart.

Howard's mother arrives, scolds Howard for spending too much time at the arcade, and then scolds Homer for being a grown man in an arcade.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Noiseland Video Arcade.

#### Scene 7: Lisa's Bedroom - Homer Tries to Help
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Lisa's Room
**Characters:** Homer, Lisa, Marge

Homer attempts to discover the cause of Lisa's sadness. She tells him she wonders if the world would be any different if she didn't exist - a deeply existential question.

Homer, clearly unable to formulate a good response, stammers. Marge intervenes, suggesting Lisa take a hot bath.

#### Scene 8: Simpson Home - Super Slugfest Rematch
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Lisa

Homer and Bart play Super Slugfest again. Despite Homer's training, Bart easily wins - his 50th consecutive victory.

Homer blames the loss on Lisa's loud saxophone playing from upstairs. He storms up to her room to yell at her.

#### Scene 9: Lisa's Bedroom - Homer's Apology
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Lisa's Room
**Characters:** Homer, Lisa

Homer barges in to yell at Lisa but immediately sees how sad she is. His anger melts away and he accidentally makes her cry.

He awkwardly apologizes for yelling, feeling terrible. Homer leaves, unsure how to help his daughter.

#### Scene 10: Simpson Home - Bart's Attempt
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Bart, Lisa, Moe (phone)

Marge tells Bart to find Lisa and try to cheer her up by being nice.

Bart finds Lisa and, in his own way of being kind, performs a prank phone call on Moe for her entertainment. Lisa is not amused.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: Marge's Dream/Flashback
**Location:** Bouvier household (flashback)
**Characters:** Young Marge, Jacqueline Bouvier

Marge has a dream that is actually a memory from her childhood. Young Marge looks sad, and her mother Jacqueline tells her to smile so that people will know what a good mother she has.

The message: hide your true feelings to make others comfortable.

**Notable First:** First flashback appearance of Marge as a child; first appearance of Jacqueline Bouvier.

#### Scene 12: Car Ride - Marge's Advice
**Location:** Marge's car, driving to school
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Marge drives Lisa to band practice. During the ride, she passes on her mother's advice: smile and keep your true feelings inside so you can fit in with the other kids. This, Marge says, is the path to happiness.

Lisa reluctantly agrees to try.

#### Scene 13: Springfield Elementary - Drop-Off
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Front
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa, classmates, Mr. Largo

Marge drops off Lisa and watches from the car. Lisa puts on a fake smile and interacts with other kids.

Some boys make fun of Lisa, and she smiles through it as instructed.

Mr. Largo comes out to gather children for practice and tells Lisa he hopes not to hear any more "creativity" from her today.

Marge watches in horror as she realizes her advice is harmful - Lisa is being taken advantage of and suppressing her authentic self.

#### Scene 14: Marge's U-Turn
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Front / Marge's car
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Marge does a tire-squealing U-turn, snatches Lisa from the sidewalk, and drives off.

In the car, Marge apologizes for her previous advice. She tells Lisa:
- It's okay to be sad
- She should always be herself no matter what
- She will always have family around to help her

Marge tells Lisa she can stop smiling now. But this time, Lisa's smile is genuine - thanks to her mother's true support.

#### Scene 15: Lisa's Bedroom - Night
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Lisa's Room
**Characters:** Lisa

That night, Lisa lies in bed still processing her feelings. She hears a saxophone playing in the distance outside her window.

Curious, she sneaks out of the house to find the source.

#### Scene 16: Bridge Overpass - Meeting Bleeding Gums Murphy
**Location:** Bridge overpass in Springfield
**Characters:** Lisa, Bleeding Gums Murphy

Lisa walks through the nighttime town and discovers a man playing saxophone alone on a bridge overpass.

This is Bleeding Gums Murphy - a jazz musician playing the blues.

**Notable First:** First appearance of Bleeding Gums Murphy.

#### Scene 17: Bridge - Learning the Blues
**Location:** Bridge overpass
**Characters:** Lisa, Bleeding Gums Murphy

Bleeding Gums Murphy teaches Lisa about the blues. He explains that the blues isn't about feeling better:

**Bleeding Gums Murphy:** "The blues ain't about feeling better! It's about making other people feel worse!"

But more importantly, he teaches her that expressing sadness through music can bring a sense of peace with her misery. Lisa doesn't need to be "fixed" - she needs to express herself.

They play saxophone together through the night.

#### Scene 18: Simpson Home - Final Rematch
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Marge, Lisa

Homer is finally about to defeat Bart in Super Slugfest. Victory is within his grasp.

At that moment, Marge unplugs the game console to announce Lisa's recovery. Homer's chance at victory evaporates.

Bart declares his retirement as an undefeated video boxing champion.

#### Scene 19: The Jazz Hole - Lisa's Song
**Location:** The Jazz Hole nightclub
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Bleeding Gums Murphy, audience

The Simpsons visit a jazz club called The Jazz Hole to see Bleeding Gums Murphy perform.

He sings a blues number written by Lisa - her feelings transformed into art, shared with an appreciative audience.

Lisa is genuinely happy, having found an outlet for her emotions and acceptance from her family.

**Notable First:** First appearance of The Jazz Hole.

## Production Notes

- First episode to focus on Lisa Simpson
- Idea suggested by producer James L. Brooks, who wanted "Lisa sad but doesn't know why"
- Writers wanted to try something "really emotional and sweet" after several joke-heavy episodes
- Bleeding Gums Murphy loosely based on blues musician Blind Lemon Jefferson
- Ron Taylor guest stars as Murphy
- Lisa's song later appeared on "The Simpsons Sing the Blues" album
- This episode established Lisa's passion for jazz and melancholy personality

## Character Development

This episode significantly develops Lisa's character:
- Establishes her introspective, melancholy nature
- Introduces her passion for jazz saxophone
- Shows her intellectual depth (existential questions)
- Differentiates her clearly from Bart
- Sets up her role as the family's conscience

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Lisa's character as introspective and melancholy
2. Her passion for jazz music and saxophone
3. Bleeding Gums Murphy as her musical mentor
4. The theme of authentic self-expression
5. Marge's supportive relationship with Lisa
6. The show's ability to handle emotional, serious topics

---

## Trivia

- One of the most emotionally significant Season 1 episodes
- Bleeding Gums Murphy would return in later episodes before his death in Season 6
- The Lisa/Marge conversation in the car is considered a landmark moment for the series
- Established The Simpsons' ability to balance comedy with genuine emotion
- Mr. Largo would become a recurring antagonist for Lisa's creativity
