## Synopsis

After Homer forgets to pick up Bart from soccer practice during a rainstorm, leaving him stranded for hours, Bart becomes furious with his neglectful father. In retaliation, Bart joins the "Bigger Brothers" program, pretending to be an orphan whose father abandoned him, and is paired with Tom, a motorcycle-riding fighter pilot who becomes the cool father figure Bart always wanted. When Homer discovers Bart's deception, he exacts revenge by becoming a Bigger Brother himself to a real orphan named Pepi. The rivalry between Homer and Tom escalates into one of the most epic fights in Simpsons history, raging across Springfield before Homer ends up with his spine bent over a fire hydrant. Meanwhile, Lisa develops an expensive addiction to the Corey Hotline, running up a $378.53 phone bill listening to recorded messages from the teen idol.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Soccer Practice
**Location:** Springfield Soccer Field
**Characters:** Bart, soccer teammates

Bart finishes soccer practice on a cloudy day.

His teammates are picked up one by one by their parents.

Bart waits alone as storm clouds gather overhead.

He expects Homer to arrive any minute.

#### Scene 2: Homer Forgets
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Marge prepares to leave and reminds Homer to pick up Bart from soccer.

Homer is completely absorbed watching a game show on television.

He doesn't register Marge's words at all.

Marge leaves, trusting Homer heard her.

#### Scene 3: Bart Stranded in the Storm
**Location:** Springfield Soccer Field
**Characters:** Bart

A fierce rainstorm begins.

Bart stands alone on the empty field, getting drenched.

Hours pass. Bart becomes angrier and more desperate.

He tries to telepathically reach Homer: "Pick up Bart. Pick up Bart."

#### Scene 4: Milhouse's Trance
**Location:** Van Houten House
**Characters:** Milhouse, Kirk Van Houten

Milhouse sits in a trance-like state.

He receives Bart's psychic message backwards.

He writes "Trab pu kcip" (Pick up Bart backwards) on the wall repeatedly.

Kirk enters and is disturbed by his son's strange behavior.

This scene parodies The Shining.

#### Scene 5: Lisa Discovers the Corey Hotline
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Lisa

Lisa discovers a phone number for the Corey Hotline.

She dials and hears a pre-recorded message from teen idol Corey.

"Hi, you've reached the Corey Hotline - $4.95 a minute. Here are some words that rhyme with Corey: Glory, story, allegory, Montessori."

Lisa is immediately hooked.

#### Scene 6: Homer Finally Remembers
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Bart

Homer eventually realizes he was supposed to pick up Bart.

He rushes out, hours late.

Bart is finally retrieved, soaking wet and furious.

Bart's anger simmers - he won't forget this betrayal.

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 7: Bart's Plan for Revenge
**Location:** Simpson Home / Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart

Bart decides to get back at Homer in the most hurtful way possible.

He learns about the "Bigger Brothers" program.

It pairs fatherless boys with adult male mentors.

Bart hatches a plan to pretend he's an orphan.

#### Scene 8: Bart Joins Bigger Brothers
**Location:** Bigger Brothers Agency
**Characters:** Bart, Agency Administrator

Bart puts on a fake accent and pretends to be a poor orphan.

He claims his father abandoned him six years ago.

The administrator is moved by his story.

She assigns him a special Big Brother she's been "saving."

#### Scene 9: Tom Arrives at School
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart, Tom, students

A motorcycle roars up to the school.

Tom, a handsome fighter pilot, arrives in style.

He gives Bart his own helmet.

Bart rides off on the back of the motorcycle.

His classmates watch in awe and envy.

#### Scene 10: Marge Discovers the Phone Bill
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Marge opens the phone bill and is shocked.

$378.53 in charges to the Corey Hotline.

She confronts Lisa about her addiction.

Lisa's room is covered in Corey posters.

#### Scene 11: Tom and Bart's Adventures
**Location:** Various - Springfield Stadium, Gym, Living Room
**Characters:** Bart, Tom

Tom takes Bart to "Tomato Day" at Springfield Stadium.

They work out together at the gym, lifting weights.

Tom introduces Bart to cartoons like Ren & Stimpy.

They watch "Tuesday Night Live" together.

Bart finally has the cool dad he always wanted.

#### Scene 12: Homer Discovers the Truth
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, family

Homer finds out about Bart's Big Brother Tom.

He's hurt and jealous.

Bart rubs it in, talking about how great Tom is.

Homer decides to get his own revenge.

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 13: Homer Joins Bigger Brothers
**Location:** Bigger Brothers Agency
**Characters:** Homer, Agency Administrator

Homer signs up as a Big Brother volunteer.

When asked his reasons, he thinks: "Don't say revenge. Don't say revenge."

Out loud: "Uh, revenge?"

He's assigned a real orphan named Pepi.

#### Scene 14: Homer and Pepi
**Location:** Simpson Home - Garage
**Characters:** Homer, Pepi

Homer shows Pepi around his house.

He demonstrates the garage door: "a wonder of modern technology."

He opens and closes it repeatedly, proud of this accomplishment.

Pepi is underwhelmed but polite.

#### Scene 15: Stargazing
**Location:** Simpson Backyard
**Characters:** Homer, Pepi

Homer takes Pepi outside to look at the stars.

It's a genuine bonding moment between them.

Homer may have started this for revenge, but he's starting to care for Pepi.

Pepi appreciates having someone pay attention to him.

#### Scene 16: Lisa Promises to Stop
**Location:** Simpson Home - Lisa's Room
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Marge talks to Lisa about her Corey addiction.

She admits she had a crush on Bobby Sherman as a kid.

Lisa finds this hilarious, slightly offending Marge.

Lisa promises: "You'll never be billed for another call."

#### Scene 17: Lisa's Continued Addiction
**Location:** Various - Dr. Hibbert's Office, Retirement Castle, Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Lisa, various

Despite her promise, Lisa continues calling the hotline.

She uses the phone at Dr. Hibbert's office.

She sneaks calls from the Springfield Retirement Castle.

Principal Skinner catches her using the school phone.

Lisa's addiction is out of control.

### ACT FOUR

#### Scene 18: Marine World - Bigger Brothers Day
**Location:** Marine World
**Characters:** Homer, Pepi, Bart, Tom, dolphins

Both pairs attend "Bigger Brothers Day" at Marine World.

Homer teases a dolphin by dangling a fish just out of reach.

The dolphin steals Homer's hot dog instead.

Homer attacks the dolphin and is dragged away by security.

#### Scene 19: The Confrontation
**Location:** Marine World
**Characters:** Homer, Tom, Bart, Pepi

Bart and Homer spot each other at the event.

Tom steps between them protectively.

Homer identifies himself as Bart's father.

Tom responds angrily: "His father... the drunken gambler?"

Homer replies: "That's right. And who might you be?"

#### Scene 20: The Epic Fight Begins
**Location:** Marine World
**Characters:** Homer, Tom

Tom answers Homer's question with his fists.

The fight is brutal and immediate.

Homer throws starfish like martial arts weapons.

Tom catches them carefully and places them in a nearby tank.

A shark immediately eats them. Homer: "D'oh!"

#### Scene 21: The Fight Rages Across Springfield
**Location:** Various - Antiques Shop, Springfield Gorge, Downtown
**Characters:** Homer, Tom

The fight continues through an antiques shop.

They battle into Springfield Gorge and back up again.

The brawl ranges across downtown Springfield.

Both men refuse to give up.

#### Scene 22: Kent Brockman Reports
**Location:** News Studio / Downtown Springfield
**Characters:** Kent Brockman

Kent Brockman interrupts regular programming.

"This just in! A fistfight is in progress in downtown Springfield."

"Early reports indicate, and these are very preliminary, that one of the fighters is a giant lizard!"

"Do we have a source on this? Uh-huh... a bunch of drunken frat boys."

#### Scene 23: The Fire Hydrant Finish
**Location:** Downtown Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Tom, Bart

Tom delivers a devastating series of punches.

Homer bends backwards painfully over a fire hydrant.

His spine cracks audibly against the metal.

Tom is shocked at the severity of the injury.

Homer collapses, requiring a stretcher.

#### Scene 24: Bart's Solution
**Location:** Street / Ambulance Area
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Tom, Pepi

Bart feels guilty for causing this fight.

He sees Tom left without a little brother.

He sees Pepi left without a Big Brother.

Bart suggests: "Why don't you be Pepi's Big Brother?"

Tom and Pepi happily agree and walk off together.

#### Scene 25: Lisa's Midnight Challenge
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge, Maggie

Marge challenges Lisa to make it until midnight without calling the hotline.

Lisa tries to resist the urge.

Maggie plays with a toy telephone, making ringing sounds.

Lisa snaps: "MUST YOU BE FOREVER DIALING THAT PHONE?!"

She struggles through the night.

#### Scene 26: Reconciliation and Dirty Fighting Lessons
**Location:** Simpson Home - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart

Homer recovers from his injuries.

Bart and Homer sit on the couch together.

Bart asks Homer to teach him how to fight.

He recalls: "Remember when Tom had you in that headlock and you screamed 'I'm a hemophiliac'?"

Homer teaches: "First, you gotta shriek like a woman. And keep sobbing until he turns away in disgust. That's when it's time to kick some back."

Father and son bond over the art of fighting dirty.

## Production Notes

- Written by Jon Vitti, a key Simpsons writer
- Directed by Jeffrey Lynch
- The role of Tom was originally written for Tom Cruise, who repeatedly declined
- Phil Hartman was cast as Tom after Cruise's refusal
- The fight scene was inspired by The Quiet Man (1952) with John Wayne
- Executive producer Sam Simon suggested the writers watch The Quiet Man for ending inspiration
- The writers came in on a Saturday to watch the film together
- Tom's fighting pose parodies the Street Fighter II introduction sequence
- Sound mixing for the fight was challenging - they wanted comedy, not horror
- The fire hydrant spine crack used the smallest realistic sound effect
- The Corey Hotline was a real 1-900 number that actually existed
- Corey is a composite of Corey Feldman and Corey Haim
- The Ren & Stimpy appearance required Nickelodeon's authorization
- Nickelodeon was strict about what The Simpsons could do with their characters
- The chalkboard gag references an abandoned running joke about Skinner's toupee

**Lisa Simpson:**
- Shows susceptibility to addiction

- Struggles with self-control
- Represents how marketing targets young girls

**Tom:**
- The perfect father figure Bart always wanted
- Protective of his Little Brother
- Quick to violence when he thinks Bart is being abused
- Happily takes on Pepi when things resolve

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. One of the most elaborate and memorable fight sequences in Simpsons history
2. Phil Hartman's versatility with yet another memorable character
3. The show's ability to address parental neglect with humor
4. Lisa's susceptibility to marketing and addiction
5. Homer and Bart's complicated but enduring relationship
6. The template for epic physical comedy in the series
7. Satire of mentorship programs and their potential for exploitation
