## Synopsis

After discovering Springfield Elementary is the worst school in the state, Marge and Homer rent an apartment in the upscale Waverly Hills neighborhood so Bart and Lisa can attend a better school. While Homer enjoys a bachelor lifestyle in the apartment, Lisa struggles to fit in and only becomes popular when Bart lies about her knowing teen pop star Alaska Nebraska. The deception unravels when Alaska refuses to help, and both children ultimately choose to return to Springfield Elementary.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Marge Goes Jogging
**Location:** Springfield Streets
**Characters:** Marge

Marge jogs through Springfield and discovers a booth offering free samples of "Science Water" (a parody of VitaminWater). She drinks too many samples and desperately needs a restroom.

#### Scene 2: Springfield Elementary Discovery
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Marge, students, teachers

Marge enters Springfield Elementary to find a bathroom. Walking through the halls, she is appalled by what she sees: overcrowded classrooms, apathetic teachers, and generally poor conditions. She learns that Springfield Elementary is ranked the worst school in the state.

#### Scene 3: Family Discussion
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Marge shares her disturbing discovery with the family. Concerned about Bart and Lisa's education and future, the parents discuss options. They decide to find a way to get the children into a better school.

#### Scene 4: Finding the Apartment
**Location:** Waverly Hills neighborhood
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Cookie Kwan

The Simpsons discover that the prestigious Waverly Hills school district has excellent schools. To enroll their children, they must prove residency. Cookie Kwan helps them find a cheap apartment in Waverly Hills to establish residency.

#### Scene 5: Moving In
**Location:** Waverly Hills Apartment
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

The family sets up the apartment. Homer will live there to maintain the ruse while the children attend school. Bart and Lisa are excited about a fresh start at a new school.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Bart's Reputation
**Location:** Waverly Hills School
**Characters:** Bart, Chief Wiggum, students

Bart wants to establish himself as a "bad boy" at the new school. In an elaborate setup, Chief Wiggum arrives and "arrests" Bart in front of students, leading him away in shackles. The students are impressed and awed. It's revealed Wiggum did this as a favor - in exchange, Bart must attend Ralph Wiggum's birthday party.

#### Scene 7: Lisa's Struggles
**Location:** Waverly Hills School
**Characters:** Lisa, teachers, students

Lisa discovers that being smart isn't as special at this competitive school. She receives a B+ on a test - a shock for someone accustomed to straight A's. She struggles to make friends among the more sophisticated student body.

#### Scene 8: Homer's Bachelor Life
**Location:** Waverly Hills Apartment
**Characters:** Homer, college boys

Homer settles into the apartment and befriends his neighbors - two college students. He adopts a carefree bachelor lifestyle: playing video games, attending parties, and living without family responsibilities. He thrives in this environment.

#### Scene 9: Homer and Marge's Romance
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

With Homer living separately, he and Marge begin "dating" again. They meet up romantically, rekindling the spark in their marriage. The arrangement unexpectedly improves their relationship.

#### Scene 10: Bart's Lie
**Location:** Waverly Hills School
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, popular girls

Bart notices Lisa's social struggles and decides to help. He tells several popular girls that Lisa is friends with Alaska Nebraska, the famous teen pop star. The girls immediately become interested in Lisa.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: Lisa's Popularity
**Location:** Waverly Hills School
**Characters:** Lisa, popular girls

Lisa becomes popular overnight. Girls want to be her friend and invite her to activities. However, Lisa realizes they only care about getting backstage passes to Alaska Nebraska's upcoming concert, not about her as a person.

#### Scene 12: Alaska Nebraska Arrives
**Location:** Springfield Concert Venue
**Characters:** Lisa, Alaska Nebraska (voiced by Ellen Page), security

Alaska Nebraska comes to town for a concert. Lisa sneaks backstage to plead her case - she needs Alaska to pretend to know her to maintain her friendships. Alaska is unsympathetic and uninterested in helping. Security removes Lisa from the dressing room.

#### Scene 13: The Inspection
**Location:** Waverly Hills Apartment
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, City Inspector (voiced by Maurice LaMarche)

The school district sends a City Inspector to verify that the Simpson children actually live at the Waverly Hills address. The inspector is a menacing figure parodying Anton Chigurh from "No Country for Old Men."

Homer and Marge frantically set up the apartment with children's toys and Krusty the Clown dolls to make it look like kids live there. The inspector uses a captive bolt pistol to shoot out the lock and enters.

#### Scene 14: Inspection Conclusion
**Location:** Waverly Hills Apartment
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, City Inspector

The inspector examines the apartment and ultimately concludes that the Simpson children do reside there. However, he ominously admits he was "hoping to kill them and make it look like a suicide." The Simpsons barely escape the dangerous situation.

#### Scene 15: The Decision
**Location:** Simpson Home / Waverly Hills
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Both Bart and Lisa approach their parents with a request: they want to return to Springfield Elementary. Lisa delivers a poignant statement: she would rather "be ostracized for being me, not who I pretend to be." Authentic rejection is better than false acceptance.

#### Scene 16: Return Home
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family moves back to their original home. Springfield Elementary may be flawed, but it's where they belong. Homer and Marge decide to continue their romantic spark by using the backyard treehouse as their new "love nest."

## Production Notes

- Written by J. Stewart Burns, a long-time Simpsons writer
- Directed by Michael Polcino (credited as Mike Polcino)
- Ellen Page guest stars as Alaska Nebraska (before coming out as Elliot Page)
- Maurice LaMarche provides voice for the menacing City Inspector
- The "No Country for Old Men" parody was topical, as the film won Best Picture in 2008

---

## Trivia

- The episode satirizes parents who use fraudulent addresses to get children into better school districts
- Alaska Nebraska is a Hannah Montana parody, but Ellen Page plays her with "Juno"-like characteristics
- Chief Wiggum's fake arrest of Bart continues the tradition of him doing favors for residents
- The City Inspector's behavior mirrors Anton Chigurh's calm, menacing demeanor and unusual weapon
- Lisa's quote about preferring authentic ostracism reflects her character's values about honesty
- The treehouse ending provides a comedic callback to Homer and Marge's romance subplot
