## Synopsis

When the Simpson house's foundation begins to crumble and needs expensive repairs, Marge takes a job at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant. She quickly catches the eye of the smitten Mr. Burns, who develops an obsessive crush on her and begins unwittingly sexually harassing her in his attempts to win her affection. When Marge rebuffs his advances, Burns resorts to kidnapping her idol, singer Tom Jones, to perform a private concert. Meanwhile, Bart repeatedly cries wolf about various ailments to skip school, until a real wolf from a Krusty the Clown Show taping escapes and attacks him - but no one believes him because of his history of lying. The episode also introduces Tibor, the foreign worker Homer always blames for his mistakes.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Sinking House
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The Simpson family notices something is wrong with their house.

The foundation is crumbling - walls are cracking, floors are uneven.

A contractor examines the damage and delivers bad news: major repairs are needed.

The cost is far more than the Simpsons can afford.

#### Scene 2: Financial Crisis
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer and Marge discuss their finances. They don't have the money for repairs.

Marge considers their options. Someone needs to bring in extra income.

Marge decides she'll get a job to help pay for the foundation repairs.

Homer is hesitant but supportive.

#### Scene 3: Bart's First Illness
**Location:** Simpson Home / Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart, Marge, Mrs. Krabappel

Bart doesn't want to go to school. He pretends to be sick.

He puts on a convincing act of various symptoms.

Marge, unsure, lets him stay home.

Mrs. Krabappel is suspicious when Bart is absent.

#### Scene 4: Surly Joe's Retirement
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Surly Joe, workers

At the nuclear plant, Mr. Burns forces the "retirement" of Surly Joe.

Joe has worked at the plant for 45 years, but Burns pushes him out.

This creates a job opening at the plant.

The workers are disturbed by Burns' callousness.

#### Scene 5: Marge Applies
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - HR
**Characters:** Marge

Marge arrives at the plant to apply for the open position.

She fills out an application, exaggerating her qualifications (with Homer's advice).

Her resume includes fabricated experience and skills.

She's nervous but determined to get the job for her family.

#### Scene 6: The Interview
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Office
**Characters:** Marge, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Marge is interviewed for the position.

Mr. Burns sees Marge for the first time and is immediately smitten.

He's captivated by her blue hair and demeanor.

Marge gets the job - though it's clearly because Burns finds her attractive.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 7: Marge's First Day
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, coworkers

Marge begins her job at the nuclear plant.

Homer gives her workplace advice, including his most important tip:

"If anything goes wrong, blame the guy who can't speak English" - referring to Tibor.

Marge is uncomfortable with the suggestion but takes note.

#### Scene 8: Introducing Tibor
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Tibor, Smithers

We meet Tibor, a foreign worker who doesn't speak much English.

Homer has been blaming Tibor for his mistakes for years.

When Homer loses an important key, Smithers assumes Tibor lost it.

Despite being scapegoated, Tibor ironically gets promoted ahead of Homer.

#### Scene 9: Bart Cries Wolf Again
**Location:** Simpson Home / School
**Characters:** Bart, Marge, Mrs. Krabappel

Bart fakes illness again to avoid school.

He uses different symptoms this time.

Marge is starting to be suspicious.

Mrs. Krabappel marks another suspicious absence.

#### Scene 10: Krusty Show Taping
**Location:** Krustylu Studios
**Characters:** Krusty, Wildlife Expert, Wolf, audience

A wildlife expert appears on The Krusty the Clown Show.

She brings animals including a large hawk and an Alaskan timber wolf.

She warns that the wolf is easily spooked by loud noises.

The show's "word of the day" is unfortunately "LOUD."

#### Scene 11: The Wolf Escapes
**Location:** Krustylu Studios
**Characters:** Krusty, Wildlife Expert, Wolf, audience

Every time someone says "loud," the audience cheers loudly.

The wolf becomes increasingly terrified by the noise.

Finally, the wolf breaks free and escapes the studio!

The dangerous animal is loose somewhere in Springfield.

#### Scene 12: Burns' Advances Begin
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Marge, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Mr. Burns begins pursuing Marge at work.

He sends her flowers, gifts, and compliments.

He invites her to private dinners and offers special treatment.

Marge is uncomfortable but tries to politely decline.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 13: Workplace Harassment
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Marge, Mr. Burns

Burns' pursuit intensifies into harassment.

He touches her arm, stands too close, makes inappropriate comments.

He doesn't understand that his advances are unwelcome.

Marge tries to focus on her work while avoiding Burns.

#### Scene 14: Bart and the Wolf
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Outside/Playground
**Characters:** Bart, Wolf

Bart, having skipped school again, is wandering outside.

He encounters the escaped wolf from the Krusty show!

The wolf is large, terrifying, and aggressive.

Bart runs, screaming for help.

#### Scene 15: No One Believes Bart
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Classroom
**Characters:** Bart, Mrs. Krabappel, students

Bart bursts into the classroom yelling "WOLF! There's a wolf!"

Mrs. Krabappel and the students don't believe him.

He's cried wolf (figuratively) too many times with his fake illnesses.

They think it's just another excuse, another lie.

#### Scene 16: The Wolf Attacks
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Hallway/Outside
**Characters:** Bart, Wolf

Bart runs through the school, the wolf pursuing him.

He's genuinely terrified - this is no act.

The wolf corners Bart. The situation is dire.

Bart screams for help, but no one comes.

#### Scene 17: Groundskeeper Willie to the Rescue
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Groundskeeper Willie, Bart, Wolf

Groundskeeper Willie hears the commotion.

He finds Bart being attacked by the wolf.

Willie fights the wolf in an epic struggle - Scotsman vs. beast.

Willie defeats the wolf, saving Bart's life.

#### Scene 18: Bart's Confession
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Classroom
**Characters:** Bart, Mrs. Krabappel, students

Bart returns to class, disheveled and injured.

He decides that after all his lies, no one will believe the truth.

So he tells them he made up the wolf story.

Then he collapses. Mrs. Krabappel realizes it was all true.

---

### ACT FOUR

#### Scene 19: Marge Confronts Burns
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Office
**Characters:** Marge, Mr. Burns

Marge finally confronts Burns about his behavior.

She tells him clearly that she's married and not interested.

She loves Homer, not him.

Burns is confused - in his world, money and power get everything.

#### Scene 20: Burns' Desperate Plan
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Office
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns refuses to accept rejection.

He learns that Marge's favorite singer is Tom Jones.

He orders Smithers to bring Tom Jones to him - by any means necessary.

Smithers reluctantly agrees.

#### Scene 21: Kidnapping Tom Jones
**Location:** Tom Jones' Location
**Characters:** Smithers, Tom Jones

Smithers approaches Tom Jones with an offer: money for a private performance.

Tom Jones refuses - he doesn't do private shows for creepy billionaires.

Smithers opens a second briefcase containing knockout gas.

Tom Jones is kidnapped!

#### Scene 22: The Private Concert
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Estate
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Marge, Tom Jones (captive), Homer

Burns brings Marge to his estate for a "romantic" surprise.

Tom Jones is forced to perform "It's Not Unusual" and other hits.

Marge is horrified that Burns kidnapped her favorite singer.

Homer arrives, having followed Marge.

#### Scene 23: Homer Confronts Burns
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Estate
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Marge, Smithers

Homer confronts Burns about harassing his wife.

For once, Homer stands up to his boss for something that matters.

Burns threatens to fire Homer, but Homer doesn't back down.

Marge is touched by Homer's defense of her.

#### Scene 24: Resolution
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Estate / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Mr. Burns, Tom Jones

Burns finally accepts that Marge will never love him.

Tom Jones is freed (and may press charges).

Marge decides to quit the plant - it's not worth the harassment.

The family figures out another way to pay for the foundation.

#### Scene 25: Tom Jones Performs
**Location:** Simpson Home or Burns Estate
**Characters:** Tom Jones, Simpson family

Before leaving, Tom Jones graciously performs for the Simpson family.

He sings one of his classic hits.

It's the wholesome private concert Marge deserved.

The episode ends with music and family togetherness.

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Tibor as Homer's perpetual scapegoat
2. Bill Oakley and Josh Weinstein as major Simpsons voices
3. The show's willingness to tackle workplace harassment
4. Groundskeeper Willie as capable of heroic feats
5. The pattern of celebrity kidnapping jokes
6. Burns' inability to understand romantic rejection

---

## Trivia

- Bill Oakley and Josh Weinstein later became showrunners for Seasons 7-8
- Tom Jones genuinely enjoyed his guest appearance
- The wolf fight scene required special animation
- "It's Not Unusual" became associated with the show after this episode
- The harassment storyline was relevant to the post-Anita Hill cultural moment
- Tibor would be referenced in future episodes
- The foundation repair plot is quietly dropped and never mentioned again

---

## Critical Reception

The episode was praised for:
- Tom Jones' charming guest performance
- The parallel storylines working well together
- Groundskeeper Willie's memorable wolf fight
- Addressing workplace harassment with humor but seriousness
- Bill Oakley and Josh Weinstein's strong debut script
- Homer standing up for Marge
