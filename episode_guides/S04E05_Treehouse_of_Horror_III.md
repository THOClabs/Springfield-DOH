## Synopsis

The third annual Treehouse of Horror Halloween special features three terrifying tales told at a Halloween party. In "Clown Without Pity," Homer buys Bart a Krusty the Clown doll that turns out to be evil and tries to kill him. In "King Homer," a 1930s-style parody of King Kong, Mr. Burns captures a giant ape Homer from Ape Island and brings him to Springfield, where he falls for Marge. In "Dial 'Z' for Zombies," Bart accidentally raises the dead while trying to resurrect Snowball I, leading to a zombie apocalypse in Springfield.

The Simpsons host a Halloween party. Guests gather to tell scary stories. Each family member takes a turn telling a frightening tale, providing the framework for the three segments.

## Segment 1: Clown Without Pity

### Segment Information
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Segment Title** | Clown Without Pity |
| **Parody Of** | "Living Doll" (Twilight Zone, 1963) / Child's Play (1988) |
| **Main Characters** | Homer, Bart, Marge, Evil Krusty Doll |

### Synopsis
Homer forgets Bart's birthday and rushes to find a last-minute gift. At a mysterious shop called "House of Evil," he buys a talking Krusty the Clown doll. The shopkeeper warns Homer that the doll is cursed, but Homer ignores him. The Krusty doll comes to life and tries to kill Homer repeatedly, attacking with a knife, trapping him in the bathtub, and stalking him throughout the house. Eventually, a repairman discovers the doll's switch is set to "Evil" - when flipped to "Good," the doll becomes friendly and does housework for Homer.

### Characters
- **Homer Simpson** - Protagonist; targeted by the evil doll; eventually masters it
- **Bart Simpson** - Birthday boy; receives the cursed Krusty doll
- **Marge Simpson** - Skeptical of Homer's doll fears
- **Lisa Simpson** - Present but minor role
- **Krusty Doll** - Evil talking doll; switches from "Evil" to "Good"
- **House of Evil Shopkeeper** - Warns Homer about the curse
- **Repairman** - Discovers the "Evil/Good" switch

### Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: Forgetting the Birthday
Homer realizes he's forgotten Bart's birthday. He rushes out to find a gift.

#### Scene 2: House of Evil
Homer enters a mysterious shop. The shopkeeper sells him a Krusty doll with a warning about a curse. The doll comes with a "free frogurt" (also cursed).

**Shopkeeper:** "Take this object, but beware - it carries a terrible curse."
**Homer:** "Ooh, that's bad."
**Shopkeeper:** "But it comes with a free frogurt!"
**Homer:** "That's good!"
**Shopkeeper:** "The frogurt is also cursed."
**Homer:** "That's bad."

#### Scene 3: The Attacks Begin
The Krusty doll comes alive and begins attacking Homer - with a knife in the bathtub, chasing him through the house.

#### Scene 4: The Switch
A repairman examines the doll and finds a switch on its back: "Evil" and "Good." It was set to "Evil." Once switched to "Good," the doll becomes a helpful servant.

**Repairman:** "There's your problem. Someone set this thing to 'Evil.'"

#### Scene 5: Resolution
The now-friendly Krusty doll does housework while Homer relaxes. The doll begrudgingly serves Homer.

## Segment 2: King Homer

### Segment Information
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Segment Title** | King Homer |
| **Parody Of** | King Kong (1933) |
| **Main Characters** | Homer (as Kong), Marge, Mr. Burns, Smithers |

### Synopsis
A 1930s-style retelling of King Kong with Simpson characters. Filmmaker Carl Burns (Mr. Burns) leads an expedition to Ape Island to capture the legendary giant ape for his movie. Marge Bouvier joins as the female lead. When they find King Homer - a massive ape version of Homer - he falls in love with Marge. Burns captures King Homer using gas and brings him to Springfield for a Broadway show. King Homer escapes, rampages weakly through the city, and attempts to climb a tall building but only gets a few stories up before falling. In the end, Marge and King Homer marry.

### Characters
- **King Homer** - Giant ape; falls in love with Marge; too out of shape to climb
- **Marge Bouvier** - Actress; object of King Homer's affection; marries him
- **Mr. Burns / Carl Burns** - Film producer; leads expedition; exploits King Homer
- **Smithers** - Burns' assistant; eaten by King Homer
- **Barney** - Native on Ape Island
- **Lenny & Carl** - Expedition crew members
- **Shirley Temple-type Girl** - Eaten by King Homer during rampage

### Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: The Expedition
1930s-style black and white. Mr. Burns recruits Marge Bouvier for an expedition to capture a legendary ape.

**Burns:** "You will be my leading lady. And you will be dangled in front of a giant ape."

#### Scene 2: Ape Island
The expedition arrives at Ape Island. Natives worship King Homer, a giant Homer-ape.

#### Scene 3: Marge Meets King Homer
King Homer is captivated by Marge's beauty. He snatches her away gently.

#### Scene 4: Capture
Burns uses gas bombs to knock out King Homer. They transport him back to Springfield.

#### Scene 5: The Broadway Show
King Homer is displayed on stage. He breaks free and escapes.

#### Scene 6: The Rampage
King Homer attempts to rampage through Springfield but gets winded quickly. He tries to climb a tall building but only makes it about two stories before falling.

**Homer:** "This is... harder than it looks..."

#### Scene 7: Resolution
King Homer and Marge marry. In a twist on the tragic ending, they live happily together.

**Burns:** "He's not dead... and he's marrying my leading lady!"

## Segment 3: Dial 'Z' for Zombies

### Segment Information
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Segment Title** | Dial 'Z' for Zombies |
| **Parody Of** | Night of the Living Dead (1968) / Pet Sematary (1989) |
| **Main Characters** | Bart, Lisa, Homer, Zombie Horde |

### Synopsis
Lisa is grieving over the death of her cat Snowball I. Bart discovers a book of black magic in the school library's occult section and convinces Lisa they can resurrect Snowball. In the Springfield Cemetery at night, Bart reads the incantation incorrectly, and instead of bringing back Snowball, they raise the entire cemetery - creating a zombie apocalypse. The zombies terrorize Springfield, eating brains (including zombie Flanders eating zombie Smithers' brain). The Simpsons barricade themselves at home, but Homer is bitten. Lisa finds the counter-spell, and Bart successfully sends the dead back to their graves. Springfield returns to normal, though the town is traumatized.

### Characters
- **Bart Simpson** - Finds spell book; accidentally creates zombies; fixes his mistake
- **Lisa Simpson** - Mourning Snowball I; reluctant participant
- **Homer Simpson** - Bitten by zombies but survives; shotgun-wielding defender
- **Marge Simpson** - Terrified; fights zombies
- **Zombie Ned Flanders** - "Hey Simpson, I'm feeling a bit peckish. Mind if I chew on your brain?"
- **Zombie Horde** - Various resurrected Springfield residents
- **School Librarian** - Oversees the occult section

### Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: Mourning Snowball I
Lisa is sad about her deceased cat. Bart tries to cheer her up.

#### Scene 2: The Occult Section
At school, Bart finds a restricted occult section in the library. He takes a book of spells.

**Librarian:** "That section is forbidden!"
**Bart:** "Yeah, whatever."

#### Scene 3: The Cemetery Ritual
Bart and Lisa go to the cemetery at midnight. Bart reads the resurrection spell but mispronounces words.

**Bart:** "Kalthos... verata... nicto... uh... necktie? Nickel? I can't read this..."

#### Scene 4: The Dead Rise
Instead of just Snowball, all the dead in Springfield rise as zombies.

#### Scene 5: Zombie Apocalypse
The zombies attack Springfield. Famous zombie moments:

**Zombie Flanders:** "Hey Simpson, I'm feeling a bit peckish. Mind if I chew on your brain?"
**Homer:** "Flanders!"

Homer shoots zombie Flanders.

**Homer:** "He was a zombie?"
**Bart:** "Dad, you shot zombie Flanders!"
**Homer:** "He was a zombie?"

#### Scene 6: Simpson Home Defense
The family barricades at home. Homer fights off zombies with a shotgun. He gets bitten but survives.

**Homer:** (shooting zombies) "Take that, Washington! Eat lead, Einstein! Show's over, Shakespeare!"
**Marge:** "Homer, those are our former presidents, scientists, and artists!"
**Homer:** "They were zombies!"

#### Scene 7: Finding the Cure
Lisa finds the counter-spell in the book. They return to the cemetery.

#### Scene 8: Resolution
Bart correctly reads the counter-spell, sending the dead back to their graves. Springfield returns to normal, though everyone is traumatized.

**Lisa:** "I'm glad that's over."
**Bart:** "Yeah, but I learned an important lesson - never read a book again."

## Trivia

- The "Evil/Good" switch on the Krusty doll became an iconic visual gag
- King Homer's inability to climb the building is a self-deprecating Homer joke
- The "That's bad/That's good" exchange became a memorable comedy routine
- Homer shooting zombie Flanders and asking "He was a zombie?" became a classic moment
- The "potassium benzoate" punchline often confuses viewers who don't get the anti-joke
- This was the last Treehouse of Horror to air in October until Treehouse of Horror VI
