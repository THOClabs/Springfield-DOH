## Synopsis

A crime wave sweeps through Springfield as an elusive cat burglar strikes home after home. Lisa is devastated when her saxophone is stolen, prompting Homer to take action. When the police prove ineffective, Homer organizes a neighborhood watch that quickly devolves into a vigilante mob. The group causes more problems than they solve, but with Grampa's help, Homer discovers the burglar is a charming elderly gentleman named Molloy living at the Springfield Retirement Castle. Though arrested, Molloy tricks the entire town into a wild treasure hunt for his supposedly hidden millions, giving him the opportunity to escape from prison.

**Couch Gag:** Clapboard variant with exploding family.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Crime Wave Begins
**Location:** Various Springfield Homes
**Characters:** Springfield residents, Cat Burglar (unseen)

A mysterious cat burglar strikes Springfield, breaking into homes and stealing valuables.

**The Music:** Pink Panther-style theme plays during burglaries.

Multiple homes are hit in quick succession.

The police are completely ineffective at stopping the crimes.

#### Scene 2: The Simpson Home Burglarized
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The Simpson home is hit by the cat burglar.

**Lisa:** Discovers her beloved saxophone has been stolen.

She is devastated - the saxophone is her most prized possession.

**Homer:** Promises Lisa he will get her saxophone back.

#### Scene 3: Kent Brockman's News Report
**Location:** TV Studio / Simpson Living Room
**Characters:** Kent Brockman, Professor (on TV)

Kent Brockman delivers a fear-mongering news report.

**Kent Brockman:** "When cat burglaries start, can mass murders be far behind? This reporter isn't saying that the burglar is an inhuman monster like the Wolfman, but he very well could be. So, professor, would you say it's time for everyone to panic?"

**Professor:** "Yes, I would, Kent."

**Production Note:** Executive producer David Mirkin said this was a joke pointing out how negative news broadcasts "are always trying to scare everybody."

#### Scene 4: The Police Fail
**Location:** Springfield Police Station / Streets
**Characters:** Chief Wiggum, officers

Chief Wiggum and the Springfield Police Department prove completely incapable of catching the burglar.

Their incompetence frustrates the town.

Homer decides to take matters into his own hands.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Forming the Vigilante Group
**Location:** Simpson Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Barney, Moe, Skinner, Apu, Otto, Sea Captain

Homer proposes forming a neighborhood watch/vigilante group.

**The Pledges:**
- **Barney:** "I'm with you, Homer!"
- **McAllister:** "I be with ya too, matey."
- **Skinner:** "I'm with you, Homer."
- **Moe:** "You're the man, Homer."
- **Apu:** "You should do it."
- **Otto:** "You're the man, man."

The vigilante group is formed with Homer as leader.

#### Scene 6: The Code Names
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, vigilante group

Homer assigns code names to the group.

**Homer:** "Okay, we've got the secret vigilante handshake. Now, we need code names. I'll be Cue-ball, Skinner can be Eight-ball, Barney will be Twelve-ball, and Moe, you can be Cue-ball."

**Moe:** "You're an idiot."

#### Scene 7: The Gun Scene
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, vigilante group, Bart

The vigilante squad checks their guns inside Homer's house.

**Marge:** Expresses concern about them using guns.

**Homer:** Attempts a reassuring speech about being responsible adults who know how to handle firearms.

**The Interruptions:** Homer's speech is repeatedly cut off by accidental gun discharges from:
1. Moe
2. Moe (again)
3. Sea Captain
4. Principal Skinner
5. Moe (third time)
6. Bart

#### Scene 8: Vigilante "Justice"
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer, vigilante group, citizens

The vigilante group patrols Springfield, but their methods are problematic.

**Homer:** "Oh my God! Underage kids drinking without a permit!"

They cause more chaos than they prevent, engaging in heavy-handed tactics against citizens.

#### Scene 9: Kent Brockman Interviews Homer
**Location:** TV Studio - Smartline
**Characters:** Kent Brockman, Homer

Kent Brockman interviews Homer on his news program Smartline.

**Brockman:** "Mr. Simpson, how do you respond to the charges that petty vandalism such as graffiti is down eighty percent, while heavy sack beatings are up a shocking nine hundred percent?"

**Homer:** "Aw, you can come up with statistics to prove anything, Kent. Forfty percent of all people know that."

**Brockman:** "I see. Well, what do you say to the accusation that your group has been causing more crimes than it's been preventing?"

**Homer:** "I'd be lying if I said my men weren't committing crimes."

**Brockman:** "Well, touché."

#### Scene 10: The Cat Burglar Calls In
**Location:** TV Studio - Smartline
**Characters:** Homer, Kent Brockman, Cat Burglar (phone)

During the live broadcast, the cat burglar calls into the show.

He taunts Homer on air.

**Homer:** Out of rage, inadvertently strangles the stage manager.

**The Burglar's Announcement:** He plans to steal the world's largest cubic zirconia from the Springfield museum.

#### Scene 11: The Failed Stakeout
**Location:** Springfield Museum
**Characters:** Homer, vigilante group

Homer sets up a stakeout to catch the burglar at the museum.

**The Problem:** Homer gets drunk during the stakeout and fails.

The burglar successfully steals the cubic zirconia.

The townspeople lose faith in Homer and abandon him.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 12: Grampa Identifies the Burglar
**Location:** Springfield Retirement Castle
**Characters:** Homer, Grampa Simpson, Molloy

Homer visits Grampa at the retirement home.

**Grampa:** Recognizes the burglar's methodology and identifies him as Molloy, another resident at the Retirement Castle.

**Molloy** (Sam Neill): A charming, sophisticated elderly gentleman.

Homer confronts Molloy.

#### Scene 13: Molloy's Surrender
**Location:** Springfield Retirement Castle / Police Station
**Characters:** Molloy, Homer, Chief Wiggum, townspeople

Molloy politely surrenders and returns all the stolen goods.

He apologizes for his crimes with genuine charm.

**Lisa:** Gets her saxophone back.

The community feels sympathy for the likeable old gentleman.

**Character Note:** Molloy is "Affably Evil" - if not for his stealing, he'd just be affable.

Chief Wiggum arrests Molloy despite the community's conflicted feelings.

#### Scene 14: Molloy's Treasure Announcement
**Location:** Springfield Jail
**Characters:** Molloy, Homer, townspeople

Molloy reveals that he has millions of dollars hidden somewhere in Springfield.

**The Clue:** The money is buried beneath a big "T."

The entire town becomes obsessed with finding the treasure.

#### Scene 15: The Treasure Hunt
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Entire town

All of Springfield embarks on a wild treasure hunt.

**Visual Reference:** The sequence parodies the 1963 film "It's a Mad, Mad, Mad, Mad World."

Citizens dig frantically at every location with a "T":
- The "T" in a sign
- Various T-shaped landmarks
- Throughout the town

#### Scene 16: The Twist
**Location:** Springfield / Empty field
**Characters:** Townspeople

The town discovers the truth: there is no treasure.

**Molloy's Con:** The treasure hunt was a diversion to allow him to escape from prison.

The citizens are left in an empty field, having wasted their time digging.

**Final Shot:** The townspeople futilely continue digging, unable to accept they've been fooled.

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. John Swartzwelder's signature absurdist humor
2. Sam Neill as a beloved guest star
3. The show's critique of vigilante justice
4. Springfield's tendency toward mob mentality
5. Kent Brockman as a media satire vehicle
6. The template for "Homer leads a movement" episodes
