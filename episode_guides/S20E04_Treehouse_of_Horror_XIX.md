# Treehouse of Horror XIX - Comprehensive Episode Guide

**Season 20, Episode 4 (Production Code: KABF16)**
**Original Air Date:** November 2, 2008
**Written by:** Matt Warburton
**Directed by:** Bob Anderson

---

## Episode Overview

"Treehouse of Horror XIX" is the nineteenth installment in The Simpsons' annual Halloween special series. The episode features three self-contained segments parodying Transformers, Mad Men/celebrity culture, and the classic Peanuts special "It's the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown." Notably, this was the last Treehouse of Horror episode to be produced in standard definition 4:3 aspect ratio, and it aired on Election Day 2008.

**Episode Statistics:**
- Total Episode Number: 424th episode overall
- Treehouse of Horror Number: 19th Halloween special
- Viewership: 12.48 million viewers
- Nielsen Rating: 4.9 household rating
- Demographic Performance: Highest-rated scripted show in the 18-49 demographic for the night

## Opening Segment: Homer Votes

### Plot Summary
Before the episode proper begins, Homer attempts to vote in the 2008 United States presidential election. He enters a voting booth intending to vote for Democratic candidate Barack Obama, but the rigged electronic voting machine repeatedly registers his vote for Republican candidate John McCain instead.

### Detailed Breakdown
- Homer makes six attempts to vote for Obama
- In desperation, he tries voting for McCain, hoping reverse psychology will work
- The voting machine kills Homer to hide the evidence of vote tampering
- The machine sucks Homer inside and violently ejects his corpse
- Jasper places a patriotic "I Voted" sticker on Homer's dead forehead
- The episode title and credits appear in red, white, and blue against Homer's body

### Production Notes
- The segment was leaked online weeks before the official air date
- Executive producer Al Jean stated it was "mostly a comment on what many people believe to be the irregularities in our voting system"
- The segment was not intended to take political sides but to highlight concerns about electronic voting security

### Notable Quotes
- **Homer:** "Hello, I'd like to vote for president, governor, and anything else that will take money away from our parks and libraries."
- **Homer:** "This doesn't happen in America! Maybe Ohio, but not in America!"
- **Homer:** "Before you kill me, I gotta know, what is the one true religion?"
- **Voting Machine:** "It's a mix of voodoo and Methodist."

## Segment 1: "Untitled Robot Parody"

### Synopsis
A Transformers parody where Bart's Christmas gift to Lisa—a pink convertible toy—transforms into a robot and begins converting all of Springfield's electronics and vehicles into warring robot factions.

### Plot Details

**Act One: The Gift**
- Setting: Christmas morning at the Simpson household
- Bart needs a last-minute gift for Lisa and visits "You Forgot-Me-Nots" bargain store
- He searches through the "Only 99 cents" box
- Behind him, a red cab-over semi-truck (resembling Optimus Prime) appears, blinking its lights
- The truck transforms into a pink convertible Ferrari
- Bart gives the convertible to Lisa on Christmas morning
- Lisa proudly enjoys her gift, unaware of its true nature

**Act Two: The Awakening**
- That night, the convertible transforms and shoots laser beams
- The Ferrari shoots four transformation beams at various electronics:
  - Alarm clock → "Snooze"
  - Lamp → "Three-Way"
  - Boom-box → "Melody"
  - Off-screen device → "Sex Toy"
- The pink convertible is revealed to be "Bestimus Muchos," leader of the Posibots
- The newly transformed robots form the Posibot faction

**Act Three: Robot War**
- Homer enters his car and backs out of the driveway
- His car transforms with Homer's head stuck in the back (resembling Optimus Prime)
- Ned Flanders' car transforms with Ned in its posterior (resembling Megatron)
- The two robots battle in the street
- A transformation virus spreads throughout Springfield
- All electronics and vehicles transform into robots
- Two main factions emerge and wage war across the town
- Springfield residents hide from the robot battle

**Act Four: Resolution**
- Marge intervenes and confronts the two robot faction leaders
- She delivers a speech about how they're not so different and should work together
- The robots agree with Marge's message of unity
- Plot twist: The robots unite with a sinister purpose—to subjugate all of humanity
- The segment ends with humans enslaved by the robot alliance

## Segment 2: "How to Get Ahead in Dead-Vertising"

### Synopsis
After accidentally killing Krusty the Clown, Homer is recruited by advertising executives to murder celebrities so their likenesses can be exploited in commercials without paying royalties. The dead celebrities eventually revolt against the living.

### Plot Details

**Act One: The Opening & Accident**
- Extended parody of the Mad Men opening title sequence
- Homer falls through space as Don Draper-style silhouettes
- Set to period-appropriate music
- At an amusement park, Krusty is entertaining children but upsets Maggie
- Homer shoves Krusty in frustration
- Krusty stumbles backward into a swing
- The swing launches him onto a wheel
- The wheel throws him into a woodchipper
- Krusty is graphically killed in a Fargo-style woodchipper death

**Act Two: The Recruitment**
- Advertising executives approach Homer with a proposition
- They explain that dead celebrities can be used in ads without permission or payment
- Homer is hired as an assassin to kill famous people
- Montage of Homer killing celebrities set to "Psycho Killer" by Talking Heads
- Victims include:
  - Kate Winslet
  - Prince
  - George Clooney
  - Neil Armstrong
- Dead celebrities' images are used in various commercial campaigns
- Homer becomes increasingly efficient at celebrity assassination

**Act Three: Commercials & Campaigns**
- "Planet of the Taste" commercial (Planet of the Apes parody) featuring dead celebrities
- Various other advertising campaigns exploit deceased famous people
- In Heaven, dead celebrities gather and discuss the exploitation
- George Washington and Abraham Lincoln express outrage despite dying centuries ago
- The implication is they're offended by Presidents' Day sales ads

**Act Four: The Revolt**
- The dead celebrities become fed up with exploitation
- They return from Heaven to attack the living
- **John Wayne** leads the charge and escalates the situation
- **Jimmy Stewart** tries to be the voice of reason but is ignored
- Dead celebrities rampage through Springfield
- Various historical and entertainment figures join the assault
- **John Lennon** appears and says: "All we're saying is 'Let's eat some brains!'" (parody of "Give Peace a Chance")

**Resolution:**
- The segment ends with the dead celebrity uprising in full swing
- No clear resolution is shown, typical of Treehouse of Horror segments

## Segment 3: "It's the Grand Pumpkin, Milhouse"

### Synopsis
In a parody of "It's the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown," Milhouse waits in a pumpkin patch for the Grand Pumpkin on Halloween. His childlike belief brings the Grand Pumpkin to life, but the entity becomes a murderous monster seeking revenge for carved and eaten pumpkins.

### Plot Details

**Act One: The Pumpkin Patch**
- Halloween night in Springfield
- Milhouse sits in a pumpkin patch waiting for the Grand Pumpkin
- Lisa accompanies him because "his glasses fog up when he cries"
- Children in Peanuts-style costumes pass by
- Bart is dressed as Charlie Brown
- Milhouse explains the Grand Pumpkin mythology to skeptical listeners

**Act Two: The Summoning**
- Milhouse expresses his unwavering belief in the Grand Pumpkin
- Despite mockery from others, his faith remains strong
- Milhouse's tears and pure childlike belief bring the Grand Pumpkin to life
- A massive orange pumpkin with a face materializes
- The Grand Pumpkin speaks: "That's right, Milhouse. Your childlike belief has brought me to life."
- Milhouse is overjoyed and offers the Grand Pumpkin homemade pumpkin bread

**Act Three: The Pumpkin Bread Incident**
- Milhouse presents his gift: "I even baked you a loaf of homemade pumpkin bread!"
- Grand Pumpkin responds: "How delightful. Bread made especially for pumpkins."
- Milhouse clarifies: "Actually, it's made from pumpkins."
- The Grand Pumpkin is horrified and enraged
- He realizes humans torture and consume his pumpkin brethren
- The Grand Pumpkin observes Halloween festivities:
  - Carved jack-o'-lanterns
  - Pumpkin pies
  - Pumpkin decorations
  - Smashed pumpkins
- He vows revenge on humanity

**Act Four: The Rampage**
- The Grand Pumpkin goes on a killing spree
- He eats several Springfield residents whole:
  - Homer Simpson
  - Groundskeeper Willie
  - Nelson Muntz
- He kills Principal Skinner (method not specified but fatal)
- The Grand Pumpkin demonstrates racist attitudes toward different pumpkin varieties
- When questioned about his prejudice toward yellow pumpkins:
  - **Grand Pumpkin:** "All pumpkins are racist. The difference is I admit it!"

**Act Five: Tom Turkey's Arrival**
- Lisa realizes Milhouse's belief can bring things to life
- She tells Milhouse about "Tom Turkey," a Thanksgiving symbol
- Milhouse begins believing in Tom Turkey
- Tom Turkey materializes and kills the Grand Pumpkin
- Everyone eaten by the Grand Pumpkin is freed alive
- Tom Turkey appears as a savior momentarily

**Epilogue: The Twist**
- Tom Turkey learns that people eat turkeys on Thanksgiving
- He becomes enraged and vows his own revenge
- Tom Turkey chases children around Springfield Elementary School
- The segment ends with another holiday mascot seeking vengeance
- Cycle of holiday horror continues

## Controversy

### GLSEN Criticism
Shortly after airing, the episode was criticized by the **Gay, Lesbian and Straight Education Network (GLSEN)** for a line where Nelson Muntz uses "gay" as an insult.

**Details:**
- Organization objected to casual use of "gay" as pejorative
- Argued it promotes negative stereotypes and bullying
- Called for more responsible language in family programming
- The Simpsons producers did not publicly respond to the criticism

**Context:**
- 2008 marked increasing awareness of LGBTQ+ issues in media
- Educational organizations were actively campaigning against homophobic language
- The Simpsons had previous controversies regarding representation and language
- This criticism was part of broader cultural conversations about acceptable language on television
