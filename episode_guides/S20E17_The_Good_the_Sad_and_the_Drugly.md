## Synopsis

The episode follows two parallel storylines. Bart falls for Jenny, an 11-year-old volunteer at a retirement home, and pretends to be a good person to impress her. When Milhouse returns from suspension and discovers Bart's deception, he threatens to expose him. Meanwhile, Lisa researches Springfield's environmental future and becomes severely depressed, leading to a prescription for antidepressants that cause her to hallucinate smiley faces everywhere.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The School Prank
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse, students, faculty

Bart and Milhouse execute an elaborate prank at school, unscrewing building fixtures. The prank causes chaos but they're eventually caught.

#### Scene 2: Milhouse Takes the Fall
**Location:** Principal Skinner's Office
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse, Principal Skinner

Rather than both being punished, Milhouse is suspended for the prank. Bart escapes punishment, leaving Milhouse bitter about taking the blame alone.

#### Scene 3: Lisa's Research Project
**Location:** Springfield Elementary / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Lisa, classmates

Lisa begins researching Springfield's environmental future for a school project. She discovers alarming predictions about pollution, climate change, and societal decline.

#### Scene 4: Bart at the Retirement Home
**Location:** Springfield Retirement Castle
**Characters:** Bart, Grampa, retirement home residents, Jenny

Under false pretenses (to escape further punishment or impress someone), Bart volunteers at the retirement home. He meets Jenny, an 11-year-old girl who genuinely volunteers there.

#### Scene 5: Bart Falls for Jenny
**Location:** Springfield Retirement Castle
**Characters:** Bart, Jenny

Bart is immediately attracted to Jenny. She's kind, genuine, and represents everything Bart usually isn't. He decides to pretend to be a good person to win her over.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Lisa's Alarming Presentation
**Location:** Springfield Elementary Classroom
**Characters:** Lisa, Mrs. Hoover, classmates

Lisa presents her research to the class. Her findings about Springfield's bleak environmental future are so alarming that she becomes visibly distressed. The teacher and students are concerned.

#### Scene 7: Lisa's Diagnosis
**Location:** Doctor's Office
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge, Dr. Hibbert

After her breakdown, Lisa is taken to see a doctor. Dr. Hibbert diagnoses her with "Environment-Related Despair" - essentially depression caused by worry about the future. He prescribes antidepressants.

#### Scene 8: Bart's Good Boy Act
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Bart, Jenny

Bart continues pretending to be a good person around Jenny. He volunteers more, acts kind to elderly residents, and suppresses his usual troublemaking nature. Jenny falls for this version of Bart.

#### Scene 9: Lisa on Medication
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge, Homer

Lisa starts taking her antidepressants. At first, she seems better - more cheerful and less worried. The family is relieved.

#### Scene 10: Nelson's Romantic Advice
**Location:** School / Street
**Characters:** Bart, Nelson

Surprisingly, Nelson gives Bart romantic advice about how to handle his feelings for Jenny. Nelson proves to be unexpectedly wise about relationships.

#### Scene 11: Milhouse Returns
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse

Milhouse returns from his suspension, still angry about taking the fall for the prank. He discovers that Bart has been pretending to be good to impress a girl.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 12: Milhouse's Jealousy and Blackmail
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse

Jealous of Bart's new romance and still bitter about the suspension, Milhouse threatens to expose Bart's true nature to Jenny. He blackmails Bart, demanding favors.

#### Scene 13: Lisa's Hallucinations
**Location:** Springfield
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge

The antidepressants cause Lisa to hallucinate - she sees smiley faces everywhere. Objects, people, and even disturbing things appear happy to her. This side effect is concerning.

#### Scene 14: Marge Stops the Medication
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Seeing Lisa's disturbing hallucinations, Marge decides to discontinue the antidepressant treatment. She believes finding natural ways to cope is better than medication with such side effects.

#### Scene 15: Bart's Truth Revealed
**Location:** Retirement Home / Park
**Characters:** Bart, Jenny, Milhouse

Unable to maintain his good-boy facade forever, and with Milhouse threatening exposure, Bart's true nature is eventually revealed to Jenny. She's disappointed and hurt by his deception.

#### Scene 16: Jenny Breaks Up with Bart
**Location:** Park / Street
**Characters:** Bart, Jenny

Jenny breaks up with Bart. She can't be with someone who deceived her about who they really are. Bart is heartbroken but understands.

#### Scene 17: Lisa's Advice to Bart
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Lisa, Bart

Having dealt with her own issues (stopping medication and finding healthier coping methods), Lisa advises Bart to face problems directly rather than avoiding them. She says he needs to confront his friendship with Milhouse.

#### Scene 18: Bart Reconciles with Milhouse
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse

Taking Lisa's advice, Bart apologizes to Milhouse. He brings flowers and asks for forgiveness for letting Milhouse take the fall and for their strained friendship. They reconcile, prioritizing their long-standing friendship over the romantic drama.

#### Scene 19: Final Prank
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse

Now friends again, Bart and Milhouse execute another prank together at school, bookending the episode with their friendship restored.
