## Synopsis

It's a not-so-merry Christmas for the Simpsons when Mr. Burns decides to cut the Christmas bonuses and Marge has to spend the Christmas savings to erase a tattoo Bart thought would make a great Christmas present. Homer takes a job as a mall Santa to save Christmas, but when that fails, a trip to the dog track unexpectedly brings the family a new pet.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence
*Note: This episode lacks the standard opening sequence with blackboard gag and couch gag, as those were introduced in the second episode.*

The title "The Simpsons Christmas Special" appears on screen against a black snowy background and the camera pans down to begin the episode.

---

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Springfield Elementary School - Christmas Pageant
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School Auditorium
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Principal Skinner, various students, Dewey Largo

The Simpson family arrives late to the Springfield Elementary School Christmas pageant. Principal Skinner introduces the second grade presentation featuring Santa Clauses from around the world. Lisa steals the show performing as "Towanga, the Santa Claus of the South Seas" with a skilled torch dance.

The fourth grade then sings a medley of Christmas carols. Bart can be heard singing alternate, rude lyrics to "Jingle Bells":
> "Jingle Bells, Batman smells, Robin laid an egg..."

Bart is removed from the performance for his disruption.

#### Scene 2: Simpson Home - Evening
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

After returning home, Marge writes the Simpson family Christmas cards while Homer retrieves Christmas decorations. Bart and Lisa write their Christmas wish lists:
- **Lisa's wish:** A pony (as always)
- **Bart's wish:** A tattoo

The tattoo request sparks family conflict with Marge disapproving.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 3: Springfield Mall - Shopping Trip
**Location:** Springfield Mall
**Characters:** Marge, Bart, Lisa, Tattoo Artist

Marge takes the children shopping. While Marge and Lisa shop, Bart slips away to the tattoo parlor. He attempts to get a tattoo reading "MOTHER" as a Christmas gift. However, Marge catches him mid-process (the tattoo only reads "MOTH" at this point).

Counting on Homer's Christmas bonus, Marge spends the family's entire Christmas savings on laser tattoo removal.

#### Scene 4: Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, other workers

At the plant, Mr. Burns makes an announcement: there will be no Christmas bonuses this year. Homer is devastated, realizing the family now has no money for Christmas.

#### Scene 5: Simpson Home - The Secret
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

When Homer discovers that Marge spent the Christmas money on tattoo removal, he decides to keep the bonus cancellation secret from the family to avoid worrying them.

#### Scene 6: Moe's Tavern
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Barney Gumble, Moe

Homer drinks alone at Moe's, feeling hopeless. A cheerful Barney walks in wearing a Santa Claus suit. Barney explains he's earning extra money working as a mall Santa. Homer suddenly has an idea to save Christmas.

#### Scene 7: Mall Santa Interview
**Location:** Springfield Mall - Santa's Workshop
**Characters:** Homer, Mall Manager

Homer applies for and is interviewed to work as a mall Santa. Despite lacking qualifications, he gets the job.

#### Scene 8: Simpson Home - Patty and Selma Visit
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Patty, Selma

Homer tries to avoid Marge, but she insists he visit with her sisters Patty and Selma who have arrived. They criticize Homer, noting the family doesn't even have a Christmas tree up yet.

#### Scene 9: Springfield Mall - Santa Exposed
**Location:** Springfield Mall - Santa's Workshop
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, mall customers

Bart visits the mall Santa, not knowing it's his father. He pulls off Homer's beard, exposing him to the crowd. Homer loses his job and receives only $13.00 in pay after deductions for the Santa suit rental.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: Springfield Downs Dog Track
**Location:** Springfield Downs - Dog Racing Track
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Barney, various gamblers, Track Owner

In a desperate last attempt to afford Christmas presents, Homer takes Bart to the dog track to bet his $13. Barney advises Homer on which dogs to pick, but Homer ignores him. He chooses a dog named "Santa's Little Helper," believing the name to be a sign of a Christmas miracle.

Despite Homer's faith, Santa's Little Helper finishes dead last in the race. Homer and Bart have lost everything.

#### Scene 11: Dog Track Parking Lot
**Location:** Springfield Downs - Parking Lot
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Track Owner, Santa's Little Helper

As Homer and Bart leave dejectedly, they witness the track owner angrily firing Santa's Little Helper and abandoning the greyhound in the parking lot. The dog runs to Homer and Bart. Despite having no money, they decide to take the dog home.

#### Scene 12: Simpson Home - Christmas Morning
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Santa's Little Helper

Homer finally arrives home and admits the truth: he never got a bonus. The family is initially concerned about Christmas without presents.

Bart then introduces the family to Santa's Little Helper. Lisa is ecstatic to have a dog. Marge declares it's the best gift of all - "something to share our love, and frighten prowlers."

The family accepts their new pet with joy, realizing that Christmas is about being together, not material gifts.

## Production Notes

- This was the series premiere and the only Simpsons episode to air in the 1980s
- Originally produced as the eighth episode (production code 7G08), it aired first because "Some Enchanted Evening" had animation issues requiring fixes
- Mimi Pond wrote the episode - her only writing credit for the series
- Al Jean created the title, referencing "The Christmas Song" ("Chestnuts Roasting on an Open Fire")
- Rich Moore storyboarded the episode and designed Ned Flanders
- Eric Stefani (brother of Gwen Stefani) laid out several scenes
- The standard opening sequence (blackboard gag, couch gag) was not yet created

---

## Trivia

- The "Jingle Bells, Batman Smells" parody sung by Bart became iconic and is still referenced in pop culture
- Santa's Little Helper's presence in early episodes is inconsistent due to production order vs. air order
- This episode established the show's signature blend of heart and humor
- The episode aired during primetime following a football game, giving it strong initial ratings
