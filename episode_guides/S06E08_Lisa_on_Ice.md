## Synopsis

When Principal Skinner announces failing grades at a school assembly, Lisa is horrified to discover she's failing gym class. Desperate to improve her grade, she joins a pee-wee hockey league and is discovered by Apu to have natural goaltending talent. She becomes the star goalie for the Kwik-E-Mart Gougers while Bart plays for the rival Mighty Pigs coached by Chief Wiggum. Homer's shameless favoritism toward whichever child is winning creates intense sibling rivalry. When the teams meet in the championship game, a final penalty shot pits brother against sister. Remembering their childhood bond, Bart and Lisa drop their equipment and embrace, ending the game in a tie - which prompts a stadium riot from the disappointed crowd.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Failing Grades Assembly
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Auditorium
**Characters:** Principal Skinner, students, teachers, Ralph Wiggum

Principal Skinner gathers students to announce which subjects they are failing.

**Ralph Wiggum:** Told he's failing English.
**Skinner:** "No, no, Ralph, this means you're failing English."
**Ralph:** "Me fail English? That's unpossible!"

**Lisa:** (horrified) Discovers she is failing gym class.

This threatens her perfect academic record.

#### Scene 2: Lisa Tries Sports
**Location:** Springfield Elementary Gym
**Characters:** Lisa, gym students

Lisa attempts various sports to improve her gym grade:
- Basketball - fails
- Volleyball - fails
- Other activities - all failures

Her lack of athletic coordination is apparent.

#### Scene 3: Watching Bart's Hockey Game
**Location:** Springfield Ice Arena
**Characters:** Simpson family, Bart, Mighty Pigs team

The family attends Bart's pee-wee hockey game.

**Bart:** Plays for the **Mighty Pigs**, coached by Chief Wiggum.

**Team Name Reference:** Parodies "The Mighty Ducks" (Disney film and NHL team) while being a police pun (Wiggum = cop = "pig").

Bart excels at hockey.

#### Scene 4: Apu Discovers Lisa's Talent
**Location:** Outside Ice Arena
**Characters:** Lisa, Bart, Apu

After the game, Bart ridicules Lisa for her lack of athletic skill.

He uses his hockey stick to pelt her with litter.

**Lisa:** Instinctively deflects all the litter thrown at her.

**Apu:** (witnessing this) Throws a hockey puck at Lisa without warning.

**Lisa:** Catches it effortlessly.

**Apu:** Recognizes her natural goaltending reflexes and recruits her for his team, the **Kwik-E-Mart Gougers**.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Lisa Joins the Gougers
**Location:** Ice Arena / Practice
**Characters:** Lisa, Apu, Gougers team

Lisa becomes the goalie for the Kwik-E-Mart Gougers.

**Team Name Meaning:** "Gouge" references both violent hockey and "price gouging" - fitting for Apu's Kwik-E-Mart.

Lisa proves to be an exceptional goalie.

Her reflexes and analytical mind make her nearly unbeatable.

#### Scene 6: Homer's Favoritism Begins
**Location:** Simpson Home / Games
**Characters:** Homer, Lisa, Bart

Homer begins shamelessly favoring Lisa over Bart.

Previously Bart was the "sports star" of the family.

Now Homer lavishes attention on Lisa while ignoring Bart.

**Homer:** Switches allegiance based entirely on who's winning.

#### Scene 7: The Rivalry Escalates
**Location:** Various / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Homer

A fierce sibling rivalry develops between Bart and Lisa.

Each wants Homer's approval.

Homer alternates treating each child as "The Un-Favorite."

**Homer's Behavior:** He outdoes himself in bad parenting, pitting his children against each other.

#### Scene 8: Marge Confronts Homer
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Marge, Homer

**Marge:** Firmly rebukes Homer for his terrible parenting.

She's horrified that he's deliberately creating rivalry between their children.

Homer is unrepentant - he just wants a winner in the family.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: The Championship Game Setup
**Location:** Springfield Ice Arena
**Characters:** Everyone

The Kwik-E-Mart Gougers and the Mighty Pigs meet in the championship game.

Lisa (goalie for Gougers) vs. Bart (forward for Mighty Pigs).

The whole town turns out to watch.

**Chief Wiggum:** Lets prisoners out to watch the game if they promise to return. They refuse to even pretend they'll agree. He lets them out anyway.

#### Scene 10: The Game
**Location:** Springfield Ice Arena
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, teams, spectators

The championship game is intense.

Both teams play hard.

The game comes down to a final penalty shot.

**The Situation:** Bart must score on Lisa to win.

#### Scene 11: The Penalty Shot
**Location:** Ice Arena - Center Ice
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa

Bart skates toward Lisa for the decisive penalty shot.

**Flashback Montage:** Both Bart and Lisa remember moments from their childhood:
- Supporting each other
- Playing together
- Protecting each other
- Being siblings, not rivals

The memories overwhelm them.

#### Scene 12: The Embrace
**Location:** Ice Arena - Center Ice
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, crowd

**The Decision:** Bart and Lisa drop their equipment.

Instead of competing, they skate toward each other and embrace.

**The Outcome:** The game ends in a tie.

**Marge:** Beams with pride - her children chose love over competition.

**Homer:** (immediately) "LOSERS!"

Homer is the first in the stadium to label them both losers.

#### Scene 13: The Riot
**Location:** Springfield Ice Arena
**Characters:** Spectators, Snake

The crowd is furious about the tie.

**The Riot:** Dissatisfied spectators trash the arena.

**Snake:** (poignantly) Reflects that youth hockey could have altered his criminal trajectory. "You know, if I had played peewee hockey as a kid instead of letting my father beat the crap out of me every night, I might not be the man I am today."

## Production Notes

- Written by Mike Scully (his first Simpsons episode)
- Directed by Bob Anderson
- Scully's passion for hockey inspired the plot
- Anderson ordered NHL VHS tapes to study the sport
- Anderson attended youth hockey games for research
- Anderson took notes on stickhandling and shooting movements
- The episode features detailed, accurate hockey animation

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Mike Scully as a major Simpsons writer (later showrunner)
2. Ralph Wiggum's "unpossible" as an iconic line
3. Lisa's hidden athletic potential
4. Homer's worst parenting tendencies
5. The show's ability to balance comedy with emotional depth
6. Bart and Lisa's underlying love for each other
