## Synopsis

After the family's television breaks, the Simpsons visit an outlet mall to find a replacement. While Homer purchases a cheap "Panaphonics" TV, Marge discovers a genuine Chanel suit marked down from $2,800 to just $90. Despite her initial hesitation, the family encourages her to buy it for herself. The suit transforms Marge's self-image and opens doors to a world of upper-class society.

At a gas station, Marge runs into Evelyn, a wealthy former high school classmate, who is impressed by Marge's designer outfit and invites the family to visit the exclusive Springfield Country Club. Marge becomes obsessed with fitting in among the wealthy members, constantly altering and re-sewing her Chanel suit to create new looks for each visit. Meanwhile, Homer discovers he has natural golfing talent and is challenged to a match by Mr. Burns, who sees him as a potential partner.

During the golf match, Homer witnesses Smithers cheating for Mr. Burns by secretly replacing Burns' poorly hit balls with ones placed favorably on the green. Burns, realizing Homer knows his secret, offers the Simpsons full country club membership in exchange for his silence. Marge is thrilled at the prospect of permanent acceptance into high society.

However, when Marge's suit finally falls apart beyond repair, she spends $3,300 on a new designer dress for the membership initiation ball. At the event, Marge realizes she has been neglecting her family and changing herself to please others. In a moment of clarity, she confesses to her family about the expensive dress and decides they should leave the country club. The Simpsons happily return to Krusty Burger, embracing their authentic selves over hollow social climbing.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Living Room - TV Breaks
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The Simpson family is watching television when it suddenly breaks, prompting Homer to find a replacement.

**Homer:** "The TV's broken!"

The family decides to go shopping for a new television.

#### Scene 2: Outlet Mall
**Location:** Springfield Outlet Mall
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Store Employees

The family arrives at a discount outlet mall. They pass various knock-off stores selling counterfeit goods.

**Bart:** "These are just crappy knock-offs."

Homer examines a suspiciously cheap television set.

**Homer:** "I know a genuine Panaphonics when I see it. And look, there's Magnetbox and Sorny!"

While Homer selects a cheap "Carnivale" brand TV, Marge wanders into a clothing outlet.

#### Scene 3: Chanel Outlet Store
**Location:** Outlet Mall - Designer Clothing Store
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Lisa, Bart, Store Clerk

Marge discovers a beautiful pink Chanel suit on a clearance rack, marked down from $2,800 to $90 due to a small flaw.

**Marge:** (gasping) "A real Chanel suit... for $90?"

**Lisa:** "Mom, you should buy it for yourself."

**Homer:** "Go ahead, Marge. You never do anything for yourself."

After some hesitation, Marge purchases the suit, feeling guilty about the expense even at the discounted price.

#### Scene 4: Gas Station Encounter
**Location:** Springfield Gas Station
**Characters:** Marge, Evelyn, Apu

Marge, wearing her new Chanel suit, stops for gas. She encounters Evelyn, a wealthy woman she knew from high school.

**Evelyn:** "Attendant, I'd like some gas."

**Apu:** (pretending) "Yes, I'm sorry, I do not speak English, okay."

**Evelyn:** "But you were just talking toâ€”"

**Apu:** "Yes, yes. Hot dog, hot dog. Yes sir, no sir. Maybe, okay."

Evelyn recognizes Marge and is impressed by her designer outfit.

**Evelyn:** "Marge? Marge Bouvier? It's Evelyn! From high school!"

Evelyn invites the Simpson family to visit the exclusive Springfield Country Club.

#### Scene 5: Simpson Home - Preparing for the Club
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Marge excitedly prepares the family for their country club visit. She instructs Homer on proper attire.

**Marge:** "Homer, I don't think you should wear a short-sleeve shirt with a tie."

**Homer:** "Ohhh, but Sipowicz does it."

**Marge:** "If Detective Sipowicz jumped off a cliff, would you do that too?"

**Homer:** "Ohhh, I wish I was Sipowicz."

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Springfield Country Club - First Visit
**Location:** Springfield Country Club
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Evelyn, Susan, Roberta, Country Club Members

The Simpsons arrive at the exclusive country club. Marge is nervous but her Chanel suit gives her confidence.

**Marge:** "We're not poor!...Well, we're not."

The wealthy women scrutinize Marge's outfit and discuss fashion.

**Susan:** "L.L. Bean and Eddie Bauer are selling me the same honey."

**Roberta:** "The vest says 'Let's have lunch' but the culottes say 'You're paying.'"

Marge is accepted into the group based on her designer attire.

#### Scene 7: Golf Course - Homer's Natural Talent
**Location:** Country Club Golf Course
**Characters:** Homer, Tom Kite, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Homer wanders onto the golf course and picks up a club. Professional golfer Tom Kite (guest starring as himself) observes Homer's swing.

**Tom Kite:** "Now, you don't want to overthink."

**Homer:** "Not an issue."

**Tom Kite:** "You're a natural, Mr. Simpson."

Mr. Burns notices Homer's surprising skill from across the course.

**Mr. Burns:** "Who is that lavatory links man, Smithers?"

**Smithers:** "Homer Simpson, sir. One of the fork and spoon operators from sector 7-G."

Burns decides to challenge Homer to a golf match.

#### Scene 8: Marge's Obsession - Altering the Suit
**Location:** Simpson Home - Marge's Sewing Room
**Characters:** Marge

Marge begins obsessively altering her Chanel suit to create different looks for each country club visit. She modifies the sleeves, hemline, and collar to appear as if she has multiple designer outfits.

Each alteration becomes more desperate as Marge tries to maintain the illusion of wealth.

#### Scene 9: The Golf Match - Burns' Cheating Revealed
**Location:** Country Club Golf Course
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Homer and Mr. Burns play their golf match. Burns hits terribly, but somehow always ends up with excellent ball placement.

**Mr. Burns:** "Quit cogitating, Steinmetz, and use an open-faced club... a sand wedge!"

**Homer:** "Mmm....open faced club sandwich."

Homer discovers the truth: Smithers has been secretly replacing Burns' poorly hit balls with ones placed favorably on the green throughout Burns' entire golfing career.

Burns notices that Homer has witnessed the cheating.

#### Scene 10: Lisa on Class
**Location:** Country Club Dining Area
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge

Lisa observes the wealthy club members and quotes literature to Marge.

**Lisa:** "The rich are different from you and me."

**Marge:** "Yes, they're better."

Lisa is disturbed by her mother's changing values.

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: Burns' Proposition
**Location:** Country Club - Private Room
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns confronts Homer about witnessing the cheating scandal. Rather than threatening him, Burns offers a deal.

**Mr. Burns:** "Simpson, I'll make you a proposition. Keep quiet about what you've seen, and I'll sponsor your family for full country club membership."

Homer considers the offer, knowing how much Marge wants to be accepted.

#### Scene 12: The Suit Falls Apart
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Marge

After countless alterations, Marge's Chanel suit finally disintegrates beyond repair. Devastated, she realizes she cannot attend the membership initiation ball without proper attire.

In desperation, Marge spends $3,300 on a new designer dress - more than the original price of her discount Chanel suit.

#### Scene 13: Country Club Tension
**Location:** Springfield Country Club
**Characters:** Marge, Susan, Country Club Women

Susan makes cutting remarks about Marge's various outfit appearances.

**Marge:** "I'll be there with bells on."

**Susan:** "Bells? Where exactly will you be attaching them to that mangled Chanel suit?"

The comment stings, but Marge is determined to attend the ball.

#### Scene 14: The Membership Ball
**Location:** Country Club Ballroom
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Country Club Members

The Simpsons attend the exclusive membership initiation ball. Marge wears her expensive new dress. Homer remains silent about Burns' cheating per their agreement.

The family stands awkwardly among the wealthy guests, clearly out of place despite their formal attire.

#### Scene 15: Marge's Realization
**Location:** Country Club Ballroom
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Marge looks at her family - Homer uncomfortable in his suit, the kids bored and out of place. She realizes she has been neglecting and changing herself for people who don't truly accept her.

**Marge:** "Homey, I like your in-your-face humanity. I like the way Lisa speaks her mind. I like Bart."

She confesses about the $3,300 dress and her desperate attempts to fit in.

**Marge:** "I've been so caught up in trying to impress these people that I forgot what really matters - my family."

#### Scene 16: Leaving the Country Club
**Location:** Country Club Parking Lot / Krusty Burger
**Characters:** Simpson Family

The Simpsons decide to leave the country club ball. Instead of accepting membership, they drive to Krusty Burger.

**Homer:** "So we're not joining the country club?"

**Marge:** "No. Let's go somewhere we really belong."

The family happily eats at Krusty Burger, comfortable in their authentic selves rather than pretending to be something they're not.

## Cultural References

- **F. Scott Fitzgerald:** Lisa's quote "The rich are different from you and me" is a famous Fitzgerald line, though often attributed to a conversation with Ernest Hemingway
- **NYPD Blue:** Homer's reference to "Sipowicz" and the short-sleeve shirt with a tie parodies Detective Andy Sipowicz from the ABC police drama
- **Chanel:** The iconic fashion house represents unattainable luxury for middle-class Americans
- **Discount Outlets:** Satirizes the American obsession with brand names and discount shopping
- **Country Club Culture:** Parodies the exclusivity and snobbery of private clubs
- **Knock-off Brands:** "Panaphonics," "Magnetbox," and "Sorny" spoof Panasonic, Magnavox, and Sony
- **Tom Kite:** Real PGA golfer appearing as himself, known for his methodical approach to the game

## Episode Themes

1. **Class and Social Mobility:** The central theme explores the appeal and emptiness of upper-class aspirations
2. **Authenticity vs. Pretense:** Marge learns that pretending to be wealthy brings no genuine satisfaction
3. **Family Values:** Despite temptation, the Simpsons choose family unity over social acceptance
4. **Materialism:** The Chanel suit represents how material possessions can change one's identity
5. **Wealth and Corruption:** Burns' cheating demonstrates that the wealthy often achieve status through dishonest means
6. **Self-Worth:** Marge's journey involves learning her value doesn't depend on others' approval
7. **Satire of Elitism:** The episode mocks the superficiality and pettiness of high society

## Trivia

- **Historic Episode:** This was the first Simpsons episode written and directed by two women - Jennifer Crittenden (writer) and Susie Dietter (director)
- Jennifer Crittenden would later write for Seinfeld and Arrested Development
- Tom Kite was an active PGA Tour player at the time of this episode; he won the 1992 U.S. Open
- The title references "Scenes from the Class Struggle in Beverly Hills" (1989 film)
- The outlet mall scene satirizes the popularity of factory outlet shopping in the 1990s
- The "Panaphonics/Magnetbox/Sorny" gag has been referenced in popular culture numerous times
- Marge's Chanel suit alterations parody the lengths to which people go to appear wealthier than they are
- The episode explores similar territory to the Season 3 episode "Homer Alone" regarding Marge's identity
