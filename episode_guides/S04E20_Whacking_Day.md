## Synopsis

Springfield celebrates "Whacking Day," an annual tradition where citizens drive snakes into the town square and beat them to death with clubs. While most of the town eagerly participates, Lisa is horrified by the cruel practice and campaigns to stop it. Meanwhile, Bart is expelled from school by Principal Skinner just before Superintendent Chalmers' visit and discovers that the town's founder, Jebediah Springfield, invented Whacking Day as an excuse to beat up the Irish. Lisa recruits Barry White to help save the snakes, using his deep bass voice to lure them away from the town square to safety. The episode ends with Lisa victorious, the snakes saved, and the town shamed into abandoning the barbaric tradition.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Whacking Day Approaches
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family watches TV. A commercial announces Whacking Day is coming - Springfield's annual snake-killing festival.

**TV Announcer:** "Whacking Day is coming! Get your clubs ready!"

**Homer:** "Ooh, Whacking Day! My favorite holiday!"

**Lisa:** "You're excited about beating helpless animals to death?"

**Homer:** "It's tradition, Lisa!"

Lisa is disturbed by the enthusiasm.

#### Scene 2: Springfield Elementary - Bart's Expulsion
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, Principal Skinner, Superintendent Chalmers, Mrs. Krabappel

Principal Skinner is panicking about Superintendent Chalmers' upcoming visit. He decides to expel troublemaking students temporarily to make the school look better.

**Skinner:** "Superintendent Chalmers will be here tomorrow. We need this school to look perfect."

Skinner finds a pretext to expel Bart.

**Skinner:** "Simpson, you're expelled!"

**Bart:** "For what?!"

**Skinner:** "For... general troublemaking! You can come back after the inspection."

Bart is kicked out of school.

#### Scene 3: Lisa's Research
**Location:** Springfield Library
**Characters:** Lisa

Lisa researches Whacking Day to find ammunition against it. She's horrified by what she learns about the tradition.

**Lisa:** "There has to be something wrong with this holiday..."

She reads historical accounts but initially finds nothing helpful.

#### Scene 4: Bart at Home
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Marge

Bart arrives home during school hours. Marge is suspicious.

**Marge:** "Bart! Why aren't you in school?"

**Bart:** "I got expelled. Skinner kicked me out so Superintendent Chalmers wouldn't see me."

**Marge:** "That's outrageous! But... maybe we can use this time productively."

Marge decides to homeschool Bart.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Marge's Homeschooling
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Marge

Marge attempts to homeschool Bart. She takes a creative approach, teaching him through things he enjoys.

**Marge:** "Let's try learning through your interests. What do you like?"

**Bart:** "Skateboarding. Pranks. Rock music."

Marge uses unconventional methods. Bart actually starts learning and becomes interested in history.

#### Scene 6: Bart Discovers the Truth
**Location:** Springfield Historical Society / Old Records
**Characters:** Bart

While studying history for Marge, Bart stumbles upon old documents about Whacking Day's true origin.

**Bart:** "Wait a minute... Jebediah Springfield invented Whacking Day as an excuse to beat up Irish immigrants?!"

He finds proof that the "tradition" is only about 100 years old and was created for bigoted purposes, not ancient heritage.

**Bart:** "Lisa's gonna love this!"

#### Scene 7: Lisa's Campaign
**Location:** Various Springfield Locations
**Characters:** Lisa, Homer, Marge, Springfield Citizens

Lisa tries to convince the town that Whacking Day is cruel. She protests, makes signs, and argues with citizens.

**Lisa:** "These snakes are innocent creatures! This is barbaric!"

**Moe:** "Aw, they're just snakes!"

**Homer:** "Lisa, it's tradition. You can't fight tradition."

No one listens. Lisa is frustrated.

#### Scene 8: Whacking Day Preparations
**Location:** Town Square / Various
**Characters:** Mayor Quimby, Citizens, Homer

The town prepares for Whacking Day with festive enthusiasm. Clubs are distributed. The town square is decorated.

**Mayor Quimby:** "Tomorrow, we whack like our forefathers whacked!"

Homer practices his whacking technique.

**Homer:** "I'm gonna whack so many snakes this year!"

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: Bart Tells Lisa
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Lisa's Room
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa

Bart shares his discovery with Lisa.

**Bart:** "Lisa! I found out where Whacking Day really came from!"

**Lisa:** "What do you mean?"

**Bart:** "Jebediah Springfield made it up to beat Irish people! It's not even a real tradition - it's based on bigotry!"

**Lisa:** "This is exactly what I need! But will anyone care?"

They realize the town might not listen even with historical evidence.

#### Scene 10: Barry White Arrives
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Barry White

Lisa had written a letter to singer Barry White, an animal lover. To her amazement, he shows up at her door.

**Barry White:** "I got your letter, little girl. I'm here to help save those snakes."

**Lisa:** "Barry White?! You came!"

**Barry White:** "I love all creatures. And I have an idea."

Barry reveals that snakes are attracted to low bass frequencies - like his voice.

#### Scene 11: Whacking Day Begins
**Location:** Springfield Town Square
**Characters:** All Citizens, Mayor Quimby, Reverend Lovejoy

Whacking Day begins. Snakes are driven into the town square. Citizens raise their clubs.

**Reverend Lovejoy:** "Dear Lord, bless these clubs, that they may whack righteously."

**Mayor Quimby:** "Let the whacking... BEGIN!"

The crowd surges forward.

#### Scene 12: Barry White Saves the Day
**Location:** Town Square / Simpson Backyard
**Characters:** Lisa, Barry White, Homer, Citizens, Snakes

Just as the whacking is about to start, Barry White begins singing in his deep bass voice through loudspeakers Lisa set up.

**Barry White:** (singing) "Oh yeah... come to me, my serpentine friends..."

The snakes are mesmerized by his voice. They slither away from the town square, following the sound to the Simpson backyard where Lisa has prepared a safe habitat.

**Homer:** "What theâ€”?! The snakes are leaving!"

**Citizen:** "They're going toward that music!"

The townspeople are confused and angry, but the snakes escape.

#### Scene 13: The Truth Revealed
**Location:** Town Square
**Characters:** Lisa, Bart, Citizens, Mayor Quimby

Lisa and Bart reveal the truth about Whacking Day's origins.

**Lisa:** "Citizens of Springfield! Whacking Day is a fraud!"

**Bart:** "Jebediah Springfield invented it to beat up Irish immigrants! It's not a proud tradition - it's based on hatred!"

The crowd murmurs. Some are shocked, others defensive.

**Irish Springfield Resident:** "Aye, me grandfather told stories about that..."

Mayor Quimby realizes the political liability.

**Quimby:** "Er, uh, in light of this new information... Whacking Day is hereby cancelled. Forever."

#### Scene 14: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Backyard / Home
**Characters:** Simpson Family, Barry White, Snakes

The snakes are safe in the Simpson backyard. Barry White prepares to leave.

**Barry White:** "You did good, Lisa. Those snakes will be relocated to a wildlife preserve."

**Lisa:** "Thank you, Mr. White. I couldn't have done it without you."

**Homer:** "I guess whacking snakes was pretty dumb."

**Marge:** "I'm proud of both of you. Bart, you actually learned something from homeschooling!"

**Bart:** "Yeah, but don't tell anyone. I have a reputation."

The family laughs as Barry White drives off into the sunset.

## Cultural References

- **Barry White:** The legendary soul singer known for his deep bass voice
- **St. Patrick's Day:** Whacking Day parodies the legend of St. Patrick driving snakes from Ireland
- **Animal Rights Movement:** Lisa's activism reflects real-world animal welfare concerns
- **Anti-Irish Discrimination:** References historical prejudice against Irish immigrants in America
- **Homeschooling:** Growing educational alternative in the 1990s

## Episode Themes

4. **Alternative Education:** Bart thrives with Marge's creative homeschooling
5. **Speaking Truth to Power:** Lisa's courage to challenge the entire town
6. **Unexpected Alliances:** Barry White helping Lisa
