## Synopsis

The Springfield Nuclear Power Plant discovers that Homer's years of radiation exposure have left him sterile. To avoid a lawsuit, Mr. Burns bribes Homer with $2,000 and the "First Annual Montgomery Burns Award for Outstanding Achievement in the Field of Excellence." Meanwhile, Homer's half-brother Herb Powell - who was ruined after Homer's car design destroyed his company - reappears, homeless and destitute. Herb comes to the Simpson home hoping to borrow money to fund his latest invention. Despite Marge's hesitations and Herb's previous declaration that Homer was "no brother" of his, the family agrees to lend Herb Homer's $2,000. Herb uses the money to perfect his invention: a baby translator that interprets infant babbling into adult speech. Testing it on Maggie, the device works perfectly. The baby translator becomes a massive commercial success, and Herb becomes wealthy again. He forgives Homer, buys the family gifts, and the brothers are reconciled at last.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - The Discovery
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Medical Office
**Characters:** Homer, Plant Doctor, Mr. Burns, Smithers

During a routine medical exam, the plant doctor makes a disturbing discovery.

**Doctor:** "Mr. Simpson, your sperm count is extremely low. Years of radiation exposure have rendered you sterile."

**Homer:** (confused) "Sterile? Does that mean I can't have any more kids?"

**Doctor:** "That's correct."

**Homer:** "Woohoo!"

Homer is thrilled, but Burns is terrified of a lawsuit.

#### Scene 2: Burns' Office - Damage Control
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Burns' Office
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns and Smithers discuss how to handle the situation.

**Burns:** "If Simpson sues, we're ruined! We need to pay him off without admitting liability."

**Smithers:** "Perhaps we could give him some kind of award, sir? Make him feel special?"

**Burns:** "Excellent idea, Smithers! We'll create an award so impressive he won't think to sue!"

They devise the "First Annual Montgomery Burns Award for Outstanding Achievement in the Field of Excellence."

#### Scene 3: Award Ceremony - The Bribe
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Auditorium
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Mr. Burns, Smithers, Plant Employees

An elaborate award ceremony is held. Homer is the sole recipient of the prestigious-sounding but meaningless award.

**Burns:** "And the winner of the First Annual Montgomery Burns Award for Outstanding Achievement in the Field of Excellence is... Homer Simpson!"

Homer is overwhelmed with pride. The award comes with a $2,000 prize.

**Homer:** "Two thousand dollars! I'm finally being recognized for my excellence!"

**Marge:** (suspicious) "Homer, don't you think this is a little strange?"

**Homer:** "Strange? Marge, they're honoring me! With money!"

#### Scene 4: Simpson Home - Dreams of Spending
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

Homer fantasizes about what to buy with his $2,000. He has his heart set on a vibrating massage chair.

**Homer:** "The Spinemelter 2000! It massages your back while you watch TV!"

**Marge:** "Homer, shouldn't we save this money? Or pay bills?"

**Homer:** "Marge, I earned this with my excellence. I deserve that chair!"

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: The Return of Herb
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Front Door
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Herb

The doorbell rings. Homer opens it to find his half-brother Herb - but not the wealthy tycoon they remember. Herb is dirty, disheveled, and clearly homeless.

**Herb:** "Homer."

**Homer:** "Herb! You look... different."

**Herb:** (bitter) "I've been living on the streets, Homer. Ever since you destroyed my car company."

**Homer:** "Oh... that."

The family is uncomfortable. Herb enters, and tensions are high.

#### Scene 6: Living Room - Herb's Pitch
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Herb

Herb explains why he's come. He has an idea for an invention but needs capital.

**Herb:** "I've been working on something big. Something that could make me rich again. But I need money to develop it."

**Marge:** "Herb, the last time Homer helped with your businessâ€”"

**Herb:** "I know. I know. But this is different. I just need a loan. I'll pay you back tenfold."

**Homer:** "How much do you need?"

**Herb:** "Two thousand dollars."

Homer's Spinemelter 2000 dreams flash before his eyes.

#### Scene 7: Family Discussion - The Decision
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

The family debates whether to give Herb the money.

**Marge:** "Homer, he said you ruined his life. He said he had no brother."

**Homer:** "But he IS my brother. Maybe this is my chance to make things right."

**Lisa:** "Dad, that's surprisingly mature of you."

**Bart:** "What's this invention anyway?"

**Homer:** "I don't know. But I trust him. He's family."

Homer decides to give Herb the money, sacrificing his dream massage chair.

#### Scene 8: Giving Herb the Money
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Herb

Homer hands Herb the $2,000.

**Homer:** "Here. Take it. I want you to have it."

**Herb:** (emotional) "Homer, I... thank you. I won't let you down this time."

**Homer:** "I know you won't."

For the first time, there's warmth between the brothers again.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: Herb's Workshop - The Invention
**Location:** Simpson Garage (converted to workshop)
**Characters:** Herb, Maggie

Herb sets up a workshop in the Simpson garage. He works day and night on his mysterious invention.

**Herb:** (talking to himself) "If I can just calibrate the frequency modulator..."

Maggie toddles in, babbling. Herb watches her curiously.

**Herb:** "That's it! Babies! Everyone wants to know what babies are saying!"

He begins building a device that will translate baby talk into adult speech.

#### Scene 10: Testing the Baby Translator
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Herb

Herb unveils his invention: the Baby Translator. It's a small device that analyzes baby sounds and speaks the translation.

**Herb:** "I call it... the Baby Translator!"

**Lisa:** "Does it actually work?"

**Herb:** "Let's find out. Maggie, say something."

Maggie babbles. The device processes her sounds.

**Baby Translator:** "I have soiled myself. How embarrassing."

Everyone gasps. It works!

**Maggie:** (babbling)

**Baby Translator:** "I want my red ball."

**Marge:** (retrieving the ball) "Here you go, sweetie!"

Maggie smiles. The family is amazed.

#### Scene 11: Commercial Success
**Location:** Various - Montage
**Characters:** Herb, Business People, Customers

A montage shows the Baby Translator becoming a massive commercial success:
- Herb pitching to investors
- Manufacturing lines producing the devices
- Parents buying them in stores
- News coverage of the "revolutionary invention"
- Money pouring in

**TV Announcer:** "The Baby Translator - finally understand what your baby is really thinking!"

Herb becomes a wealthy man again.

#### Scene 12: Herb's Gratitude - Buying Gifts
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Herb

Herb returns to the Simpson home, now in expensive clothes and driving a luxury car.

**Herb:** "Family! I owe you everything!"

He presents gifts to each family member:
- For Bart: "I'll have my goons take care of anyone who bothers you."
- For Lisa: A check for her college education
- For Marge: A new washer and dryer set
- For Maggie: A hug and thanks for being the test subject

**Homer:** "What about me?"

**Herb:** "Homer, you get the best gift of all."

He presents Homer with... the Spinemelter 2000.

**Homer:** "Oh, Herb!"

**Herb:** "You gave up your dream chair to help me. Now you have it."

They embrace.

#### Scene 13: Reconciliation
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Herb, Family

Herb and Homer have a heart-to-heart.

**Homer:** "Herb, I'm sorry I ruined your car company."

**Herb:** "And I'm sorry I blamed you for everything. You believed in me when no one else did."

**Homer:** "You're my brother."

**Herb:** "And you're mine. For real this time."

They hug. The family watches, moved.

**Herb:** "I have to go now. I've got a business to run. But I'll be back."

**Homer:** "You'd better."

Herb drives off into the sunset, wealthy and happy. Homer sits in his Spinemelter 2000, vibrating contentedly.

**Homer:** "Ahhhhh... this is the life."

**Marge:** "I'm proud of you, Homer. You did a good thing."

**Homer:** "I know. And I got a chair out of it!"

The family laughs as the episode ends.

## Cultural References

- **Title Reference:** Parody of "Brother, Can You Spare a Dime?", the Depression-era song (1931)
- **Baby Translator Concept:** Satire of the 1990s tech boom and gadget culture
- **Corporate Cover-ups:** Satirizes how companies handle liability issues
- **Self-Help Gadgets:** The Spinemelter 2000 parodies as-seen-on-TV products

---

## Episode Themes

1. **Redemption:** Homer gets a chance to make up for destroying Herb's company
2. **Family Forgiveness:** Herb ultimately forgives Homer despite his anger
3. **Faith and Trust:** Homer believes in Herb when logic says he shouldn't
4. **Sacrifice:** Homer gives up his dream chair to help his brother
5. **Second Chances:** Both brothers get another opportunity to be family
6. **Corporate Greed:** Burns' cover-up of the radiation exposure
