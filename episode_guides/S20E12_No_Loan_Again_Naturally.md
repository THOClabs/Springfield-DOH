## Synopsis

After throwing an extravagant Mardi Gras party funded by their home equity line, the Simpsons discover their adjustable-rate mortgage payment has skyrocketed. Unable to afford the increased payments, they lose their house at auction. Ned Flanders compassionately outbids Mr. Burns and purchases the home, then rents it back to the Simpsons at an affordable rate. However, the family exploits Ned's landlord responsibilities with excessive repair requests. When Homer publicly slanders Ned as a "slumlord," Ned evicts them. After seeing a photo of the family's happiness, Ned reconsiders and welcomes them back home.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Mardi Gras Party Planning
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

The Simpsons plan to throw an extravagant Mardi Gras party. Marge asks Homer to invite Ned Flanders, which he reluctantly agrees to do.

#### Scene 2: The Party
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Simpson family, most of Springfield

The Simpsons throw a massive Mardi Gras party, inviting practically the entire city. The celebration is lavish and expensive.

#### Scene 3: Homer's Financial Confession
**Location:** Party / Home
**Characters:** Homer, guests

Homer gleefully confesses that he's been borrowing from their home equity line of credit to pay for the party. He calls their home a "sucker" for getting stuck with the bill, showing no concern for the consequences.

#### Scene 4: The Mortgage Crisis
**Location:** Gil Gunderson's Office
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Gil Gunderson

After receiving a concerning letter, Homer and Marge visit their mortgage broker, Gil Gunderson. They learn their adjustable-rate mortgage payment has sharply increased due to Homer's irresponsible borrowing. They can no longer afford their home.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Home Auction
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace (Exterior)
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Ned Flanders, Mr. Burns, auctioneer

The Simpson home goes to auction. The family watches in sorrow as their home is bid upon. Mr. Burns participates in the bidding, but Ned Flanders outbids him, purchasing the home for $101,000.

#### Scene 6: Ned's Offer
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Ned Flanders, Simpson family

Rather than evicting the Simpsons, Ned compassionately offers to let them rent the property from him at an affordable rate. The family is overjoyed and grateful.

#### Scene 7: Move-In Celebration
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Simpson family, Ned

The Simpsons celebrate their ability to stay in their home. A photograph is taken capturing their genuine happiness. Ned is pleased to have helped his neighbors.

#### Scene 8: Landlord Responsibilities
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Ned

As the new landlord, Ned offers to fix things around the house - repairs are now his responsibility. However, the Simpsons begin making constant requests for repairs.

#### Scene 9: Excessive Demands
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Homer, Ned

Homer and the family exploit Ned's landlord status by making endless, unreasonable repair requests. They call him at all hours with trivial issues. Ned grows increasingly tired of their abuse of his generosity.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: Ned Refuses
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / Flanders Home
**Characters:** Homer, Ned

When Ned finally refuses a late-night request, Homer becomes angry. Instead of accepting that he's been unreasonable, Homer decides to retaliate against Ned.

#### Scene 11: Media Slander
**Location:** Springfield media outlets
**Characters:** Homer, reporters, Springfield residents

Homer goes to the media and publicly slanders Ned as a "dirty slumlord." The story is broadcast throughout Springfield, damaging Ned's reputation.

#### Scene 12: Confrontation and Eviction
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Ned

Ned confronts Homer about the slander. When Homer refuses to apologize for his ingratitude, Ned tells them they must leave by the end of the month. He evicts them.

#### Scene 13: The Grampa Gambit
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Grampa Simpson, Ned

The Simpsons bring Grampa to live with them, believing that having someone over 65 in the household prevents eviction. However, Ned outsmarts them by inviting Grampa to live with him instead.

#### Scene 14: Homeless
**Location:** Springfield Homeless Shelter
**Characters:** Simpson family, shelter residents

The family spends the night in the town's homeless shelter. They experience what it's like to be without a home, surrounded by other unfortunate residents.

---

### ACT FOUR

#### Scene 15: Tenant Interviews
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Ned, potential tenants

Ned interviews potential tenants to rent the Simpson home. He meets with various candidates who want to move in.

#### Scene 16: The Photograph
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Ned

While preparing the house for new tenants, Ned discovers the photograph from the Simpsons' move-in celebration. Seeing the family's genuine happiness reminds him of why he helped them in the first place.

#### Scene 17: Change of Heart
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Ned, potential tenants

Ned realizes he should allow the Simpsons to return. He sends away the potential tenants, deciding not to rent to anyone else.

#### Scene 18: Homecoming
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Simpson family, Ned, neighbors

The Simpsons return to their home. Ned welcomes them back, despite their previous behavior. The neighborhood expresses dismay at their return, but the family is home again.

## Trivia

- The title references both Gilbert O'Sullivan's 1972 hit song and a previous Simpsons episode about Ned
- The 2008 financial crisis made the episode's premise very timely
- Ned buying the Simpson home at $101,000 is remarkably low for any property
- The "over-65" eviction protection gambit with Grampa is a real legal concept in some jurisdictions
- Ned's forgiveness despite Homer's slander shows his Christian character
- The episode explores landlord-tenant relationships in a comedic context
