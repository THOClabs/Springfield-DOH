## Synopsis

When Springfield's economy tanks, the town legalizes gambling and Mr. Burns opens an extravagant casino. The episode follows multiple storylines: Homer becomes an incompetent blackjack dealer, Marge develops a serious gambling addiction, Bart opens a rival casino in his treehouse, and Burns transforms into a Howard Hughes-like germophobic recluse. Lisa prepares for a state costume pageant while her mother spirals into compulsive gambling.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Springfield's Economic Crisis
**Location:** Town Hall / Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Mayor Quimby, Principal Skinner, townspeople

Springfield's economy is in serious trouble. Businesses are failing, unemployment is rising.

At a town meeting, Principal Skinner proposes a radical solution: legalize gambling.

The town votes in favor.

#### Scene 2: Mr. Burns' Casino Opens
**Location:** Springfield Waterfront
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Homer, townspeople

Mr. Burns builds a massive, extravagant casino on the waterfront: "Mr. Burns' Casino."

The grand opening is a spectacular event. Retired boxer Gerry Cooney greets guests at the door.

Homer gets a job as a blackjack dealer.

#### Scene 3: Lisa's Pageant Preparation
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge

Lisa is preparing for the 50 States costume pageant at school. She needs to choose a state and create a costume.

Marge suggests Nevada or Florida as options.

Lisa will need Marge's help with the costume.

#### Scene 4: Homer the Dealer
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Casino - Blackjack tables
**Characters:** Homer, casino patrons

Homer works as a blackjack dealer. He is spectacularly incompetent.

His dealing is so poor that players consistently win against the house. Homer doesn't understand the rules properly.

Burns hasn't noticed yet because he's distracted by bigger concerns.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Marge Discovers Slots
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Casino - Slot machines
**Characters:** Marge, casino patrons

While visiting Homer at work, Marge tries a slot machine. She wins.

The thrill of winning triggers something in Marge. She tries again. And again.

Marge begins spending more and more time at the slots.

#### Scene 6: Burns' Obsession Begins
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Office / Casino
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Mr. Burns becomes obsessed with the casino's success. He monitors every detail.

He stops sleeping. He becomes increasingly paranoid about germs and contamination.

Burns starts to resemble Howard Hughes in his later years.

#### Scene 7: Bart Gets Ejected
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Casino
**Characters:** Bart, security guards

Bart tries to gamble at the casino but is caught and ejected for being underage.

This gives him an idea.

#### Scene 8: Bart's Treehouse Casino
**Location:** Simpson Backyard - Treehouse
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse, Nelson, kids

Bart opens his own casino in the treehouse. It's a kids-only gambling establishment.

He runs card games and other gambling activities for neighborhood children.

Robert Goulet performs as entertainment (he's been reduced to playing children's parties).

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: Marge's Addiction Worsens
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Casino / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Lisa

Marge's gambling addiction spirals out of control. She spends all day at the slot machines.

She neglects her family. Lisa's pageant costume goes unmade.

Homer tries to get through to Marge but she can't stop.

#### Scene 10: Burns Becomes Howard Hughes
**Location:** Burns' Casino Penthouse
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns has completely transformed. He hasn't slept since the casino opened.

He's become germophobic, wearing tissue boxes on his feet. He grows long fingernails. He becomes paranoid about everyone.

In one scene, Burns pulls a gun on Smithers out of paranoia.

This is a direct parody of Howard Hughes' famous decline.

#### Scene 11: Lisa's Pageant Crisis
**Location:** Simpson Home / School
**Characters:** Lisa, Homer

Lisa's pageant is approaching and she has no costume. Marge is at the casino.

Lisa is devastated. She's been forgotten by her gambling-addicted mother.

Homer realizes the severity of the problem.

#### Scene 12: Homer Confronts Marge
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Casino
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer goes to the casino to confront Marge about her addiction.

He delivers a memorable line about perspective - referencing his own past mistakes while pointing out that Marge has a gambling problem.

Marge finally sees what she's become. She breaks down.

#### Scene 13: Resolution
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** All main characters

Marge acknowledges her addiction and stops gambling.

Lisa improvises a last-minute costume (Florida, made from materials at hand) and performs at the pageant.

Burns' Howard Hughes phase presumably ends (though it's left somewhat ambiguous).

Bart's treehouse casino is shut down.

The family is reunited, having learned a lesson about the dangers of gambling addiction.

## Howard Hughes Parody

Burns' transformation into Howard Hughes includes:

| Hughes Trait | Burns Version |
|--------------|---------------|
| Germophobia | Tissue boxes on feet |
| Long fingernails | Unkempt appearance |
| Insomnia | Hasn't slept since casino opened |
| Paranoia | Pulls gun on Smithers |
| Reclusive behavior | Locks himself in penthouse |
| Business obsession | Monitors every casino detail |

This is considered one of the best Burns parodies in the series.
