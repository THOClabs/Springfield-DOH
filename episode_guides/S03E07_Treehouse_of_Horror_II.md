## Synopsis

The second annual Treehouse of Horror Halloween special presents three nightmare segments, each dreamed by a different Simpson family member after eating too much Halloween candy. Lisa dreams of a cursed monkey's paw that grants wishes with terrible consequences. Bart dreams of having omnipotent powers that terrorize Springfield. Homer dreams of Mr. Burns transplanting his brain into a robot body. This is the only Treehouse of Horror episode where the segment titles are not announced within the episode.

---

## Episode Structure

Unlike regular episodes, Treehouse of Horror II is an anthology with three separate non-canon segments, framed by the family eating Halloween candy and having nightmares.

## SEGMENT ONE: Lisa's Nightmare - "The Monkey's Paw"

### Characters
- **Homer Simpson** - Purchases the cursed paw; makes final wish
- **Marge Simpson** - Warns about the paw's danger
- **Bart Simpson** - Wishes for wealth and fame
- **Lisa Simpson** - Wishes for world peace
- **Maggie Simpson** - Makes first wish (pacifier)
- **Ned Flanders** - Receives the paw; benefits from it
- **Kang and Kodos** - Alien invaders who conquer Earth
- **Moroccan Shopkeeper** - Warns Homer about the paw

---

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: The Moroccan Market
**Location:** Morocco - Street Market
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Shopkeeper

The Simpson family is vacationing in Morocco. Homer browses a mysterious shop and discovers a mummified monkey's paw.

The shopkeeper warns Homer that the paw grants four wishes but brings "grave misfortune" to its owner.

Homer dismisses the warnings and purchases the paw anyway.

#### Scene 2: First Wish - Maggie's Pacifier
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Back home, the family examines the paw. Maggie reaches for it and inadvertently makes the first wish.

A new pacifier appears - seemingly harmless.

The family realizes the paw actually works.

#### Scene 3: Second Wish - Wealth and Fame
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Springfield residents

Bart wishes for the Simpson family to be rich and famous.

The wish is granted. The Simpsons become celebrities with enormous wealth.

However, the public quickly tires of the family's constant media presence and ubiquity. They become overexposed and hated.

The Simpsons are miserable despite their riches.

#### Scene 4: Third Wish - World Peace
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / Earth / Space
**Characters:** Lisa, Homer, Marge, Bart, Kang, Kodos

Lisa, trying to use the paw for good, wishes for world peace.

All weapons on Earth disappear. Nations disarm. Humanity lives in harmony.

But in space, the aliens Kang and Kodos observe that Earth is now completely defenseless.

They launch an invasion and easily conquer the planet, enslaving humanity with primitive weapons (boards with nails in them).

#### Scene 5: Fourth Wish - The Turkey Sandwich
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer

Frustrated that all wishes have backfired, Homer decides to make a foolproof final wish.

Homer wishes for a turkey sandwich - specifically requesting no "weird surprises."

The sandwich appears. Homer takes a bite.

The turkey is "a little dry."

Homer is enraged. Even his simple wish had a flaw.

#### Scene 6: Flanders Gets the Paw
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / Flanders Home
**Characters:** Homer, Ned Flanders

Homer throws the cursed paw away. Ned Flanders finds it.

Flanders wishes for the aliens to be gone. The aliens leave, noting that eventually humans will build bigger and bigger boards with nails until they destroy themselves.

Flanders becomes a hero. His wishes seem to work perfectly.

Homer is furious that Flanders succeeds where he failed.

## SEGMENT TWO: Bart's Nightmare - "The Bart Zone"

### Characters
- **Bart Simpson** - Has godlike omnipotent powers
- **Homer Simpson** - Turned into a jack-in-the-box
- **Marge Simpson** - Tries to keep peace
- **Lisa Simpson** - Terrified of Bart

- **Springfield residents** - Live in fear of Bart
- **Mrs. Krabappel** - Bart's teacher
- **Otto Mann** - School bus driver

---

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: Bart's Powers Revealed
**Location:** Springfield / Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, classmates, teachers, townspeople

Bart Simpson has terrifying powers: he can read minds and alter reality at will.

Everyone in Springfield must think happy thoughts around Bart, or face terrible consequences.

The town lives in constant fear.

#### Scene 2: "Bonerland"
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, Mrs. Krabappel, students

Bart has used his powers to rewrite American history. The country is now called "Bonerland."

Teachers and adults must go along with whatever Bart says.

#### Scene 3: Moe's Punishment
**Location:** Moe's Tavern (via phone)
**Characters:** Bart, Moe

Bart makes a prank call to Moe. When Moe expresses anger, Bart punishes him with his powers.

Moe is forced to say humiliating things about himself.

#### Scene 4: Homer Becomes a Jack-in-the-Box
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge

Homer, fed up with living in fear, attempts to attack Bart with a chair.

Bart catches him in the act - he can read minds, after all.

As punishment, Bart transforms Homer into a jack-in-the-box.

#### Scene 5: Dr. Marvin Monroe's Advice
**Location:** Dr. Marvin Monroe's Office
**Characters:** Bart, Homer (as jack-in-the-box), Dr. Marvin Monroe

Marge takes Bart and the jack-in-the-box Homer to see Dr. Marvin Monroe.

Monroe, terrified of Bart's powers, carefully suggests that Bart and Homer need to spend quality time together.

If they bond, perhaps Bart will restore Homer.

Monroe's office is part of the "Bonerland Medical Association."

#### Scene 6: Father-Son Bonding
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Bart, Homer (jack-in-the-box / restored)

Bart and the jack-in-the-box Homer spend time together - playing catch, going to the park.

Their bond strengthens. Bart's heart softens.

Eventually, Bart restores Homer to his normal form.

Homer and Bart hug. They've learned to appreciate each other.

## SEGMENT THREE: Homer's Nightmare - "If I Only Had a Brain"

### Characters
- **Homer Simpson** - Brain transplanted into robot; later shares body with Burns
- **Mr. Burns** - Mad scientist who creates the robot
- **Waylon Smithers** - Burns's assistant in the experiment
- **Marge Simpson** - Homer's wife
- **Robot** - Frankenstein-like creation with Homer's brain

---

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

#### Scene 1: Homer Fired
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Mr. Burns fires Homer for incompetence and laziness.

Homer is devastated. He can't find other work.

#### Scene 2: The Graveyard
**Location:** Springfield Cemetery
**Characters:** Homer

Desperate for money, Homer takes a job as a gravedigger.

One night, exhausted from work, Homer falls asleep in an open grave.

#### Scene 3: Burns's Laboratory
**Location:** Burns's Secret Laboratory
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers

Burns is building a giant robot laborer to replace all human workers at the plant.

The robot is nearly complete, but it needs a human brain to function.

Burns and Smithers go to the cemetery to find a fresh corpse.

**Visual Note:** The animators created impressive concave/convex imagery of Burns and Smithers descending to the lab - a deliberate tour-de-force moment.

#### Scene 4: Brain Extraction
**Location:** Springfield Cemetery / Burns's Laboratory
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Homer

Burns and Smithers find Homer asleep in the open grave. They mistake him for a corpse.

Burns extracts Homer's brain while humming "If I Only Had a Brain" from The Wizard of Oz.

They transport the brain to the laboratory and implant it in the robot.

#### Scene 5: The Robot Awakens
**Location:** Burns's Laboratory / Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Robot (Homer's brain)

The giant Frankenstein-like robot awakens with Homer's brain inside.

Burns is excited - his perfect worker is alive!

But the robot immediately proves to be lazy, clumsy, and only interested in eating doughnuts.

It has all of Homer's personality flaws.

Burns is deeply disappointed. His creation is useless.

#### Scene 6: Burns Crushed
**Location:** Burns's Laboratory
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Robot

Burns decides to remove the brain and return it to Homer's body.

During the procedure, the heavy robot topples over and crushes Mr. Burns.

Burns's body is destroyed. Only his head survives.

#### Scene 7: Two Heads
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns (head), Marge

The only way to save Burns is to attach his head to a living body.

Homer wakes up to find Mr. Burns's head sewn onto his body.

Homer now shares his body with his hated boss.

Burns delivers the closing line: "Oh, that's right! It's all a dream! Or is it?"

## Production Notes

- Written by six writers: Al Jean, Mike Reiss, Jeff Martin, George Meyer, Sam Simon, and John Swartzwelder
- Directed by Jim Reardon
- First Treehouse of Horror to establish the "scary names" credit gag
- After this episode, it was decided Kang and Kodos would appear in every future Halloween special
- The laboratory descent scene was a deliberate animation tour-de-force with concave/convex imagery
- Nominated for two Primetime Emmy Awards:
  - Outstanding Individual Achievement in Sound Mixing
  - Outstanding Music Composition (Alf Clausen)
- Aired on Halloween night (October 31, 1991)

## Themes

- **Be careful what you wish for** - Segment 1's monkey paw grants wishes with terrible consequences
- **Power corrupts** - Segment 2 shows Bart's absolute power terrorizing everyone
- **Father-son relationships** - Segments 2 and 3 both explore Homer's relationship with others
- **The perils of science** - Segment 3 parodies Frankenstein's hubris
- **Class commentary** - Burns's desire to replace workers with robots

## Character Development

**Homer Simpson:**
- Segment 1: Shows his inability to think through consequences
- Segment 2: Learns to bond with Bart despite differences
- Segment 3: His laziness literally transfers to a robot

**Bart Simpson:**
- Segment 2 explores what would happen if Bart had unlimited power
- Despite his cruelty, he's capable of love and can be reached through bonding

**Lisa Simpson:**
- Her well-intentioned wish for world peace causes disaster
- Shows that even good intentions can have unintended consequences

**Mr. Burns:**
- Segment 3 portrays him as a mad scientist willing to use humans as spare parts
- His desire to eliminate workers backfires spectacularly

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. The "scary names" credit tradition for Treehouse of Horror episodes
2. Kang and Kodos as recurring Halloween special characters
3. The anthology format with three distinct segments
4. Higher production values for Halloween specials (laboratory animation)
5. Template for cultural parodies in future Halloween episodes
6. Non-canon status allowing for character deaths and transformations

---

## Trivia

- Only Treehouse of Horror where segment titles aren't announced on screen
- The decision to include Kang and Kodos in all future Halloween specials was made after this episode
- Alf Clausen's score was Emmy-nominated
- The "Bonerland" joke in Segment 2 was considered edgy for 1991
- Burns humming "If I Only Had a Brain" while removing Homer's brain is darkly ironic
- The laboratory animation with concave/convex imagery was time-consuming but considered worth the effort
- This episode helped establish Treehouse of Horror as an annual tradition fans anticipated
- IGN later ranked the third segment as the eighth best Treehouse of Horror segment ever
