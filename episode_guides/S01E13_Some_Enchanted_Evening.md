## Synopsis

After Marge calls a radio therapist to complain about feeling neglected by Homer, an embarrassed Homer takes Moe's advice to win her back with a romantic evening out. They hire a babysitter through a service, but the children soon discover that Ms. Botz is actually the "Babysitter Bandit," a wanted criminal who ties up children and robs houses. Bart, Lisa, and Maggie must work together to capture her before their parents return.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Home - Morning
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

A typical chaotic morning in the Simpson household. Marge feels underappreciated as she manages the household while Homer seems oblivious to her efforts.

Homer leaves for work without showing Marge any affection or appreciation.

#### Scene 2: KBBL Radio Station
**Location:** KBBL Radio
**Characters:** Marge (caller), Dr. Marvin Monroe

Feeling neglected and depressed, Marge calls into the radio show of Dr. Marvin Monroe, a radio therapist.

Marge pours out her feelings about Homer's neglect. She describes feeling invisible and unappreciated in her marriage.

Dr. Monroe offers advice on air, telling Marge her feelings are valid.

**Notable First:** This is Dr. Marvin Monroe's first appearance.

#### Scene 3: Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, coworkers

Homer is at work when he hears Marge's call playing on the radio. His coworkers recognize her voice.

Homer is mortified and embarrassed. His wife just aired their marital problems to all of Springfield.

#### Scene 4: Moe's Tavern
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Moe, Barney

Homer goes to Moe's to drown his sorrows and figure out what to do.

Moe offers surprisingly sage romantic advice: Homer should buy Marge flowers, take her to a fancy dinner, and show her a romantic evening.

**Moe:** Tells Homer to romance his wife - flowers, dinner, the works.

Homer is inspired. He'll make it up to Marge with a special night out.

#### Scene 5: Simpson Home - Homer's Arrival
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer returns home with flowers and apologizes to Marge. He proposes a romantic evening out - just the two of them.

Marge is touched and agrees. They just need to find a babysitter.

#### Scene 6: Calling for a Babysitter
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Babysitting Service Receptionist (phone)

Homer calls the Rubber Baby Buggy Bumpers Babysitting Service. The receptionist asks his name.

Homer realizes the Simpsons have been blacklisted by babysitters due to their children's reputation. He lies and says he's "Mr. Samson."

When the receptionist mentions the terrible Simpson family who lives nearby, Homer plays along, defending the "Simpsons" as his misunderstood neighbors.

The receptionist agrees to send a babysitter to "Mr. Samson."

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 7: Ms. Botz Arrives
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Ms. Botz

The babysitter arrives: a tall, intimidating woman named Ms. Botz. She carries her own suitcases.

Marge gives instructions: Maggie should be put to bed right away, Bart and Lisa can stay up one extra hour watching "The Happy Little Elves Meet the Curious Bear Cub."

Homer and Marge leave for their romantic evening.

#### Scene 8: Chez Paree Restaurant
**Location:** Chez Paree
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Maitre D', restaurant staff

Homer takes Marge to the fancy restaurant Chez Paree. Homer picks out a live lobster for dinner.

The couple enjoys a romantic meal and even dances the mambo together.

They're reconnecting and having a wonderful time.

#### Scene 9: Simpson Home - Television Battle
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Ms. Botz

Back at home, Bart is forced to watch "The Happy Little Elves" with Lisa. He tries to negotiate for different programming.

Ms. Botz intimidates Bart into silence with a menacing look and threat. Bart is actually scared of her.

Ms. Botz goes to put Maggie to bed, leaving Bart and Lisa alone.

#### Scene 10: Channel Surfing - The Discovery
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa

Unable to stand the Happy Little Elves anymore, Bart changes the channel when Ms. Botz leaves the room.

He lands on "America's Most Armed and Dangerous," a crime show featuring wanted fugitives.

The show profiles "The Babysitter Bandit" - a criminal who poses as a babysitter, ties up children, and robs homes.

A photo of the Babysitter Bandit appears on screen: It's Ms. Botz! Her real name is Lucille Botzcowski.

Bart and Lisa are terrified.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: The Attack
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Ms. Botz

Ms. Botz walks in with rope just as the TV segment ends. She's ready to tie up the children.

Bart screams and runs to the basement. Lisa runs to the kitchen to call the police.

Ms. Botz pursues them methodically.

#### Scene 12: Lisa's Call Attempt
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Lisa, Ms. Botz

Lisa finds the phone and dials the toll-free fugitive hotline shown on TV. The line is busy!

She keeps trying desperately as Ms. Botz closes in.

#### Scene 13: Bart Captured
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Basement
**Characters:** Bart, Ms. Botz

Ms. Botz finds Bart hiding in the basement. She captures him despite his attempts to escape.

#### Scene 14: Ye Olde Off-Ramp Inn
**Location:** Ye Olde Off-Ramp Inn (Motel)
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Meanwhile, Homer and Marge have made their way to a romantic motel.

They're enjoying their private time together, completely unaware of the danger at home.

#### Scene 15: Lisa Captured
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Lisa, Ms. Botz

Lisa finally gets through on the hotline and is about to report Ms. Botz's location.

Ms. Botz catches her before she can complete the call. Lisa is captured.

#### Scene 16: Tied Up
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Ms. Botz

Ms. Botz ties up Bart and Lisa and places them on the couch. She forces them to watch "The Happy Little Elves" on loop.

Ms. Botz begins ransacking the house, stealing valuables.

The phone lines have been disabled so the children can't call for help.

#### Scene 17: Maggie's Rescue
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Maggie, Bart, Lisa

Baby Maggie, who was supposed to be asleep, crawls out of her crib.

Using her pacifier and baby skills, Maggie manages to untie Bart and Lisa.

The children are free!

#### Scene 18: Capturing the Babysitter Bandit
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Ms. Botz

The children work together to subdue Ms. Botz:
- Maggie acts as a distraction/lure
- Ms. Botz is caught off guard
- The children knock her out

They hog-tie Ms. Botz with the same rope she used on them and tape her mouth shut.

They position her in front of the TV, forcing her to watch "The Happy Little Elves."

Unable to use the house phone, the children call for help from a pay phone.

#### Scene 19: Homer and Marge Return
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Ms. Botz (tied up), reporters

Homer and Marge return home from their romantic evening.

They find Ms. Botz hog-tied in the living room, staring helplessly at the Happy Little Elves video.

Homer, thinking the kids are playing a prank, scolds them for being cruel to the nice babysitter. He unties Ms. Botz!

Ms. Botz quickly escapes.

#### Scene 20: The Aftermath
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Exterior
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, reporters

Reporters arrive at the scene. They explain to Homer that Ms. Botz was a wanted criminal - the Babysitter Bandit.

Homer realizes his terrible mistake. He let the criminal escape after his children had heroically captured her.

The news broadcast identifies Homer as a "local boob" who freed the Babysitter Bandit.

Homer is humiliated, but Marge consoles him: Any man who raised three children capable of hog-tying a dangerous criminal must be doing something right.

**Marge:** Reassures Homer that their kids are amazing.

The family is reunited, and despite Homer's blunder, the evening ends on a positive note.

## Production Notes

### Animation Crisis

This episode has a unique production history:

- **Production Code 7G01** - This was literally the FIRST episode produced for The Simpsons
- Originally intended to premiere the series in fall 1989
- Severe animation problems with the Korean animation studio
- Only about 30% of the original animation was salvageable
- Required near-total redo by a different studio
- Delayed to become the season finale instead of premiere
- "Simpsons Roasting on an Open Fire" (7G08) became the actual premiere instead

### Guest Stars

- Penny Marshall (of "Laverne & Shirley" fame) voiced Ms. Botz
- First celebrity guest voice in Simpsons history
- The name "Ms. Botz" was based on someone who actually babysat Matt Groening as a child

## Character Development

**Maggie Simpson:**
This episode establishes Maggie as surprisingly capable despite being a baby. She:
- Escapes her crib
- Unties her siblings
- Helps subdue a dangerous criminal

This pattern of Maggie's hidden competence continues throughout the series.

**Homer Simpson:**
Despite his final blunder, Homer shows growth:
- Takes advice and acts on it
- Plans a romantic evening
- Shows he cares about Marge
- Ultimately raised children who can handle themselves

## Episode Significance

As production code 7G01, this episode holds special importance:

1. First episode ever produced for The Simpsons
2. First villain (Ms. Botz) created for the series
3. First celebrity guest voice (Penny Marshall)
4. Introduces Dr. Marvin Monroe
5. Establishes Maggie's surprising capabilities
6. Shows the strength of the Simpson family unit
7. Demonstrates Homer's capacity for romantic gestures
8. Nearly killed the show due to animation problems

## Trivia

- Ms. Botz's name comes from someone who babysat Matt Groening
- Penny Marshall was initially hesitant to voice a cartoon character
- The animation crisis taught the production team valuable lessons about quality control
- This is the Season 1 finale despite being first produced
- "The Happy Little Elves" becomes a recurring fictional show
- The irony of Homer freeing the criminal became a template for future Homer blunders
- Ms. Botz would later return in future Simpsons media
