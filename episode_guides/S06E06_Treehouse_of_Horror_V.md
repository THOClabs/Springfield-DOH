## Synopsis

The fifth annual Simpsons Halloween special features three terrifying tales. In "The Shinning," the Simpson family becomes winter caretakers at Mr. Burns' isolated mountain lodge, where Homer goes insane without TV and beer. In "Time and Punishment," Homer accidentally creates a time machine from a broken toaster and repeatedly alters history with disastrous results. In "Nightmare Cafeteria," Principal Skinner solves the school's overcrowding and budget problems by serving detention students as cafeteria food. A running gag throughout all three segments features Groundskeeper Willie getting an axe in the back while trying to help.

# SEGMENT 1: The Shinning

## Segment Information

| Field | Value |

---

## Synopsis

The Simpson family is hired as winter caretakers for Mr. Burns' isolated mountain lodge. Mr. Burns cuts the TV cable and removes all beer to make Homer a more efficient worker, despite Smithers' warning that this is what caused previous caretakers to go insane and murder their families. Bart discovers he has telepathic powers ("the Shinning"), and Groundskeeper Willie tells him to use them if the family is in danger. Without TV and beer, Homer gradually descends into madness and attempts to murder his family with an axe.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: Arrival at the Lodge
**Location:** Mr. Burns' Mountain Lodge
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Mr. Burns, Smithers

The Simpson family arrives at the isolated mountain lodge.

**Mr. Burns:** Explains they'll be caretakers for the winter.

**Burns:** Mentions the previous caretakers "went a little crazy."

**Smithers:** (worried) "Actually, sir, they went completely insane and murdered their families."

Burns dismisses the concern and leaves.

### Scene 2: The Discovery
**Location:** Lodge Interior
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer discovers a horrifying truth.

**Homer:** "No TV and no beer make Homer... something something."

**Marge:** "Go crazy?"

**Homer:** "Don't mind if I do!"

Homer begins his descent into madness.

### Scene 3: Bart's Shinning
**Location:** Lodge Exterior / Willie's Shack
**Characters:** Bart, Groundskeeper Willie

Willie senses Bart's telepathic powers.

**Willie:** "You've got the Shinning!"

**Bart:** "You mean the Shining."

**Willie:** "Shh! Do you want to get sued?"

Willie explains Bart can use his power to call for help if the family is in danger.

### Scene 4: Homer's Madness
**Location:** Lodge - Various Rooms
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Marge finds Homer's typing project.

**Homer:** Has been typing obsessively.

**Marge:** Reads the pages: "No beer and no TV make Homer go crazy" - repeated thousands of times.

She realizes the danger.

### Scene 5: The Chase
**Location:** Lodge Interior
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer chases his family with an axe.

**Homer:** Breaks through a door: "Here's Johnny!"

(Finds an empty room)

**Homer:** Breaks through another door: "David Letterman!"

(Wrong room again)

**Homer:** Breaks through a third door: "I'm Mike Wallace, I'm Morley Safer, I'm Ed Bradley... All this and Andy Rooney tonight on 60 Minutes!"

The family flees through the lodge.

### Scene 6: Willie's Rescue Attempt
**Location:** Lodge Interior
**Characters:** Willie, Homer

Bart telepathically summons Willie.

**Willie:** Arrives to save the family.

**Homer:** Throws an axe into Willie's back.

**Willie:** Dies trying to help.

### Scene 7: The Frozen Chase
**Location:** Lodge Exterior - Snow
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family flees into the frozen wilderness.

Homer pursues them but begins to freeze.

**Marge:** Discovers a portable TV in the snow.

Homer is distracted by the TV, becoming frozen in place.

**TV Announcer:** "Coming up next, A Chorus Line!"

**Homer:** (frozen, watching happily) Continues to enjoy TV despite being a frozen corpse.

## Synopsis

While trying to fix a broken toaster, Homer accidentally creates a time machine and is transported to the age of dinosaurs. Remembering his father's advice to never change anything in the past, Homer tries to avoid altering history but repeatedly fails. Each time he returns to the present, he finds increasingly bizarre alternate timelines: Ned Flanders as ruler of the world, raining donuts with no humans, and his family as frog-like creatures. He eventually settles for a reality that's "close enough" to his original.

- **Patty & Selma** - Become executioners in Flanders' dystopia

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Broken Toaster
**Location:** Simpson Home - Kitchen
**Characters:** Homer

Homer attempts to fix the broken toaster.

He gets his hand stuck and tries to pry it out.

**Homer:** Accidentally activates something.

A vortex opens and pulls him back in time.

### Scene 2: Prehistoric Times - First Trip
**Location:** Age of Dinosaurs
**Characters:** Homer, Dinosaurs

Homer arrives in the prehistoric era.

**Homer:** Remembers his father's wedding night advice.

**Grampa (flashback):** "If you ever travel back in time, don't step on anything!"

Homer carefully tries not to disturb anything.

He accidentally kills a mosquito by sitting on a rock.

**Homer:** "This can't be good."

He returns to the present.

### Scene 3: Flanders' World
**Location:** Alternate Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Ned Flanders, Patty, Selma, Various Townsfolk

Homer returns to find a dystopian world.

**Ned Flanders:** Is the "Unquestioned Lord and Master of the World."

Everyone must be happy or face "Re-Neducation."

**Patty & Selma:** Serve as executioners.

Homer screams and travels back in time again.

### Scene 4: Prehistoric Times - Second Trip
**Location:** Age of Dinosaurs
**Characters:** Homer, Dinosaurs

Homer arrives back in the past.

**Homer:** "This time I won't touch anything!"

He sneezes, accidentally killing multiple dinosaurs with a domino effect.

**Homer:** "D'oh!"

He returns to the present.

### Scene 5: Various Alternate Timelines
**Location:** Multiple Alternate Springfields
**Characters:** Homer, Alternate Family Members

Homer cycles through increasingly bizarre realities:

- A world where it rains donuts (but humans don't exist)
- His family as frog-like creatures with forked tongues
- A timeline where Maggie speaks with James Earl Jones' voice

**Maggie (James Earl Jones):** "This is indeed a disturbing universe."

Homer continues trying to fix things.

### Scene 6: Willie's Second Death
**Location:** One of the Alternate Timelines
**Characters:** Homer, Groundskeeper Willie

Willie appears to help Homer navigate the timelines.

**Homer:** Accidentally throws something.

**Willie:** Gets an axe in the back again.

**Willie:** Dies for the second time in the episode.

### Scene 7: "Close Enough"
**Location:** Almost-Normal Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer finally returns to a timeline that's mostly normal.

**Homer:** "It's close enough."

One difference: everyone in the family has forked lizard tongues.

**Family:** Uses their tongues to eat breakfast.

**Homer:** "Eh, close enough."

# SEGMENT 3: Nightmare Cafeteria

| Field | Value |

## Synopsis

Springfield Elementary faces severe overcrowding and budget cuts. Principal Skinner discovers an efficient solution: serving detention students as cafeteria food. One by one, students begin disappearing, and the remaining kids realize they're being eaten. Bart, Lisa, and Milhouse try to escape but are cornered. Just as they're about to be slaughtered, Bart wakes up - it was all a dream. However, in the closing sequence, a mysterious fog turns everyone inside out.

---

- **Lisa Simpson** - Discovers the truth; trapped
- **Homer Simpson** - Appears in dream sequence/closing

- **Maggie Simpson** - Appears in closing

### Springfield Elementary Staff

### Scene 1: Overcrowding Problem
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Principal Skinner, Mrs. Krabappel, Students

The school faces severe overcrowding and budget cuts.

**Skinner:** "We're out of money and space."

Skinner and Krabappel discuss possible solutions.

### Scene 2: The "Solution"
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Cafeteria/Kitchen
**Characters:** Skinner, Lunchlady Doris

Skinner observes the detention students.

He notices Lunchlady Doris needs meat for the cafeteria.

**Skinner:** Has a terrible idea.

**Lunchlady Doris:** Begins preparing "Sloppy Jimbos."

### Scene 3: Students Start Disappearing
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Various
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Milhouse, Teachers

Students begin vanishing one by one.

**Bart:** "Has anyone seen Jimbo?"

The cafeteria serves suspiciously delicious new dishes.

**Skinner:** "Try the Sloppy Jimbos. It's a new recipe."

Lisa grows suspicious.

### Scene 4: Uter's Fate
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Uter, Skinner, Students

Uter, the German exchange student, senses danger.

**Uter:** "Don't make me run! I'm full of chocolate!"

He is caught and taken to the kitchen.

The remaining students realize the horrifying truth.

### Scene 5: Willie's Third Death
**Location:** Springfield Elementary
**Characters:** Willie, Skinner

Willie discovers what's happening.

**Willie:** "I'll save you, children!"

**Skinner:** Throws an axe into Willie's back.

**Willie:** "Ach! I'm bad at this."

### Scene 6: The Final Chase
**Location:** Springfield Elementary - Corridors
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Milhouse, Skinner, Krabappel, Lunchlady Doris

Bart, Lisa, and Milhouse try to escape.

The staff corners them over a giant blender.

**Skinner:** Prepares to push them in.

All seems lost...

### Scene 7: It Was All a Dream... Or Was It?
**Location:** Bart's Bedroom / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge, Lisa, Maggie

Bart wakes up screaming.

**Bart:** "It was just a dream!"

**Marge:** Comforts him: "There's nothing to be afraid of."

The family gathers, relieved.

---

## Closing Sequence: The Fog

**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

A strange fog rolls in through the window.

**Marge:** "What's happening?"

Everyone begins to feel strange.

**The Family:** Turns inside out.

Despite being grotesquely transformed, they break into song and dance.

**Song:** A parody of "One" from **A Chorus Line**

The inside-out family dances as the episode ends.

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. The gold standard for Treehouse of Horror episodes
2. The "Willie gets axed" running gag
3. "The Shinning" as possibly the greatest Simpsons parody ever
4. The show's willingness to go extremely dark for Halloween
5. Multi-writer format for anthology episodes

## Trivia

- The title "The Shinning" was used to avoid copyright issues with Stephen King
- James Earl Jones' cameo as Maggie is one of the most memorable guest appearances
- David Mirkin intentionally pushed for maximum violence as a response to critics
- The inside-out ending was controversial but became beloved
- "No TV and no beer" became one of the show's most quotable lines
- This episode is frequently cited as the best Halloween special in animation history
- The Scotchgard joke was an inside reference to sponsor concerns about violence

---

## Continuity Notes

- Willie's psychic abilities are only canon within this episode
- The time travel toaster was never referenced again in the main series
- The horror movie monsters (Freddy, Jason, etc.) make rare crossover appearances
- This establishes the tradition of guest voice actors in Treehouse episodes
