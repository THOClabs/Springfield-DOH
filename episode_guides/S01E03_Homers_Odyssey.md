## Synopsis

After causing an accident during Bart's school field trip to the nuclear plant, Homer is fired from his job. Unable to find work and feeling like a failure, Homer contemplates suicide. When his family nearly gets hit by a car at a dangerous intersection, Homer finds new purpose as a public safety advocate, eventually taking on the nuclear plant itself - until Mr. Burns offers him a job as Safety Inspector.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Springfield Elementary - Bus Departure
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School - Parking Lot
**Characters:** Bart, Mrs. Krabappel, Otto, Sherri, Terri, Wendell, classmates

Mrs. Krabappel's class boards the school bus for a field trip to the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant. Otto is introduced as the bus driver. Bart misbehaves on the bus and is forced to sing as punishment.

The bus passes various locations from past field trips:
- Springfield Toxic Waste Dump
- Springfield Tire Yard
- Springfield State Prison

Wendell Borton feels sick from the bus ride.

#### Scene 2: Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Tour
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Bart, Mrs. Krabappel, students, Homer, Smithers, SNPP Supervisor, Smilin' Joe Fission

The class arrives at the power plant for their tour. They watch a humorous educational film about nuclear energy featuring the animated character "Smilin' Joe Fission."

**Note:** Waylon Smithers appears for the first time visually in this episode, though he was mistakenly colored as Black due to an animation error.

#### Scene 3: Nuclear Plant - Homer's Accident
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Interior
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, SNPP Supervisor, students

While driving a motorized cart through the plant, Homer spots Bart's class on their tour. Distracted while waving at his son, Homer crashes the cart into a cooling vent pipe, causing dangerous gas to leak.

Homer's supervisor immediately fires him on the spot - in front of Bart's entire class. Bart witnesses his father's humiliation.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 4: Simpson Home - Unemployment
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer returns home unemployed and searches for a new job without success. He sinks into depression, spending his days on the couch watching TV.

The family struggles with the financial and emotional strain of Homer's unemployment.

#### Scene 5: Moe's Tavern - Drowning Sorrows
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Moe, Barney

Homer drowns his sorrows at Moe's Tavern, spending what little money the family has on beer.

**Note:** This episode debuts the running gag of Bart's prank calls to Moe.

#### Scene 6: Simpson Home - Rock Bottom
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room/Kitchen/Bart's Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart (sleeping)

Late at night, Homer watches a Duff beer commercial. Craving a beer, he searches the fridge but finds none. Desperate, he sneaks into Bart's room and steals his son's piggy bank while Bart sleeps.

In the kitchen, Homer smashes open the piggy bank only to find Bart doesn't have enough money for even one beer. Homer realizes how low he has sunk.

#### Scene 7: Simpson Home - The Suicide Note
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Kitchen
**Characters:** Homer

Overwhelmed by shame and failure, Homer writes a farewell note to his family. He plans to end his life by attaching a boulder to himself and jumping off a bridge.

Homer leaves the house with the boulder.

#### Scene 8: Simpson Home - Lisa Finds the Note
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Marge, Bart, Maggie

Lisa discovers Homer's suicide note and immediately alerts the family. They rush out to find Homer before it's too late.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: The Bridge / Dangerous Intersection
**Location:** Bridge overlooking Springfield / Nearby intersection
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The family races toward the bridge to save Homer. As they approach, they nearly get run over by a speeding truck at an unmarked intersection.

Homer, still alive, pulls his family to safety just in time.

In this moment, Homer experiences an epiphany. Rather than being filled with despair, he's suddenly inspired with a new purpose: to make Springfield safer by placing a stop sign at this dangerous intersection.

#### Scene 10: City Council Meeting
**Location:** Springfield City Hall
**Characters:** Homer, City Council Members, Springfield residents

Homer petitions the city council to install a stop sign at the dangerous intersection. His passionate plea succeeds, and the sign is installed.

Emboldened by this victory, Homer embarks on a full public safety crusade.

#### Scene 11: Safety Crusade Montage
**Location:** Various locations throughout Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Springfield residents, Chief Wiggum

Homer campaigns throughout Springfield for improved safety measures:
- Speed bumps installed on residential streets
- Warning signs placed at hazardous locations
- Safety barriers erected at dangerous spots

Chief Wiggum makes his first appearance as Homer works with local authorities.

Homer becomes a local celebrity and safety advocate, beloved by the community.

#### Scene 12: Nuclear Plant Protest
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Exterior
**Characters:** Homer, protesters, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Unsatisfied with small victories, Homer sets his sights on the biggest danger in Springfield: the nuclear power plant where he was fired.

Homer rallies citizens to protest the plant's unsafe conditions. A crowd gathers outside the facility demanding changes.

#### Scene 13: Mr. Burns' Office - The Offer
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Mr. Burns' Office
**Characters:** Homer, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Mr. Burns summons Homer to his office. Rather than fighting the protest, Burns makes Homer an offer: become the plant's new Safety Inspector with a significant raise.

Burns wants Homer to tell the crowd that the plant is actually safe. Homer hesitates, claiming this would violate his moral principles.

#### Scene 14: Nuclear Plant - Homer's Address
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant - Exterior
**Characters:** Homer, protesters, crowd

Homer steps outside to address his followers. Torn between his principles and providing for his family, Homer makes his decision.

**Homer's Speech:**
> "Friends, you have come to depend on me as your safety watchdog. So you won't scrape yourself, or stub your toes, or blow yourselves up. But you can't depend on me all your life. You have to learn that there's a little Homer Simpson in all of us..."

He pauses, then continues:

> "I've just been appointed the new Safety Inspector at this very plant, and a big fat raise!"

Homer tearfully tells his followers they must fight their battles alone from now on - and accepts the job.

## Reception

In its original broadcast, "Homer's Odyssey" finished 28th in ratings for the week of January 15-21, 1990, with a Nielsen rating of 14.9 (approximately 13.7 million households). It was the highest-rated show on Fox that week.

## Trivia

- This is considered one of the darkest early Simpsons episodes due to the suicide storyline
- The episode establishes Homer's position as Safety Inspector, which becomes his permanent job
- Blinky the three-eyed fish's cameo here sets up his larger role in Season 2's "Two Cars in Every Garage and Three Eyes on Every Fish"
- The title is a play on the ancient Greek epic "The Odyssey" and the protagonist Homer Simpson sharing the name with the legendary poet Homer

## Episode Significance

This episode is crucial to The Simpsons canon as it:
1. Establishes Homer's permanent position as Safety Inspector at the plant
2. Introduces the dynamic between Homer and Mr. Burns
3. Shows the first visual appearance of Smithers
4. Debuts Otto, Chief Wiggum, and other recurring characters
5. Sets up Blinky for future storylines
6. Establishes the running gag of Bart's prank calls to Moe
