## Synopsis

When an attractive teenage girl named Laura Powers moves in next door with her mother Ruth, Bart falls head over heels in love. He tries to impress her, but his heart is broken when he learns she's dating bully Jimbo Jones. Using his expertise in prank calls, Bart manipulates Moe into threatening Jimbo, exposing the bully's cowardice and causing Laura to break up with him. Meanwhile, Homer gets kicked out of an all-you-can-eat seafood buffet and sues the restaurant with Lionel Hutz's help.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence

**Chalkboard Gag:** "I will not bring sheep to class"
**Couch Gag:** Falling couch variation

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Winfields Leave
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace / Next door
**Characters:** Simpson family, The Winfields

The elderly Winfields, the Simpsons' longtime neighbors, are moving away. They're clearly relieved to escape life next to Homer.

A moving truck arrives with new neighbors.

#### Scene 2: Meeting Laura Powers
**Location:** Powers House (former Winfield home)
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Laura Powers, Ruth Powers

Bart is in the Powers' basement trying to scare Lisa when he's caught by the new neighbor - Laura Powers, an attractive teenage girl.

Bart is immediately smitten. Laura is cool, confident, and treats Bart kindly.

Her mother, Ruth Powers, is a single mom who has just divorced.

**First appearances:** Laura Powers, Ruth Powers

#### Scene 3: The Frying Dutchman Commercial
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Captain McAllister (on TV)

Homer sees a TV commercial for "The Frying Dutchman" - an all-you-can-eat seafood restaurant run by Captain Horatio McAllister.

The commercial promises unlimited seafood for one low price.

Homer is immediately obsessed with going.

**First appearance:** Captain McAllister (The Sea Captain)

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 4: Bart's Crush Develops
**Location:** Simpson Home / Powers Home
**Characters:** Bart, Laura

Bart finds excuses to spend time with Laura. She's friendly and treats him like a little brother/friend.

He has a fantasy sequence where they dance together in a Fred Astaire/Ginger Rogers style romantic ballroom number from "Top Hat" (1935).

#### Scene 5: Homer at The Frying Dutchman
**Location:** The Frying Dutchman restaurant
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Captain McAllister

Homer and Marge go to The Frying Dutchman. Homer takes the "all-you-can-eat" offer extremely seriously.

He eats for hours. Captain McAllister watches in horror as Homer devours endless amounts of seafood.

Eventually, past midnight, McAllister physically throws Homer out, declaring he's eaten too much.

**Captain McAllister:** "T'is no man. T'is a remorseless eating machine!"

Homer is outraged - it said ALL you can eat!

#### Scene 6: Laura Babysits
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Laura

Laura agrees to babysit Bart and Lisa. Bart is overjoyed and prepares carefully for the evening.

They have a nice time together. Bart feels like he has a chance.

#### Scene 7: Jimbo Revealed
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Laura, Jimbo Jones

Laura mentions her boyfriend. Bart assumes it's some cool guy.

Then Jimbo Jones arrives. Bart is crushed - Laura is dating one of Springfield's bullies.

Jimbo treats Bart dismissively. Laura seems attracted to Jimbo's "tough outlaw" persona.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 8: Homer Sues
**Location:** Courtroom
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Lionel Hutz, Captain McAllister, jury, judge

Homer hires Lionel Hutz to sue The Frying Dutchman for false advertising.

Hutz argues that "all-you-can-eat" means exactly that - no limits.

The jury is notably obese, sympathizing with Homer.

Marge testifies tearfully about driving Homer around all night searching for other all-you-can-eat restaurants after he was kicked out.

#### Scene 9: Bart's Revenge Plan
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Laura, Jimbo, Moe (on phone)

Laura babysits again and invites Jimbo over. Bart devises a plan to expose Jimbo.

He makes his signature prank call to Moe's Tavern, but this time gives Jimbo's real name and the Simpson address (1094 Evergreen Terrace in this episode).

Moe, believing he's finally found his tormentor, rushes over with a large rusty knife.

#### Scene 10: Jimbo's Cowardice Exposed
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Laura, Jimbo, Moe

Moe bursts in threatening "Jimbo Jones." The tough bully immediately breaks down crying and begging for his life.

**Jimbo:** (sobbing) "Please don't kill me! I'm only a kid!"

Moe, pitying the pathetic display, spares him and leaves.

Laura is disgusted. Jimbo is not the "tough outlaw" she thought. She breaks up with him on the spot.

#### Scene 11: Homer's Victory
**Location:** Courtroom / The Frying Dutchman
**Characters:** Homer, Captain McAllister

Homer wins his lawsuit. Rather than pay damages, Captain McAllister negotiates a settlement.

Homer can eat at The Frying Dutchman for free anytime - but he must be displayed in the window as "Bottomless Pete, Nature's Cruelest Mistake."

Homer happily accepts, eating while customers stare.

#### Scene 12: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Bart, Laura

Laura thanks Bart for exposing Jimbo's true nature. She kisses him on the cheek.

**Laura:** "If you were only old enough to grow a bad teenage moustache, I'd go out with you in a second."

Bart is thrilled. They end the episode by prank-calling Moe together, laughing as friends.

## Production Notes

- **Writer:** Conan O'Brien (later host of Late Night, The Tonight Show, Conan)
- **Director:** Wes Archer
- **Guest casting:** Sara Gilbert (Darlene from "Roseanne") as Laura
- **Sea Captain voice:** Hank Azaria based it on actor Robert Newton (Long John Silver)
- **Title reference:** Pun on the band New Kids on the Block
- **Address error:** Bart gives "1094 Evergreen Terrace" instead of 742
- **Fred Astaire homage:** Bart's fantasy based on "Top Hat" (1935)
- **Laura's fate:** Character never had another speaking role

## The Prank Call Resolution

This episode cleverly uses Bart's signature prank call skill to drive the plot:

- **Setup:** Moe has been tormented by prank calls for years
- **Bart's plan:** Uses Jimbo's real name and real address
- **Result:** Moe finally thinks he's found his tormentor
- **Payoff:** Jimbo's cowardly reaction destroys his tough image
- **Twist:** Laura and Bart bond over making prank calls together
