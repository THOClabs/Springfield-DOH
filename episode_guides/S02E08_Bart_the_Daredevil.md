## Synopsis

After attending a monster truck rally featuring the legendary daredevil Lance Murdock, Bart becomes inspired to pursue dangerous stunts on his skateboard. Despite a hospital visit where Dr. Hibbert shows him injured children who tried similar stunts, Bart continues his daredevil career and announces he will jump Springfield Gorge. When Homer discovers the plan, he attempts to jump the gorge himself to show Bart the danger, resulting in one of the most famous scenes in Simpsons history as Homer falls repeatedly down the gorge.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Home - Watching Wrestling
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Living Room
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Barney

Homer and Bart are watching a wrestling match on television with their friends. During the broadcast, a commercial airs for an upcoming monster truck rally featuring Truckasaurus.

Homer and Bart are immediately excited about the event.

#### Scene 2: The Schedule Conflict
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa

There's a conflict: the monster truck rally is on Saturday night, the same evening as Lisa's school band recital.

Marge tells them that Lisa's recital is earlier in the evening, so they can attend both events. The family agrees to this plan.

#### Scene 3: Lisa's Recital
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School Auditorium
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, audience

The Simpson family attends Lisa's recital. Homer is visibly impatient throughout the performance.

He constantly checks his watch and tries to mentally speed up the concert, eager to get to the monster truck rally.

When the recital finally ends, Homer grabs Lisa (letting her take a bow first) and rushes the family out the door.

#### Scene 4: The Monster Truck Rally - Arrival
**Location:** Springfield Speedway / Monster Truck Arena
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The Simpsons arrive at the monster truck rally. The arena is packed with fans.

The centerpiece attraction is Truckasaurus, a giant fire-breathing robotic dinosaur made from monster trucks.

#### Scene 5: Truckasaurus Attack
**Location:** Monster Truck Arena
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Truckasaurus Operator

In a mishap, the Simpsons accidentally drive their car into the arena at the wrong time.

Truckasaurus attacks and destroys their car, crushing and burning it in its mechanical jaws.

The rally organizers are apologetic and accommodating after the incident. They write the Simpsons a check to cover the damages to their vehicle.

#### Scene 6: Lance Murdock's Stunt
**Location:** Monster Truck Arena
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Lisa, Marge, Maggie, Lance Murdock, crowd

The grand finale features legendary daredevil Captain Lance Murdock performing a death-defying motorcycle stunt.

Murdock successfully completes the jump over a tank filled with dangerous animals (sharks, lions, etc.), but as he waves to the audience, his motorcycle falls backward into the tank.

Murdock is attacked by the animals and severely injured. Paramedics rush him to the hospital.

Despite the horrific outcome, Bart is completely inspired. Lance Murdock is now his hero.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 7: Bart's First Stunt
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - Driveway/Garage
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge

Inspired by Murdock, Bart attempts his first daredevil stunt: jumping the family car on his skateboard.

The stunt fails. Bart crashes and injures himself.

#### Scene 8: Springfield General Hospital
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge, Dr. Hibbert

The family takes Bart to the hospital. Dr. Julius Hibbert treats Bart's injuries.

**Notable First:** This is Dr. Hibbert's first appearance in the series.

To discourage Bart from further stunts, Dr. Hibbert takes him on a tour of the children's ward.

#### Scene 9: The Dare Devils Ward
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital - Children's Ward
**Characters:** Bart, Dr. Hibbert, Homer, Marge, injured children

Dr. Hibbert shows Bart the "Dare Devils Ward" - a section of the hospital filled with children who injured themselves attempting dangerous stunts inspired by TV.

**Dr. Hibbert:** "This little boy broke his leg trying to fly like Superman. This boy's brother hit him in the head with a wrench, mimicking a recent TV wrestling match."

He adds ominously: "I won't even subject you to the horrors of our Three Stooges ward."

**Marge:** "Gee, I never realized TV was such a dangerous influence."

**Dr. Hibbert:** "Well, as tragic as all this is, it's a small price to pay for countless hours of top-notch entertainment."

**Homer:** "Amen!"

The tour is meant to scare Bart straight. It doesn't work.

#### Scene 10: Bart's Stunt Career - Montage
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Bart, neighborhood kids

Undeterred by his injuries or the hospital visit, Bart continues performing increasingly dangerous skateboard stunts:
- Jumping over lined-up cars
- Jumping over animals
- Jumping over swimming pools

He builds a following among the neighborhood kids. His reputation as a daredevil grows.

#### Scene 11: Class Trip to Springfield Gorge
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa, Principal Skinner, Otto, classmates

Bart's class takes a field trip to Springfield Gorge, a massive canyon outside of town.

Looking at the vast chasm, Bart makes a fateful announcement: He will jump Springfield Gorge on his skateboard next Saturday.

His classmates are amazed and terrified. Word spreads quickly.

#### Scene 12: Lisa's Concern
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Bart

Lisa is horrified by Bart's announcement. She knows the jump would be suicidal.

She tries to talk sense into Bart, but he refuses to back down. His mind is made up.

#### Scene 13: Visiting Lance Murdock
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital - Murdock's Room
**Characters:** Lisa, Bart, Lance Murdock

Lisa takes Bart to visit Lance Murdock in the hospital, hoping the injured daredevil will convince Bart to give up his dangerous plans.

Murdock is in a full body cast, suspended from traction equipment. He's clearly in terrible shape from his injuries.

But instead of discouraging Bart, Murdock enthusiastically encourages him to pursue his daredevil dreams.

Lisa is dismayed. Her plan has backfired completely.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 14: Lisa Tells the Parents
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Lisa, Homer, Marge

With no other options, Lisa tells Homer and Marge about Bart's plan to jump Springfield Gorge.

Homer and Marge are horrified. They must stop Bart before it's too late.

#### Scene 15: Saturday at Springfield Gorge
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Bart, crowd of spectators

Saturday arrives. Bart is at the edge of Springfield Gorge with his skateboard and a homemade ramp.

A large crowd has gathered to watch the stunt.

Bart prepares to make the jump.

#### Scene 16: Homer Arrives
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, crowd

Just as Bart is about to start his run, Homer arrives.

Homer confronts Bart. He's determined to stop his son from killing himself.

Homer has an idea: He'll jump the gorge himself to show Bart what it feels like to watch a family member unnecessarily risk their life.

#### Scene 17: The Father-Son Moment
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Homer, Bart

Bart is moved by Homer's willingness to sacrifice himself. He realizes how much his father loves him.

**Bart:** "I love you, Dad."

**Homer:** "I love you too, Son."

Bart agrees to abandon the stunt and promises to stop being a daredevil.

Father and son embrace.

#### Scene 18: Homer's Accidental Jump
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, crowd

As Homer hugs Bart, his foot nudges Bart's skateboard.

The skateboard begins rolling down the ramp toward the gorge - with Homer still on it!

Homer realizes what's happening too late. He rockets off the ramp and sails over Springfield Gorge.

**Homer:** "I'm gonna make it! I'm gonna make it! This is the greatest thrill of my life! I'm king of the world!"

For a moment, it looks like Homer might actually make it across...

But he loses momentum at the last second. Homer falls short of the other side.

He plummets down into the gorge, hitting every jagged rock and outcropping on the way down.

THUD. THUD. THUD. THUD.

Homer hits the bottom.

#### Scene 19: The Rescue
**Location:** Springfield Gorge
**Characters:** Homer, paramedics, helicopter crew

A rescue helicopter arrives. Paramedics load Homer onto a stretcher and airlift him up from the bottom of the gorge.

They load him into an ambulance at the top.

But the ambulance drives forward and crashes into a tree!

The back doors fly open and Homer's stretcher slides out - sending him tumbling back down into the gorge!

He hits every rock on the way down. Again.

#### Scene 20: Springfield General Hospital
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital
**Characters:** Homer, Lance Murdock

Homer ends up in the hospital, sharing a room with Lance Murdock.

Both men are in full body casts, suspended from traction equipment.

Homer turns to Murdock and delivers the episode's final line:

**Homer:** "You think you've got guts? Try raising my kids."

## Production Notes

- Written by Jay Kogen and Wallace Wolodarsky
- Directed by Wes Archer
- Co-produced by George Meyer
- Produced by Richard Sakai and Larina Jean Adamson
- Lance Murdock was based on real-life daredevil Evel Knievel
- Dr. Hibbert was originally written as a woman named "Julia Hibbert" (named after Julia Sweeney)
- When Fox moved The Simpsons to Thursday nights to compete with The Cosby Show, the writers made Hibbert a parody of Bill Cosby's Dr. Cliff Huxtable
- Matt Groening has called this his favorite episode of the series
- The gorge fall scene is Groening's favorite moment in the entire series
- Wallace Wolodarsky also identified this as his favorite episode he wrote
- Homer's gorge fall became one of the most replayed clips in Simpsons history

## Character Development

**Bart Simpson:**
- Shows his impressionable nature and desire for fame
- Ultimately learns the value of family over glory
- His love for Homer overcomes his daredevil ambitions

**Homer Simpson:**
- Demonstrates genuine love for his son
- Willing to sacrifice himself to protect Bart
- His bumbling nature leads to accidental heroism and injury

**Lisa Simpson:**
- Shows her role as the family's voice of reason
- Takes action when worried about family members
- Ultimately resorts to telling parents when other methods fail

**Dr. Hibbert:**
- Introduced as compassionate but somewhat detached doctor
- Uses visual horror to try to scare children
- His ironic commentary on TV violence sets his character tone

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Dr. Hibbert as Springfield's resident physician
2. Springfield Gorge as an iconic location
3. Homer's capacity for physical comedy and suffering
4. The show's ability to balance heart with humor
5. One of the most memorable and repeated scenes in TV history
6. Matt Groening's personal favorite episode
7. The formula of Homer's good intentions leading to disaster

## Trivia

- Homer's gorge fall has appeared in multiple clip shows and later episodes
- The scene was referenced in The Simpsons Movie (2007)
- Matt Groening consistently names this his favorite episode in interviews
- The gorge fall animation took considerable effort due to its length and complexity
- Lance Murdock returns in later episodes including "Bart's Inner Child" and "Viva Ned Flanders"
- Dr. Hibbert became one of the show's most recurring characters
- The "Three Stooges ward" joke references real-life concerns about media violence
- The ambulance crash gag extends the joke beyond expectations, a hallmark Simpsons technique
- Kevin Michael Richardson took over voicing Dr. Hibbert in 2021
