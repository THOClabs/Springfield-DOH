## Synopsis

Moe falls in love with a woman named Maya whom he met online. When they finally meet in person, he discovers she is only three feet tall. Despite initial concerns, Moe's friends are supportive, and the relationship blossoms. However, Moe continuously makes insensitive height-related jokes, leading Maya to reject his marriage proposal. When Moe considers surgery to reduce his height to match hers, Maya leaves permanently, wanting someone who truly accepts her. Meanwhile, Homer takes Maggie to happy hour at Moe's, where she's bullied by other babies at a nearby playground until Homer heroically intervenes.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Homer and Maggie
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Maggie

Marge expresses concern that Homer isn't spending enough quality time with Maggie. She wants him to be more involved in their youngest child's life.

#### Scene 2: Happy Hour at Moe's
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie, Moe, Lenny, Carl, Barney

In an unconventional parenting choice, Homer decides to take Maggie to happy hour at Moe's Tavern. While there, Moe tells Homer about an exciting development - he's met a woman online named Maya.

#### Scene 3: Moe's Online Romance
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Moe, Homer, bar patrons

Moe enthusiastically describes his online relationship with Maya. They've been chatting extensively and she's sent him beautiful photos. Moe is genuinely happy for perhaps the first time in years.

#### Scene 4: The Playground
**Location:** Playground near Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie, other babies, Kearney Jr.

While Homer drinks inside, Maggie plays at a nearby playground. Other babies, including Kearney's son, begin bullying her. Marge later discovers this through a hidden camera she planted in Maggie's bow.

#### Scene 5: Meeting Maya
**Location:** Restaurant / Public place
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Moe finally meets Maya in person for their first date. She looks exactly like her photos - beautiful, with a warm personality. However, Moe notices one "small detail": Maya is only three feet tall. She is a little person.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Moe's Initial Reaction
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Moe is initially uncertain how to react. Maya is wonderful in every way, but he worries about what his friends will think and whether he can handle the differences.

#### Scene 7: Friends' Reactions
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Moe, Homer, Lenny, Carl, Barney

Moe nervously tells his bar friends about Maya's height. Contrary to his fears, Homer, Lenny, Carl, and Barney are completely supportive. They encourage Moe to pursue the relationship and not let superficial concerns interfere with his happiness.

#### Scene 8: Moe's Joy
**Location:** Maya's House / Various
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Moe embraces the relationship. Upon realizing Maya genuinely finds him attractive, Moe joyfully exclaims (referencing Lewis Carroll's "Jabberwocky"). At Maya's house, the TV shows "Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory" with Oompa Loompas - an ironic moment. Maya mentions her photo was taken at Legoland.

#### Scene 9: The Relationship Blossoms
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Moe and Maya date successfully. They enjoy time together, and Moe seems genuinely happy. The relationship appears to be working despite their height difference.

#### Scene 10: Marge's Hidden Camera
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Maggie, camera footage

Marge reviews footage from the hidden camera she placed in Maggie's bow. She's shocked to see Maggie being bullied by other babies at the playground. One baby (Kearney Jr.) notably bangs three bottles together on his fingers (referencing "The Warriors").

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: Moe Proposes
**Location:** Restaurant / Romantic Setting
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Feeling confident in the relationship, Moe decides to propose to Maya. However, during his proposal speech, he continuously makes insensitive height-related jokes. He can't seem to help himself.

#### Scene 12: Maya Rejects the Proposal
**Location:** Restaurant
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Hurt by Moe's constant height jokes during what should be a romantic moment, Maya rejects his marriage proposal. She realizes Moe isn't truly comfortable with her height and uses humor to mask his discomfort.

#### Scene 13: Moe's Desperate Plan
**Location:** Doctor's Office / Various
**Characters:** Moe, Dr. Hibbert

Desperate to win Maya back, Moe comes up with a radical plan. He decides to undergo a risky height-reduction surgery to literally reduce his height to match hers. He thinks this will prove his love.

#### Scene 14: Homer Defends Maggie
**Location:** Playground near Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Maggie, Kearney Jr., other babies

Homer discovers that Maggie has been bullied. He heroically intervenes at the playground to defend her. In a comedic scene, Homer actually gets beaten up by the larger baby (Kearney Jr.) until Maggie rescues him. This proves Homer's dedication to his daughter.

#### Scene 15: Maya Stops Moe
**Location:** Hospital / Surgical Center
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Maya learns about Moe's planned surgery and convinces him not to go through with it. However, instead of being touched by his gesture, she is put off by his willingness to try something so extreme.

#### Scene 16: Maya Leaves Moe
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Moe, Maya

Maya explains why she must leave. She wants to be with a man who is truly comfortable with her size - not someone who needs surgery or makes constant jokes to cope. Moe's willingness to undergo the procedure proved he wasn't the right man for her.

#### Scene 17: Moe's Memories
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Moe, bar patrons

Although heartbroken, Moe is grateful for the happy memories he shared with Maya. He keeps her picture displayed in his bar as a reminder of a time when he experienced genuine love and happiness.

#### Scene 18: Family Reassured
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Maggie

Marge is reassured of Homer's parental devotion after seeing him defend Maggie. Despite his unconventional parenting style, Homer truly loves and protects his children.
