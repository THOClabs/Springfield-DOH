## Synopsis

When Bart is struck by Mr. Burns' limousine while skateboarding, the Simpson family is approached by ambulance-chasing lawyer Lionel Hutz, who convinces Homer to sue for a million dollars. With the help of quack physician Dr. Nick Riviera, they fabricate and exaggerate Bart's injuries to strengthen their case. But when Marge is called to testify and refuses to lie under oath, the lawsuit crumbles and the Simpsons lose everything. The episode culminates with Homer feeling betrayed by Marge's honesty, but ultimately the couple reconciles when Homer realizes her integrity is more valuable than money.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Springfield Street - The Accident
**Location:** Springfield street near crosswalk
**Characters:** Bart, Mr. Burns, Smithers

Bart is skateboarding recklessly through Springfield's streets. He weaves through traffic, performs tricks, and ignores traffic signals.

Meanwhile, Mr. Burns' limousine approaches an intersection. Smithers is driving while Burns sits in the back, oblivious to his surroundings.

At the crosswalk, Bart skates directly into the path of Burns' car. The limousine strikes Bart, sending him flying through the air.

**Mr. Burns:** Initially dismissive of the accident
**Smithers:** Concerned about legal implications

#### Scene 2: Bart's Near-Death Experience - Ascending
**Location:** Supernatural realm / stairway to Heaven
**Characters:** Bart, Heavenly Voice

Bart's soul separates from his body and begins ascending toward a bright light. He floats upward on an escalator-like stairway toward the gates of Heaven.

A heavenly voice speaks to Bart, welcoming him to the afterlife.

**Visual:** Clouds, golden light, and celestial imagery surround Bart

Bart approaches the pearly gates, but before he can enter, something goes wrong.

#### Scene 3: Bart's Near-Death Experience - Descending to Hell
**Location:** Hell
**Characters:** Bart, The Devil/Satan

The escalator reverses direction. Bart is sent hurtling downward through fire and brimstone, eventually arriving in Hell.

The Devil appears before Bart. Hell is depicted with traditional imagery - flames, demons, and eternal suffering.

**The Devil:** Informs Bart he's not supposed to be there yet - it's not his time.

Satan tells Bart he's being sent back to Earth, but ominously suggests they'll meet again in the future.

**The Devil:** "You're not due here for another few decades. But I'll be seeing you later."

Bart is thrust back toward the living world.

#### Scene 4: Springfield General Hospital - Bart Wakes Up
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital - patient room
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge, Lisa, Dr. Hibbert

Bart awakens in a hospital bed, surrounded by his worried family. He's disoriented but alive.

**Dr. Hibbert:** Examines Bart and provides his diagnosis - minor injuries, nothing serious. Some bumps and bruises, but Bart will make a full recovery.

Homer and Marge are relieved their son will be okay.

Dr. Hibbert explains that Bart was very lucky - the injuries could have been much worse.

#### Scene 5: Hospital Hallway - Lionel Hutz Appears
**Location:** Springfield General Hospital - hallway
**Characters:** Homer, Lionel Hutz

As Homer walks through the hospital hallway, he's approached by a man in a powder-blue suit.

**Lionel Hutz:** Introduces himself as an attorney. He gives Homer his business card, which reads "Lionel Hutz, Attorney at Law" with the tagline "Works on Contingency? No, Money Down!"

Hutz explains that Homer could be entitled to significant compensation for Bart's injuries. He mentions that Mr. Burns is extremely wealthy.

**Lionel Hutz:** "Mr. Simpson, you could be looking at a million dollars!"

Homer's eyes light up at the mention of money.

Hutz hands Homer another business card - this one falls apart, but his pitch has landed.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Simpson Home - Discussing the Lawsuit
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - living room
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Lisa

Homer excitedly tells Marge about his meeting with Lionel Hutz and the potential lawsuit against Mr. Burns.

**Marge:** Expresses moral reservations about the scheme. She questions whether it's right to sue for injuries that weren't serious.

**Lisa:** Also voices concerns about honesty and integrity.

**Homer:** Dismisses their concerns. He's focused on the potential payout.

Homer argues that Mr. Burns is rich, the accident was Burns' fault, and they deserve compensation.

#### Scene 7: Burns Manor - The Initial Offer
**Location:** Burns Manor - Mr. Burns' office
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Homer, Lionel Hutz

Homer and Lionel Hutz visit Burns Manor to discuss a settlement.

Mr. Burns, seated behind his massive desk, is flanked by Smithers.

**Mr. Burns:** Offers Homer $100 and a waiver releasing Burns from all liability.

**Homer:** Is initially tempted by the money (it's $100 more than he had before).

**Lionel Hutz:** Intervenes, outraged by the insulting offer. He dramatically rejects it and storms out with Homer.

Burns is taken aback by this rejection.

#### Scene 8: Lionel Hutz's Office - Building the Case
**Location:** Lionel Hutz's law office
**Characters:** Lionel Hutz, Homer, Bart

Hutz meets with Homer and Bart to prepare for the lawsuit.

The office is shabby and clearly indicates Hutz is not a successful attorney. Papers are strewn everywhere, and his law degree is questionable.

**Lionel Hutz:** Explains that to win big, they need to prove Bart's injuries are severe and lasting.

He begins coaching Bart on how to exaggerate his symptoms - limping, complaining of constant pain, wearing unnecessary bandages and a neck brace.

**Bart:** Initially uncomfortable with lying but is gradually convinced.

#### Scene 9: Dr. Nick Riviera's Clinic - The Fake Examination
**Location:** Dr. Nick Riviera's medical office
**Characters:** Dr. Nick Riviera, Homer, Bart, Lionel Hutz

Hutz introduces Homer and Bart to Dr. Nick Riviera, a physician of questionable credentials.

**Dr. Nick Riviera:** "Hi everybody!" (his signature greeting - first appearance)

Dr. Nick's office is filled with dubious diplomas and medical equipment that looks more like props.

He performs a theatrical "examination" of Bart, making up elaborate injuries and complications.

**Dr. Nick:** Produces a wildly exaggerated medical report documenting injuries Bart doesn't have.

The report claims Bart has suffered permanent damage requiring years of treatment.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: Simpson Home - Marge's Objections
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - kitchen
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Marge confronts Homer about the lawsuit's dishonest direction.

**Marge:** She's deeply uncomfortable with the lies being told. This isn't right.

**Homer:** Argues that Burns is evil, rich, and deserves to pay. Besides, Bart WAS hit by the car.

The couple's disagreement intensifies. Marge believes that lying in court is wrong regardless of who they're lying about.

**Homer:** "Marge, we could be rich! Don't you want nice things?"

**Marge:** "Not if we have to lie to get them, Homer."

Their marriage begins to strain under the weight of this moral divide.

#### Scene 11: Springfield Courthouse - The Trial Begins
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - courtroom
**Characters:** Judge Snyder, Lionel Hutz, Blue-Haired Lawyer, Homer, Mr. Burns, Bart, Jury

The trial of Simpson vs. Burns begins.

Judge Snyder presides. The Blue-Haired Lawyer represents Mr. Burns.

Lionel Hutz makes his opening statement, portraying Bart as a grievously injured child and Burns as a callous monster.

The Blue-Haired Lawyer counters, suggesting the Simpsons are opportunistic frauds seeking undeserved compensation.

#### Scene 12: Courtroom - Bart's Testimony
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - witness stand
**Characters:** Bart, Lionel Hutz, Blue-Haired Lawyer

Bart takes the witness stand, wearing a neck brace and pretending to be in pain.

**Lionel Hutz:** Leads Bart through rehearsed testimony about his "suffering."

**Bart:** Describes phantom pains, nightmares, and inability to enjoy normal childhood activities.

The Blue-Haired Lawyer cross-examines, trying to catch Bart in inconsistencies.

Bart struggles to maintain his act but manages to stick to the script.

#### Scene 13: Courtroom - Mr. Burns' Testimony
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - witness stand
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Blue-Haired Lawyer, Lionel Hutz

Mr. Burns takes the stand to present his version of events.

Burns gives wildly implausible testimony, claiming Bart was the aggressor and describing the boy in impossibly negative terms.

His testimony is so exaggerated and obviously false that it actually helps the Simpsons' case.

**Mr. Burns:** Describes a fictional scenario where Bart attacked his vehicle

The jury appears skeptical of Burns' account.

#### Scene 14: Courtroom - Dr. Nick's Testimony
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - witness stand
**Characters:** Dr. Nick Riviera, Lionel Hutz, Blue-Haired Lawyer

Dr. Nick Riviera testifies as a medical expert about Bart's injuries.

**Dr. Nick:** Presents his fraudulent medical findings with theatrical flair.

He describes invented conditions and claims Bart needs extensive (and expensive) long-term care.

The Blue-Haired Lawyer challenges Dr. Nick's credentials but struggles to completely discredit him.

---

### ACT FOUR

#### Scene 15: Burns Manor - The Settlement Offer
**Location:** Burns Manor - sitting room
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Smithers, Homer, Marge, Lionel Hutz

Burns invites the Simpsons to his mansion for settlement negotiations.

Seeing the trial going poorly, Burns decides to offer a substantial settlement to make the case go away.

**Mr. Burns:** Offers $500,000 to settle out of court.

Homer is ecstatic. This is more money than he's ever dreamed of.

**Lionel Hutz:** Advises accepting the deal.

**Marge:** Remains silent, deeply troubled by profiting from lies.

#### Scene 16: Simpson Home - The Decision
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace - bedroom
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

That night, Homer and Marge discuss the settlement offer.

**Homer:** Eager to accept. Half a million dollars would change their lives.

**Marge:** Cannot reconcile taking money for a fraud. She believes the entire lawsuit is wrong.

The argument escalates. Homer accuses Marge of not caring about the family's future. Marge accuses Homer of caring more about money than integrity.

**Marge:** "Homer, I can't be part of this lie."

Their marriage is at a breaking point.

#### Scene 17: Courtroom - Marge's Testimony
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - witness stand
**Characters:** Marge, Blue-Haired Lawyer, Lionel Hutz, Homer, Judge Snyder

The trial continues. The Blue-Haired Lawyer calls Marge Simpson to the stand.

**Marge:** Takes the oath to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth.

The Blue-Haired Lawyer asks Marge about Dr. Nick's credibility and Bart's true condition.

Marge hesitates. She looks at Homer, who silently pleads with her to stick to their story.

**Marge:** Cannot lie under oath. She tells the truth.

She admits that Dr. Nick was hired by Hutz specifically to exaggerate Bart's injuries. She confirms that Dr. Hibbert's original diagnosis was accurate - Bart's injuries were minor.

She reveals that Bart was coached to lie on the stand and that Homer pushed for the lawsuit out of greed.

**Lionel Hutz:** Objects frantically but the damage is done.

The jury's sympathies shift entirely against the Simpsons.

**Homer:** Watches in horror as the case crumbles.

#### Scene 18: Courtroom - The Verdict
**Location:** Springfield Municipal Court - courtroom
**Characters:** Judge Snyder, Jury, Homer, Marge, Mr. Burns

The jury deliberates and returns with a verdict.

The Simpsons' case is dismissed. They receive nothing.

**Mr. Burns:** Smirks victoriously.

**Homer:** Is devastated. He glares at Marge with anger and betrayal.

The courtroom empties. The Simpsons have lost.

#### Scene 19: Simpson Home - Homer Leaves
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer storms through the house, furious at Marge.

**Homer:** Blames Marge for costing the family $500,000. He can't even look at her.

**Marge:** Tries to explain that she couldn't lie under oath. It's perjury. It's wrong.

**Homer:** Doesn't want to hear it. He leaves the house, slamming the door.

#### Scene 20: Moe's Tavern - Homer Drinks Alone
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Moe, Marge

Homer sits at Moe's bar, drowning his sorrows.

**Moe:** Listens as Homer complains about Marge ruining everything.

Homer can't understand why Marge would choose honesty over half a million dollars.

**Homer:** "She threw away our fortune, Moe. For what? Being honest?"

The tavern door opens. Marge has come looking for Homer.

#### Scene 21: Moe's Tavern - Reconciliation
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Moe

Marge approaches Homer at the bar.

**Marge:** Tries to explain herself. She loves Homer, but she couldn't live with herself if she lied in court.

Homer initially resists, still angry about the lost money.

But then Marge asks Homer to look into her eyes.

**Marge:** "Homer, look at me."

Homer turns and looks into Marge's eyes.

In that moment, he sees the woman he married - honest, loving, principled. He realizes that her integrity is exactly why he fell in love with her.

**Homer:** His anger melts away. He understands.

**Homer:** "When I looked into your eyes, I saw my wife... and I couldn't stay mad."

The couple embraces, reconciling.

**Thematic Resolution:** Homer chooses love and integrity over money.

#### Scene 22: Moe's Tavern - Final Moment
**Location:** Moe's Tavern
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Moe

Homer and Marge prepare to leave together, their marriage intact.

**Moe:** Makes a sarcastic comment about the touching moment.

Homer and Marge exit the bar, hand in hand.

**Final Image:** The couple walking out together, having chosen each other over wealth.

## Production Notes

- Written by John Swartzwelder, who would become one of The Simpsons' most prolific writers
- The emotional ending was pitched by executive producer James L. Brooks
- Director Mark Kirkland drew inspiration from classic courtroom films
- Dr. Nick's design was based on supervising director Gabor Csupo (animators thought Azaria was imitating him)
- Phil Hartman's performance as Lionel Hutz was immediately recognized as a standout
- The episode established a template for Simpsons "legal drama" episodes

## Character Introductions

### Lionel Hutz
- **Full Name:** Lionel Hutz
- **Occupation:** Attorney at Law (barely)
- **Voice:** Phil Hartman
- **Appearance:** Powder-blue suit, slicked-back hair, perpetually disheveled
- **Traits:** Unethical, incompetent, ambulance-chasing, works on contingency
- **Catchphrase:** "Works on Contingency? No, Money Down!" (intentionally ambiguous)
- **Future:** Becomes a recurring character until Hartman's death in 1998

### Dr. Nick Riviera
- **Full Name:** Dr. Nicholas Riviera
- **Occupation:** Physician (questionable credentials)
- **Voice:** Hank Azaria
- **Appearance:** Dark hair, mustache, white coat
- **Catchphrase:** "Hi everybody!" (crowd responds "Hi Dr. Nick!")
- **Traits:** Cheerful, incompetent, willing to fabricate medical records
- **Future:** Becomes a recurring character throughout the series

### Blue-Haired Lawyer
- **Occupation:** Corporate attorney for Mr. Burns and others
- **Appearance:** Blue hair, glasses, three-piece suit
- **Traits:** Competent, ruthless, represents wealthy clients
- **Design basis:** Modeled after Roy Cohn

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. **Lionel Hutz** as a recurring character representing legal incompetence
2. **Dr. Nick Riviera** as a recurring character representing medical quackery
3. **The Simpsons' afterlife mythology** with Bart's near-death experience
4. **Homer and Marge's core values** - their marriage survives through honesty
5. **Legal satire** as a recurring theme in the series
6. **Mr. Burns' willingness** to use lawyers and money to avoid consequences
7. **Marge as the family's moral center** - she won't compromise her integrity

## Trivia

- The episode's plot mirrors Billy Wilder's "The Fortune Cookie" (1966)
- Hank Azaria created Dr. Nick's voice as a "bad Ricky Ricardo impression"
- Animators designed Dr. Nick after Gabor Csupo, thinking Azaria was imitating him
- Phil Hartman would voice Lionel Hutz in 33 episodes before his death
- This is the first of many episodes to satirize the American legal system
- The Devil's appearance established that supernatural elements exist in The Simpsons universe
- Marge's testimony scene is considered one of the series' early emotional highlights
- The reconciliation at Moe's Tavern was added at James L. Brooks' insistence
