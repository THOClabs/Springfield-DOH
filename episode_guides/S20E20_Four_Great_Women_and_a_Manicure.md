## Synopsis

Marge and Lisa visit a nail salon where they debate whether women can be intelligent, powerful, and beautiful all at once. To explore this question, they share four tales of accomplished women - historical and fictional - featuring Springfield residents in the roles. The stories span from Queen Elizabeth I to Snow White to Lady Macbeth to Ayn Rand's Howard Roark (reimagined as Maggie).

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence
**Chalkboard Gag:** Standard
**Couch Gag:** Standard (Bart's only appearance in episode)

### FRAMING STORY: Nail Salon

#### Scene 1: Marge and Lisa Visit Nail Salon
**Location:** Springfield Nail Salon
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa, Patty, Selma, salon workers

Marge and Lisa arrive at a nail salon for manicures. Lisa questions whether women should prioritize beauty treatments. This sparks a debate about whether women can be smart, powerful, AND beautiful simultaneously. Marge decides to tell stories of great women to prove her point.

---

### SEGMENT 1: Queen Elizabeth I (1588)

#### Scene 2: The Queen's Court
**Location:** English Royal Court
**Characters:** Selma (as Elizabeth I), various suitors

Marge narrates. Queen Elizabeth (Selma) sits on her throne, bored and dismissive of the many suitors trying to win her hand in marriage. She rejects them all - she has no interest in giving up her independence or power.

#### Scene 3: Sir Walter Raleigh Arrives
**Location:** Royal Court
**Characters:** Selma (Elizabeth), Homer (Sir Walter Raleigh)

Sir Walter Raleigh (Homer) returns from exploring the New World. He presents the Queen with tobacco, attempting to impress her. The Queen is mildly intrigued but still not interested in marriage.

#### Scene 4: King Julio's Visit
**Location:** Royal Court
**Characters:** Selma (Elizabeth), King Julio, Court Jester

King Julio of Spain arrives as another suitor. However, he becomes more interested in the court jester than the Queen. His romantic rejection of Elizabeth for the jester insults the Spanish monarchy.

#### Scene 5: The Spanish Armada
**Location:** English Coast
**Characters:** Selma (Elizabeth), Homer (Raleigh), English Navy

Humiliated, King Julio attacks England with the Spanish Armada. Sir Walter Raleigh accidentally sets fire to his own ships while smoking tobacco. However, the burning English ships drift into and destroy the Spanish fleet, securing an accidental victory.

#### Scene 6: Victory Celebration
**Location:** Royal Court
**Characters:** Selma (Elizabeth), Homer (Raleigh)

Queen Elizabeth knights Raleigh for his "victory." She declares triumphantly: "I don't need a man. I have England." Her independence and power are affirmed.

---

### SEGMENT 2: Snow White and the Seven Legally-Distinct Dwarfs

#### Scene 7: Lisa's Story Begins
**Location:** Nail Salon / Fairy Tale Kingdom
**Characters:** Lisa (narrator), Blue-Haired Lawyer

Lisa begins to tell Snow White. The Blue-Haired Lawyer interrupts, claiming the story is owned by Disney and any retelling infringes copyright. Lisa counters that Snow White is a classical fairy tale from the Brothers Grimm, predating Disney, and belongs to the public domain.

#### Scene 8: The Evil Queen's Jealousy
**Location:** Castle
**Characters:** Patty (Evil Queen), Magic Mirror

The Evil Queen (Patty) asks her magic mirror who is fairest. The mirror reveals Snow White (Lisa) is more beautiful. The jealous Queen plots Snow White's death.

#### Scene 9: The Huntsman's Mission
**Location:** Forest
**Characters:** Groundskeeper Willie (Huntsman), Lisa (Snow White)

The Queen orders her huntsman (Willie) to take Snow White into the forest and kill her. Willie takes Snow White deep into the woods but cannot bring himself to stab her. (Running gag: Willie can't stab anything throughout the episode - not Snow White, not a pig, not even red construction paper.)

#### Scene 10: The Seven Dwarfs' Cottage
**Location:** Cottage in the Woods
**Characters:** Lisa (Snow White), The Seven Dwarfs

Snow White finds a cottage where seven dwarfs live. They include Moe, Mr. Burns, Barney, Homer, Lenny, Kearney, and Dr. Hibbert in dwarf roles. They welcome her to stay with them.

#### Scene 11: The Poisoned Apple
**Location:** Cottage
**Characters:** Patty (Evil Queen in disguise), Lisa (Snow White)

The Evil Queen disguises herself as an old woman and offers Snow White a poisoned apple. Snow White takes a bite and falls into a death-like sleep.

#### Scene 12: The Female Doctor
**Location:** Cottage
**Characters:** Lisa (Snow White), Female Doctor, Dwarfs

True to Lisa's feminist beliefs, Snow White is NOT awakened by a prince's kiss. Instead, a female doctor arrives and uses medical knowledge to revive her. The story ends with a message about women saving themselves.

---

### SEGMENT 3: Macbeth (Modern Theatre Production)

#### Scene 13: Theatre Rehearsal
**Location:** Springfield Community Theatre
**Characters:** Homer (cast as Tree), Marge, Sideshow Mel (Lead Actor), Dr. Hibbert, Chazz Busby (Director)

Marge narrates. Homer has been cast as a tree in a community theatre production of Macbeth. He has minimal lines and is frustrated by his small role. Sideshow Mel has the lead role of Macbeth.

#### Scene 14: Lady Macbeth's Ambition
**Location:** Backstage / Home
**Characters:** Marge (Lady Macbeth mindset), Homer

Marge, channeling Lady Macbeth, convinces Homer that he deserves the lead role. She encourages him to eliminate the competition - literally. Her ambition corrupts them both.

#### Scene 15: The Murders
**Location:** Theatre
**Characters:** Homer, Sideshow Mel, Dr. Hibbert, other cast members

Homer begins murdering his fellow cast members to advance to the lead role. Sideshow Mel is killed. Dr. Hibbert is killed. One by one, Homer eliminates the competition.

#### Scene 16: Homer Gets the Lead
**Location:** Theatre
**Characters:** Homer (as Macbeth), Marge, Director

With everyone else dead, Homer finally gets the lead role of Macbeth. However, his acting is terrible. Reviews are devastating - critics suggest nearly anyone else would be better in the role.

#### Scene 17: The Ghosts Appear
**Location:** Theatre / Home
**Characters:** Marge, Ghosts of murdered actors

The ghosts of Homer's victims begin appearing to Marge, just as Banquo's ghost appears in the actual play. Marge is driven mad by guilt and dies from the haunting.

#### Scene 18: Marge's Ghost
**Location:** Theatre
**Characters:** Homer, Marge's Ghost

Marge's ghost appears to Homer and haunts him into actually performing well. In death, she finally gets him to give a good performance.

---

### SEGMENT 4: The Fountainhead (Maggie Roark)

#### Scene 19: Mediocri-Tots Daycare
**Location:** Mediocri-Tots Daycare
**Characters:** Maggie (Maggie Roark, voiced by Jodie Foster), Mr. Ellsworth Toohey, Gerald, other babies

Based on Ayn Rand's novel "The Fountainhead." Maggie attends a daycare called "Mediocri-Tots" run by the conformist Mr. Ellsworth Toohey. He demands all children be identical and mediocre. No individuality is allowed.

#### Scene 20: Maggie's Gift
**Location:** Daycare
**Characters:** Maggie Roark, Mr. Toohey, other children

Maggie refuses to conform. She builds incredible, innovative structures out of blocks while other children stack simple towers. Her artistic genius is evident. Mr. Toohey sees her individuality as a threat.

#### Scene 21: Conflict with Authority
**Location:** Daycare
**Characters:** Maggie Roark, Mr. Toohey

Mr. Toohey destroys Maggie's creations and demands she build like everyone else. Maggie defiantly rebuilds, bigger and better. The conflict escalates.

#### Scene 22: The Trial
**Location:** Parent-Teacher Conference / Courtroom Setting
**Characters:** Maggie Roark (voiced by Jodie Foster), Mr. Toohey, Parents

At a parent-teacher conference that becomes a trial, Maggie is put on trial for her nonconformity. Mr. Toohey prosecutes her individuality as dangerous to society.

#### Scene 23: Maggie's Speech
**Location:** Courtroom
**Characters:** Maggie Roark

Maggie delivers a passionate speech (voiced by Jodie Foster) defending artistic individuality, genius, and the right to be exceptional. Her speech echoes Howard Roark's famous courtroom defense in the novel.

#### Scene 24: Maggie Triumphant
**Location:** Future Springfield
**Characters:** Maggie Roark

Maggie grows up to become a renowned architect, designing buildings throughout Springfield. Her individuality and genius are vindicated. She builds monuments to human achievement.

---

### CLOSING: Return to Nail Salon

#### Scene 25: Conclusion
**Location:** Nail Salon
**Characters:** Marge, Lisa

Marge and Lisa conclude their visit. The stories have proven that women throughout history have been smart, powerful, and beautiful. Lisa acknowledges Marge's point while maintaining her own values about substance over appearance.

## Production Notes

- This is the only episode where Bart Simpson doesn't appear outside the opening couch gag
- This is the second Simpsons episode to have four acts (after "Simpsons Bible Stories")
- Jodie Foster provides the voice for Maggie Simpson's extended speaking role
- The episode is considered non-canon as an anthology/fantasy episode
- Written by Valentina L. Garza, one of the show's female writers

---

## Trivia

- Running Gag: Groundskeeper Willie cannot bring himself to stab anything - not Snow White, not a pig, not even red construction paper
- The Seven Dwarfs are "legally distinct" to avoid Disney copyright claims
- Maggie's Fountainhead segment is the only time she has extended dialogue (thanks to guest voice Jodie Foster)
- The episode explores feminist themes through different eras and stories
- Queen Elizabeth I's declaration "I don't need a man. I have England" reflects the historical "Virgin Queen"

## Themes

- **Women's Power and Independence** - Each segment features a woman who succeeds through her own abilities
- **Conformity vs. Individuality** - Especially prominent in The Fountainhead segment
- **Beauty vs. Substance** - Lisa and Marge's initial debate
- **Feminist Retellings** - Snow White saved by a doctor, not a prince
- **Historical Female Power** - Elizabeth I as unmarried ruler
