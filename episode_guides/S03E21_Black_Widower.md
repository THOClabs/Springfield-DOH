## Synopsis

Selma Bouvier announces she's engaged to marry her prison pen-pal: Sideshow Bob, Bart's arch-nemesis. Despite Bob's claims of rehabilitation, Bart remains suspicious. During the wedding reception, Bart pieces together clues from Bob's video letter and realizes Bob is planning to murder Selma for her money. Bart races to the honeymoon hotel to save his aunt before Bob's gas explosion scheme can succeed.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Opening Sequence

**Chalkboard Gag:** "Funny noises are not funny"
**Couch Gag:** The family arrives to find the couch has been stolen/robbed.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Selma's Announcement
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie, Selma, Patty

The Simpson family gathers as Selma makes a shocking announcement: she's engaged to be married.

The family is surprised - they didn't even know she was dating anyone.

Selma reveals she met her fiancé through a prison pen-pal program.

#### Scene 2: The Reveal
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Selma, Patty

Selma announces who her fiancé is: Sideshow Bob.

Bart is horrified. This is the man who framed Krusty the Clown and tried to kill Bart.

The family watches a video letter from Bob in prison.

#### Scene 3: Bob's Prison Video
**Location:** Springfield State Prison (on video)
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Prison Guards

On the video, Bob appears reformed and sincere. He speaks lovingly about Selma.

However, there's a moment of frustration on the tape - an outburst that hints at his true nature.

A memorable scene shows Bob's Emmy Award being confiscated.

**Prison Guard:** "You know the rules. Awards for excellence in entertainment are contraband. No Emmys, no Oscars, not even a Golden Globe."

Bob's Emmy is added to a huge pile of confiscated awards.

**Notable First:** Snake Jailbird's full name is first mentioned in this episode when Bob says goodbye to his prison friends.

#### Scene 4: Bob's Release
**Location:** Springfield State Prison
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Snake Jailbird, prison inmates

Bob is granted parole. He says goodbye to his prison friends, including Snake Jailbird.

Bob walks free, supposedly a changed man.

#### Scene 5: Meeting the Family
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Selma, Sideshow Bob

Bob arrives at the Simpson home to meet the family.

He's charming, cultured, and seemingly reformed. He quotes poetry and speaks eloquently.

Homer, Marge, and Lisa are won over.

Bart remains deeply suspicious. He doesn't trust Bob at all.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Bob's Rehabilitation Act
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Selma, Homer, Marge, Bart

Bob continues his charm offensive. He dotes on Selma and impresses the family.

He appears genuinely devoted to Selma.

Bart watches carefully, looking for any sign of Bob's true intentions.

#### Scene 7: The Wedding
**Location:** Springfield Church
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Selma, Reverend Lovejoy, Simpson family, Patty, wedding guests

Sideshow Bob and Selma are married.

Reverend Lovejoy officiates the ceremony.

The Simpson family attends. Bart watches with dread.

Bob and Selma exchange vows. They're now husband and wife.

#### Scene 8: The Wedding Reception
**Location:** Reception venue
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Selma, Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Patty, guests

The wedding reception is in full swing.

The family watches the wedding video, including Bob's earlier prison tape.

Bart notices something on the video - a subtle clue about Bob's real plan.

#### Scene 9: Bart's Realization
**Location:** Reception venue
**Characters:** Bart, Lisa

Bart reviews the clues:
1. Bob mentioned Selma lost her senses of smell and taste in a childhood accident
2. Bob emphasized Selma's heavy smoking habit
3. Bob focused on Selma's stock market fortune and life insurance

Bart realizes Bob's scheme: He's going to kill Selma and inherit her money!

But how? And when?

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 10: The Honeymoon
**Location:** Hotel at Mount Springfield
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Selma

Bob and Selma check into their honeymoon suite at a hotel on Mount Springfield.

The room has a gas fireplace.

Bob is attentive and loving... but he's actually setting his trap.

#### Scene 11: Bob's Scheme Unfolds
**Location:** Hotel room
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Selma

Bob's plan:
1. Open the gas valve on the fireplace, filling the room with gas
2. Leave Selma alone to watch her favorite show, MacGyver
3. After MacGyver ends, Selma will light her post-show cigarette
4. The spark will ignite the gas, causing an explosion
5. Bob inherits Selma's fortune

Selma can't smell the gas because she lost her sense of smell.

Bob opens the gas valve and leaves.

**Sideshow Bob:** "The wedding was very tough on you. And the honeymoon is going to be... murder!"

#### Scene 12: Bart Races to Save Selma
**Location:** Various / Hotel at Mount Springfield
**Characters:** Bart, Homer (driving)

Bart figures out the complete plan. MacGyver! The cigarette! The gas!

He desperately tries to reach Selma before she lights up.

Homer drives Bart to the hotel as fast as possible.

#### Scene 13: The Confrontation - Psycho Reference
**Location:** Hotel room
**Characters:** Bart, Sideshow Bob, Selma

Bob returns to the hotel room, expecting to find Selma dead.

He approaches a chair with its back to him. He turns it around...

Bart is sitting in the chair, alive and smirking.

Bob turns and sees Selma standing in the doorway, unharmed.

**Visual Reference:** This sequence directly references the climax of Alfred Hitchcock's Psycho (1960), right down to the camera angles and Alf Clausen's Bernard Herrmann-style score.

#### Scene 14: Bob Exposed
**Location:** Hotel room
**Characters:** Bart, Sideshow Bob, Selma, Chief Wiggum, police

Bart explains how he figured out Bob's scheme:
- Bob opened the gas valve
- Selma couldn't smell it due to her condition
- Bob left during MacGyver, knowing she'd light a cigarette afterward
- The spark would cause an explosion

Bob is caught. The police arrive with Chief Wiggum.

#### Scene 15: The Explosion
**Location:** Hotel room
**Characters:** Chief Wiggum, police, Sideshow Bob

Chief Wiggum lights a celebratory cigar after the arrest.

He absent-mindedly throws the lit match into the gas-filled room.

BOOM! The explosion happens anyway, just not with Selma inside.

#### Scene 16: Bob's Arrest and Vow
**Location:** Outside the hotel
**Characters:** Sideshow Bob, Bart, police

Bob is arrested and led away in handcuffs.

He vows revenge on Bart, swearing to return.

**Sideshow Bob:** "I'll be back! You can't keep the Democrats out of the White House forever!"

Bob is returned to prison. Selma is safe. Bart is a hero once again.

## Production Notes

- Written by Jon Vitti (teleplay) with story by Sam Simon and Thomas Chastain
- Directed by David Silverman
- Kelsey Grammer's second appearance as Sideshow Bob
- The Psycho homage required careful coordination between animation and Alf Clausen's score
- Bob's prison number 24601 references Jean Valjean from Les Misérables
- Also used as Principal Skinner's Vietnam prisoner number and Hank Jennings' number in Twin Peaks
- Snake Jailbird named for the snake tattoo on his arm
- Finished 39th in Nielsen ratings for the week

## Character Development

**Sideshow Bob:**
- Established as recurring villain, not one-time antagonist
- His intelligence and cultured manner make him dangerous
- His need for revenge on Bart becomes central motivation
- Kelsey Grammer's voice work praised by critics

**Bart Simpson:**
- Shows detective skills and persistence
- Protects family despite their doubts
- His history with Bob makes him uniquely able to see through the act
- Cements his role as Bob's nemesis

**Selma Bouvier:**
- Her loneliness makes her vulnerable to Bob's manipulation
- The loss of smell/taste (established earlier) becomes plot-critical
- Her MacGyver obsession provides the murder timeline

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Sideshow Bob as a recurring villain, not a one-off
2. Bob and Bart's ongoing nemesis relationship
3. Bob's willingness to commit murder
4. Snake Jailbird's full name
5. The template for future Bob episodes (elaborate schemes, literary references)
6. Bob's prison number as a running gag
7. The show's ability to do mystery/thriller plots
