## Synopsis

After Bart orders a mail-order spy camera, he accidentally photographs Homer dancing intimately with exotic belly dancer Princess Kashmir at a coworker's bachelor party. When the scandalous photo spreads throughout Springfield and reaches Marge, she kicks Homer out of the house. To reconcile with Marge and teach Bart that women are not objects, Homer must track down Princess Kashmir across Springfield's many gentlemen's clubs so Bart can learn she's a real person deserving of respect.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: Simpson Home - Bart's Mail Order
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Bart, Homer, Marge, Lisa

Bart excitedly receives a package in the mail. He's ordered a miniature spy camera from a mail-order catalog (alongside other novelty items like X-Ray Glasses and a Hyp-No Coin).

The camera is tiny and perfect for covert photography. Bart immediately begins taking candid shots around the house.

A title card appears: "Six Months Later"

#### Scene 2: Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Location:** Springfield Nuclear Power Plant
**Characters:** Homer, coworkers

Time has passed. Homer's former assistant Eugene Fisk has been promoted to become Homer's supervisor. Eugene is getting married, and the coworkers are planning a bachelor party.

Homer is invited to Eugene Fisk's bachelor party at The Rusty Barnacle restaurant that evening.

#### Scene 3: Simpson Home - Evening Preparations
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Homer tells Marge he's going to a bachelor party for a coworker. Marge is fine with this.

While Homer is away, Marge decides to take the children out for a nice dinner at The Rusty Barnacle - the same seafood restaurant where the bachelor party will be held.

#### Scene 4: The Rusty Barnacle - Bachelor Party
**Location:** The Rusty Barnacle Restaurant
**Characters:** Homer, Barney, Lenny, Carl, Eugene Fisk, Princess Kashmir, partygoers

The bachelor party is in full swing in a private room. Homer is having a great time with his coworkers including Barney Gumble, Lenny Leonard, and Carl Carlson.

**Notable First:** Carl Carlson's first appearance on the show.

The entertainment arrives: an exotic belly dancer named Princess Kashmir. She performs for the party and invites Homer to dance with her onstage.

Homer, caught up in the moment, dances enthusiastically with Princess Kashmir. She playfully puts him in suggestive poses.

#### Scene 5: The Rusty Barnacle - The Photo
**Location:** The Rusty Barnacle Restaurant - Dining Area
**Characters:** Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

Meanwhile, in the main dining area, the Simpson family is having dinner. Bart wanders away from the table, camera in hand.

Bart discovers the bachelor party room. Through a window, he spots Homer dancing intimately with Princess Kashmir. Homer is tucking money into her costume.

Bart raises his spy camera and snaps the photo.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 6: Springfield Elementary School
**Location:** Springfield Elementary School
**Characters:** Bart, Milhouse, Martin Prince, students

Bart brings the developed photo to school, showing off the scandalous image to his classmates.

Milhouse is impressed and asks for a copy. Word spreads quickly through the school. Martin Prince and other students all want copies.

Bart becomes a celebrity, mass-producing copies of the photo.

#### Scene 7: The Photo Spreads - Montage
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Various Springfield residents

The photo spreads like wildfire throughout Springfield:
- Workers at the nuclear plant pass it around
- Patrons at Moe's Tavern see it
- Shoppers at the Kwik-E-Mart encounter it
- Church members glimpse it
- Mr. Burns and Smithers receive a copy

Everyone in Springfield seems to have seen the picture of Homer and Princess Kashmir.

#### Scene 8: Simpson Home - Discovery
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Marge, Homer

Marge discovers the photo. She is furious and hurt.

Marge confronts Homer about his behavior. She's not only upset about his actions but worried about what message this sends to their children, especially Bart.

Marge kicks Homer out of the house.

#### Scene 9: Homer's Exile
**Location:** Various - Moe's Tavern, Barney's apartment
**Characters:** Homer, Barney, Moe

Homeless and in disgrace, Homer stays at Barney's apartment.

Homer is miserable. He tries to figure out how to win Marge back.

#### Scene 10: Simpson Home - The Condition
**Location:** 742 Evergreen Terrace
**Characters:** Homer, Marge

Homer returns to apologize. Marge says she'll only take him back under one condition:

Homer must find Princess Kashmir and introduce her to Bart. Bart needs to understand that she's a real person with feelings, not just an object for male entertainment. Homer must teach Bart to respect women.

Homer agrees to the mission.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 11: The Search Begins
**Location:** Various strip clubs throughout Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Bart

Homer takes Bart on a quest to find Princess Kashmir. They visit multiple gentlemen's clubs across Springfield:

1. **Florence of Arabia** - She's not there
2. **The Girlesque** - They just missed her
3. **Foxy Boxing** - Wrong place
4. **Mud City** - No luck

Homer calls Marge to update her. He'll try one more place: The Sapphire Lounge.

**Production Note:** The Simpsons staff visited real strip clubs in Hollywood for reference, including one based on the Seventh Veil.

#### Scene 12: The Sapphire Lounge - Backstage
**Location:** The Sapphire Lounge - Backstage
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Princess Kashmir (Shauna Tifton)

At the Sapphire Lounge, Homer and Bart finally find Princess Kashmir backstage, preparing for her performance.

Homer awkwardly explains why they're there. Princess Kashmir understands and is touched that Homer is trying to teach his son respect.

She introduces herself properly: "My real name is Shauna Tifton. My pet peeve is rude people, and my turn-ons include silk sheets and a warm fireplace."

She shows Bart that she's a real person with a normal life outside her job.

#### Scene 13: The Sapphire Lounge - Onstage Accident
**Location:** The Sapphire Lounge - Main Stage
**Characters:** Homer, Bart, Princess Kashmir, Gulliver Dark, showgirls, audience, Marge

The burlesque show begins. The emcee Gulliver Dark introduces the international showgirl revue featuring dancers in costumes from around the world.

Homer accidentally finds himself pushed onstage during the performance. The spotlight hits him.

Someone in the audience recognizes Homer from the famous photo! The crowd goes wild, chanting for Homer.

#### Scene 14: Homer's Speech
**Location:** The Sapphire Lounge - Main Stage
**Characters:** Homer, audience, showgirls, Marge, Bart

Caught up in the attention, Homer briefly dances with the showgirls. But then he remembers why he's there.

Homer stops the show and addresses the audience. He delivers an impassioned speech about treating women with respect:

- Women are not objects for entertainment
- They are mothers, daughters, sisters, and wives
- They deserve to be treated with dignity
- He's trying to teach his son to be a better man

The audience is moved by Homer's sincerity.

In the back of the room, Marge has arrived. She watches Homer's speech with tears in her eyes.

#### Scene 15: Reconciliation
**Location:** The Sapphire Lounge
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart

After the speech, Marge approaches Homer. She's touched by his genuine effort and his heartfelt words.

Marge forgives Homer. The family is reunited.

Bart has learned an important lesson about respecting women as people.
