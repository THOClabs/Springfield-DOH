## Synopsis

When Homer fails to get tickets to the big football game between Springfield and Shelbyville, Ned Flanders wins radio contest tickets and invites Homer along. After an unexpectedly wonderful time, Homer becomes excessively grateful and clingy, reversing their usual dynamic. As Homer invades the Flanders family's every activity, Ned experiences something he's never felt before: hatred for Homer Simpson. The episode culminates in a wild Terminator 2-style chase and a touching church scene where Homer defends Ned's reputation.

**Couch Gag:** Sliced family couch gag

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Big Game
**Location:** Simpson Home / KBBL Radio
**Characters:** Homer, family

The Pigskin Classic football game between Springfield and Shelbyville is the hottest ticket in town.

Homer desperately tries to get tickets but fails completely.

KBBL Radio announces a ticket giveaway contest.

#### Scene 2: Homer's Failed Attempts
**Location:** Various Springfield locations
**Characters:** Homer

Homer tries everything to get tickets:
- Calling radio stations
- Scalpers
- Begging

Nothing works. Homer is despondent.

#### Scene 3: Ned Wins Tickets
**Location:** Flanders Home
**Characters:** Ned Flanders, Homer

Ned Flanders wins radio contest tickets to the big game.

In a moment of Christian generosity, Ned invites Homer to join him.

Homer is shocked - and accepts eagerly.

#### Scene 4: The Football Game
**Location:** Football Stadium
**Characters:** Homer, Ned

Homer and Ned attend the game together. Against all expectations, they have a wonderful time.

They bond over the excitement. Springfield wins.

Ned catches a game ball and gifts it to Homer, signed by all the players.

Homer is deeply moved by this gesture.

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 5: Homer's Gratitude Begins
**Location:** Simpson Home / Flanders Home
**Characters:** Homer, Ned, both families

Homer becomes excessively grateful. He visits the Flanders constantly.

He proposes a toast: "If this were a more perfect world, we'd all be known as the Flimpsons."

The Flanders family is polite but growing uncomfortable.

#### Scene 6: Homer Invades
**Location:** Various Flanders locations
**Characters:** Homer, Flanders family

Homer's clinginess escalates:
- Showing up uninvited to family dinners
- Inserting himself into Flanders activities
- Constantly expressing gratitude

Ned tries to maintain his Christian patience, but it's wearing thin.

#### Scene 7: The Homeless Shelter
**Location:** Springfield Homeless Shelter
**Characters:** Homer, Ned, Springfield Shopper reporter

Ned invites Homer to volunteer at a homeless shelter.

Homer's impatient, aggressive food distribution is misinterpreted by a reporter as enthusiasm.

Homer is celebrated in the newspaper as a local hero.

Ned feels overshadowed in his own charitable work.

#### Scene 8: The Lake Trip
**Location:** Lake Springfield
**Characters:** Simpson family, Flanders family

Both families go on a camping trip to Lake Springfield.

Disaster ensues. Homer's antics cause considerable damage:
- Wrecks the Flanders' car
- Destroys their boat
- Creates conflict after conflict

The Flanders family is miserable.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 9: Ned's Nightmare
**Location:** Flanders bedroom (dream sequence)
**Characters:** Ned, various

Ned has a disturbing nightmare involving violent imagery.

He wakes up and confesses to Maude: he has grown to hate Homer Simpson.

This is a profound revelation - Ned has never truly hated anyone.

#### Scene 10: Ned Tries to Escape
**Location:** Flanders Home
**Characters:** Ned, Maude, Homer

Ned becomes desperate to avoid Homer.

Homer arrives expecting to play golf. Ned and his family jump in their car and race away.

#### Scene 11: The Terminator Chase
**Location:** Springfield streets
**Characters:** Homer, Ned, Wiggum

In a famous sequence parodying Terminator 2, Homer chases the Flanders' car.

Homer emerges through hedges like the T-1000 shape-shifter.

The chase is intense and ridiculous.

Chief Wiggum pulls Ned over for speeding.

#### Scene 12: "Goofballs" Arrest
**Location:** Springfield street
**Characters:** Ned, Wiggum, passing townspeople

Wiggum gives Ned a sobriety test. Ned, flustered from the chase, appears impaired.

A church bus passes. Wiggum plays it up: "High as a kite, everybody! Goofballs!"

The congregation witnesses their beloved Ned being arrested.

This introduces the iconic phrase "hepped up on goofballs."

#### Scene 13: Church Shunning
**Location:** First Church of Springfield
**Characters:** Flanders family, congregation, Homer

The next Sunday, the Flanders reluctantly attend church.

Ned confesses to Maude: "I can't do it, Maude. I can't face their accusing eyes!"

The congregation shuns them. Everyone believes Ned is a drug user.

#### Scene 14: Homer's Defense
**Location:** First Church of Springfield
**Characters:** Homer, Ned, congregation

Homer stands up and defends Ned publicly.

He admits his own behavior was overbearing and wrong. He explains that Ned was only fleeing from Homer's excessive friendship.

The congregation is ashamed of their judgment.

Ned is touched by Homer's support.

#### Scene 15: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Lisa

Everything returns to normal. When Ned approaches, Homer dismisses him with annoyance.

Lisa questions: "Don't you think this means our adventures will end?"

The status quo is restored - Homer back to being annoyed by Ned rather than obsessed with him.

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Ned has limits to his Christian patience
2. Homer is capable of genuine gratitude (to excess)
3. The show's willingness to mock sitcom conventions
4. "Goofballs" as a recurring phrase
5. That the status quo always resets
