## Synopsis

This groundbreaking anthology episode presents a day in the life of Springfield through approximately 22 interconnected vignettes, each focusing on different residents of the town. The episode opens with Bart and Milhouse sitting on an overpass, wondering if "anything interesting ever happens" to the people of Springfield. What follows is a kaleidoscopic journey through the untold stories of the town's citizens.

The format pays homage to the 1993 film "Thirty Two Short Films About Glenn Gould" while incorporating elements of Quentin Tarantino's "Pulp Fiction" in its structure and specific sequences. The stories range from the mundane to the absurd, from Lisa getting gum stuck in her hair to Principal Skinner attempting to pass off fast-food burgers as "steamed hams," to Chief Wiggum and Snake getting held hostage in a military antique shop.

The episode demonstrates that every Springfield resident, no matter how minor, has their own fascinating story to tell. It concludes with Bart and Milhouse realizing that yes, interesting things do happen to everyone in Springfield - they just don't have time to hear them all.

## Segments Overview

Despite the title, the episode contains approximately 19 distinct segments (17 if Lisa's three segments are counted as one storyline). Here is a breakdown of all major vignettes:

### Segment 1: Bart & Milhouse - Opening
**Characters:** Bart Simpson, Milhouse Van Houten

Bart and Milhouse sit on an overpass watching traffic below. They contemplate whether the various Springfield residents have interesting lives.

**Bart:** "Sometimes I wonder about all the people in this town. Do you think anything interesting ever happens to 'em?"

This framing device bookends the episode.

---

### Segment 2: Apu's Five-Minute Break
**Characters:** Apu Nahasapeemapetilon, Sanjay Nahasapeemapetilon

Apu reluctantly leaves his Kwik-E-Mart for the first time in years to attend a party at his cousin Sanjay's house. He dances, enjoys himself for exactly five minutes, then rushes back to work.

**Apu:** "I have been working for 96 hours straight!"

This segment showcases Apu's dedication to his job.

---

### Segment 3: Lisa's Gum Crisis (Part 1)
**Characters:** Lisa Simpson, Marge Simpson, Various Townspeople

At school, someone throws gum into Lisa's hair. Marge attempts various home remedies to remove it, including peanut butter and mayonnaise.

**Marge:** "Don't worry, sweetie. We'll get this out somehow."

This storyline recurs throughout the episode.

---

### Segment 4: Skinner & The Superintendent ("Steamed Hams")
**Characters:** Principal Seymour Skinner, Superintendent Gary Chalmers, Agnes Skinner

*The most famous segment of the episode.*

Principal Skinner invites Superintendent Chalmers over for lunch. When Skinner burns his roast, he secretly buys Krusty Burger hamburgers and attempts to pass them off as his own cooking.

**Skinner:** "I call them 'steamed hams.'"

**Chalmers:** "You call hamburgers 'steamed hams'?"

**Skinner:** "Yes! It's a regional dialect."

**Chalmers:** "Uh-huh. What region?"

**Skinner:** "Upstate New York."

**Chalmers:** "Really? Well, I'm from Utica and I've never heard anyone use the phrase 'steamed hams.'"

**Skinner:** "Oh, not in Utica, no. It's an Albany expression."

When the kitchen catches fire, Skinner claims it's the "aurora borealis."

**Chalmers:** "GOOD LORD, WHAT IS HAPPENING IN THERE!?"

**Skinner:** "Aurora borealis."

**Chalmers:** "AURORA BOREALIS!? AT THIS TIME OF YEAR, AT THIS TIME OF DAY, IN THIS PART OF THE COUNTRY, LOCALIZED ENTIRELY WITHIN YOUR KITCHEN!?"

**Skinner:** "Yes."

**Chalmers:** "...May I see it?"

**Skinner:** "No."

Agnes Skinner calls for help as the house burns while Chalmers departs, complimenting Skinner's "good ham."

---

### Segment 5: Mr. Burns & Smithers - Tandem Bicycle
**Characters:** Mr. Burns, Waylon Smithers

Burns and Smithers ride a tandem bicycle when a bee flies into Burns' eye. Burns gives increasingly incoherent directions while Smithers desperately tries to steer.

**Burns:** (with bee in eye) "Smithers! There's a bee in my eye!"

---

### Segment 6: Chief Wiggum & Lou - "Royale with Cheese"
**Characters:** Chief Wiggum, Officer Lou

In a direct parody of Pulp Fiction's famous "Royale with Cheese" scene, Wiggum and Lou sit at Krusty Burger discussing the differences between Springfield and other cities' fast food.

**Wiggum:** "You know what they call a Krusty Burger with Cheese?"

**Lou:** "A Krusty Burger with Cheese?"

**Wiggum:** "That's right."

---

### Segment 7: Snake's Escape / Wiggum Chase
**Characters:** Snake Jailbird, Chief Wiggum

Snake drives through Springfield listening to a rock version of The Simpsons theme. He spots Chief Wiggum walking with doughnuts and attempts to run him down. Wiggum chases Snake, not for attempted murder, but to exchange insurance information after the collision.

---

### Segment 8: Dr. Nick's Malpractice Hearing
**Characters:** Dr. Nick Riviera, Hospital Board, Patient

In a parody of the TV show ER, Dr. Nick faces a malpractice hearing. When a patient arrives with severe symptoms, Nick diagnoses "bonus eruptus" and treats it with improvised methods, miraculously earning his exoneration.

**Dr. Nick:** "Hi, everybody!"

**Board/Patients:** "Hi, Dr. Nick!"

---

### Segment 9: Cletus Gets Boots
**Characters:** Cletus Spuckler, Brandine Spuckler

Hillbilly Cletus discovers a pair of boots on a telephone wire and excitedly brings them home to his wife Brandine.

**Cletus:** "Hey Brandine! You might could wear these to your job interview!"

This segment parodies rural poverty stereotypes.

---

### Segment 10: Lisa's Gum Crisis (Part 2)
**Characters:** Lisa Simpson, Marge Simpson, Various Townspeople

Various townspeople offer absurd suggestions for removing the gum, including using Baltic squid and preserving it as "evidence."

---

### Segment 11: Bumblebee Man's Bad Day
**Characters:** Bumblebee Man (Pedro)

The Spanish-language TV star experiences a series of slapstick disasters even when off-camera, including encounters with woodpeckers, electrical failures, and baseballs.

**Bumblebee Man:** "Ay, ay, ay! No me gusta!"

This segment plays like a Mad Magazine comic strip.

---

### Segment 12: Moe's Bar Tab Revelation
**Characters:** Moe Szyslak, Barney Gumble, Robber

Moe calculates Barney's lifetime bar tab, revealing it totals $14 billion. A robber attempts to hold up the bar but becomes trapped behind bulletproof glass.

---

### Segment 13: Herman's Hostage Situation (Pulp Fiction Parody)
**Characters:** Snake Jailbird, Chief Wiggum, Herman

Snake and Wiggum's chase ends at Herman's Military Antiques, where the one-armed Herman holds them both at gunpoint. This sequence directly parodies the Marsellus Wallace/Butch storyline from Pulp Fiction.

**Herman:** "Neither of you is going anywhere."

The segment includes references to Pulp Fiction's basement scene.

---

### Segment 14: Reverend Lovejoy's Dog Walk
**Characters:** Reverend Lovejoy, Ned Flanders

Reverend Lovejoy deliberately walks his dog onto Ned Flanders' lawn to let it defecate. When Ned confronts him, Lovejoy quotes scripture sarcastically.

---

### Segment 15: Milhouse Needs a Bathroom
**Characters:** Milhouse Van Houten, Comic Book Guy

Milhouse desperately searches for a bathroom, eventually finding one at the Android's Dungeon comic book store. Comic Book Guy reluctantly allows him access.

---

### Segment 16: Nelson Mocks the Tall Man
**Characters:** Nelson Muntz, Tall Man in Small Car

Nelson laughs "Ha-ha!" at a very tall man driving a comically small car. The tall man parks, gets out, and forces Nelson to march through town in his underwear as punishment.

**Nelson:** "Ha-ha!"

**Tall Man:** "Do you find something comical about my appearance when I'm driving my automobile?"

This segment delivers poetic justice for Nelson's signature mockery.

---

### Segment 17: Professor Frink's Interrupted Story
**Characters:** Professor Frink, Laboratory Monkey

Frink attempts to tell his story but is immediately interrupted when a monkey steals his glasses.

**Frink:** "Well, my story begins inâ€” Oh no! My glasses!"

---

### Segment 18: Lisa's Gum Crisis (Resolution)
**Characters:** Lisa Simpson, Hair Stylist

A professional hair stylist finally removes the gum by simply cutting it out, giving Lisa a new hairstyle.

---

### Segment 19: Homer & Maggie at Railroad Tracks
**Characters:** Homer Simpson, Maggie Simpson

Homer's car stalls on railroad tracks with Maggie inside. After various failed attempts to move the car, a mysterious resolution occurs.

---

### Segment 20: Bart & Milhouse - Closing
**Characters:** Bart Simpson, Milhouse Van Houten

Bart and Milhouse conclude that yes, interesting things do happen to everyone in Springfield. They just don't have time to hear all the stories.

**Bart:** "Yeah, I guess interesting stuff does happen to people in this town."

## Cultural References

- **Thirty Two Short Films About Glenn Gould (1993):** The title and anthology format directly reference this Canadian biographical film
- **Pulp Fiction (1994):** Multiple segments parody Tarantino's film:
  - The "Royale with Cheese" dialogue between Wiggum and Lou
  - The Snake/Wiggum chase parallels the Marsellus/Butch storyline
  - Herman's hostage situation references the pawn shop basement scene
- **ER (TV Series):** Dr. Nick's malpractice segment parodies the medical drama
- **Mad Magazine:** Bumblebee Man's segment evokes the publication's visual comedy style
- **Classic Newspaper Comics:** Homer and Maggie's segment has an old comic strip quality
- **Sitcom Tropes:** "Steamed Hams" parodies the classic "boss comes to dinner" setup

## Episode Themes

1. **Everyone Has a Story:** Every minor character has a life worth exploring
2. **Interconnected Community:** The vignettes show how Springfield residents' lives intersect
3. **Comedy of Manners:** Social situations gone wrong (Skinner's dinner)
4. **Poetic Justice:** Nelson gets what he deserves for mocking others
5. **Absurdist Humor:** Situations escalate to ridiculous extremes
6. **Parody and Homage:** Loving tributes to various film and TV genres

## "Steamed Hams" - Internet Legacy

The "Skinner & The Superintendent" segment (known as "Steamed Hams") became one of the most famous scenes in television history:

- **2016 Meme Explosion:** The scene went viral, spawning thousands of parody and remix videos
- **YouTube Phenomenon:** Creators made countless variations including musical remixes, mashups with other media, and extended versions
- **Showrunner Shares Script:** Bill Oakley shared the original script on Twitter, revealing the scene was remarkably similar to the final version from the first draft
- **Cultural Impact:** "Steamed Hams" became shorthand for elaborate lies that somehow work

## Episode Rating Context

"22 Short Films About Springfield" is widely regarded as one of the greatest Simpsons episodes:
- **Critical Acclaim:** Consistently appears on "best episodes" lists

- **Cultural Impact:** "Steamed Hams" became a defining internet meme
- **Showrunner Approved:** Bill Oakley's personal favorite
- **Character Showcase:** Proved that every Springfield resident could carry a story
- **Quotability:** Contains numerous iconic lines still referenced today
- **Rewatchability:** The dense, segmented structure rewards multiple viewings

The episode represents peak Simpsons creativity, demonstrating the show's ability to experiment with format while maintaining its comedic excellence.
