## Synopsis

After Homer embarrasses Marge at a movie theater, they quarrel and Homer drives off to cool down. He ends up at a redneck bar called "The Beer-N-Brawl" where he discovers Lurleen Lumpkin, a talented country singer-songwriter working as a waitress. Homer becomes her manager, helping launch her music career. But Lurleen has fallen in love with Homer, creating tension with Marge. Homer must ultimately choose between the exciting world of country music management and his commitment to his marriage.

### ACT ONE

#### Scene 1: The Movie Theater
**Location:** Springfield Movie Theater
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, Maggie

The Simpson family attends a movie. Homer behaves obnoxiously, embarrassing Marge in front of other patrons.

Marge is furious. A major argument erupts between Homer and Marge.

#### Scene 2: Homer Drives Off
**Location:** Springfield Streets / Rural Highway
**Characters:** Homer

To cool down after the fight, Homer drives aimlessly into the countryside. He ends up far from Springfield in a rural, redneck area.

He spots a roadside bar called "The Beer-N-Brawl" and decides to stop.

#### Scene 3: The Beer-N-Brawl
**Location:** The Beer-N-Brawl Bar
**Characters:** Homer, Lurleen Lumpkin, bar patrons, M.C.

Homer enters the rough-and-tumble bar. A young woman named Lurleen Lumpkin is working as a waitress.

The M.C. announces that Lurleen will perform a song. Most patrons ignore her or are indifferent.

Lurleen performs "Your Wife Don't Understand You" - an original country song she wrote.

Homer is captivated. Despite the crowd's disinterest, he recognizes her incredible talent.

**Song:** "Your Wife Don't Understand You" (written by Beverly D'Angelo)

---

### ACT TWO

#### Scene 4: Homer Becomes Manager
**Location:** The Beer-N-Brawl / Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Lurleen, Marge

Homer returns to the bar and offers to become Lurleen's manager. She's thrilled - finally someone believes in her talent.

Homer tells Marge about his new venture. She's skeptical but allows it.

Homer starts calling himself "Colonel Homer" - a reference to Colonel Tom Parker, Elvis Presley's famous manager.

#### Scene 5: Building Lurleen's Career
**Location:** Various venues / Recording studio
**Characters:** Homer, Lurleen

Homer works tirelessly to promote Lurleen. He books her at various venues and helps her record demo tapes.

Lurleen performs another original song: "Bagged Me a Homer"

**Song:** "Bagged Me a Homer" (written by Beverly D'Angelo)

The song is clearly about her feelings for Homer, but he's oblivious to the romantic subtext.

#### Scene 6: Lurleen's Feelings
**Location:** Various
**Characters:** Lurleen, Homer

Lurleen has fallen deeply in love with Homer. She writes songs about him and makes increasingly obvious romantic gestures.

Homer, characteristically oblivious, doesn't realize she's attracted to him.

#### Scene 7: Marge's Suspicion
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Marge, Homer, Bart, Lisa

Marge grows increasingly upset. Homer is spending all his time with an attractive young woman who sings love songs.

She suspects Homer is having an affair, or at minimum, that Lurleen is trying to steal her husband.

The marriage is strained.

---

### ACT THREE

#### Scene 8: Lurleen's Advance
**Location:** Recording studio / Lurleen's trailer
**Characters:** Homer, Lurleen

Lurleen finally makes an overt romantic advance toward Homer. She confesses her love and tries to kiss him.

This is Homer's moment of truth.

#### Scene 9: Homer's Choice
**Location:** Lurleen's trailer
**Characters:** Homer, Lurleen

Despite being flattered and tempted, Homer realizes his commitment to Marge is more important.

He gently rejects Lurleen's advances. He explains that he loves his wife.

Homer decides he can no longer be Lurleen's manager if she has feelings for him.

#### Scene 10: Selling the Contract
**Location:** Music industry office
**Characters:** Homer, Record executives

Homer sells Lurleen's management contract to a legitimate music company for a large sum.

He walks away from the music industry, but ensures Lurleen will have a real career.

#### Scene 11: Resolution
**Location:** Simpson Home
**Characters:** Homer, Marge, family

Homer returns home to Marge. He explains that he remained faithful and has given up the management business.

Marge is relieved and grateful. Their marriage is repaired.

The money from selling Lurleen's contract provides a nice windfall for the family.

Lurleen goes on to have a successful country music career (she would return in later episodes).

## Production Notes

- **Writer:** Matt Groening (his only solo writing credit in the series)
- **Director:** Mark Kirkland
- **Guest star:** Beverly D'Angelo (Patsy Cline in "Coal Miner's Daughter")
- **Casting:** D'Angelo met Groening at Frank Zappa's house; auditioned based on her Patsy Cline performance
- **Songs:** Beverly D'Angelo wrote both songs in one hour before the table read
- **Character inspiration:** Lurleen based partly on Loretta Lynn (alliterative L.L. name, country style)
- **Name origin:** "Lumpkin" from Tony Lumpkin in the 1773 play "She Stoops to Conquer"
- **Character design:** Also inspired by Kathi Kamen Goldmark, founder of The Rock Bottom Remainders
- **Nielsen rating:** 14.8 - highest-rated Fox show that week
- **Critical reception:** Entertainment Weekly named D'Angelo's performance one of 16 best guest appearances

## Episode Significance

This episode establishes:
1. Lurleen Lumpkin as a recurring character (returns in later seasons)
2. Homer's fundamental commitment to Marge despite temptation
3. Matt Groening's only solo writing credit
4. Beverly D'Angelo as a memorable guest performer
5. The show's ability to handle romantic tension seriously
